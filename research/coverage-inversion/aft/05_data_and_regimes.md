# §5 — Data and Regime Labeler

**Universe.** We evaluate $K = 10$ symbols across three asset classes: eight US equities (SPY, QQQ, AAPL, GOOGL, NVDA, TSLA, HOOD, MSTR — the underliers of the SPYx…MSTRx Backed tokens on Solana mainnet), tokenised gold (GLD), and tokenised long rates (TLT). GLD and TLT share the NYSE-hours closed-market structure but exercise different factors and vol indices, so calibration across all three classes evidences broader RWA reach than equity-only evaluation. Backtest targets are the *underlying-venue* (NYSE) opens from Yahoo Finance; we do not use on-chain xStock prices as input or target.

**Panels.** Each weekend row is a single $(s,t)$ window: for every consecutive trading-day pair separated by a calendar gap $\ge 3$ days we emit `fri_close`, `mon_open`, `gap_days`, `fri_vol_20d`, `factor_ret` (weekend return of the per-symbol conditioning factor), `vol_idx_fri_close`, `earnings_next_week`, and `is_long_weekend`. The weekend panel spans 2014-01-17 → 2026-04-24: 5,996 raw rows across 639 weekends (MSTR from 2014; HOOD post-IPO from 2021). The **overnight panel** (§5.2.1) applies the identical pipeline with the gap selector admitting consecutive-trading-day pairs (close→next-open) instead of Friday→Monday — 22,624 rows across 2,412 weeknights — with two cadence-specific treatments: earnings are assigned to the gap they straddle by `bmo`/`amc` session timing (scryer `yahoo/earnings/v2`, complete from 2015), and ex-dividend-morning opens are reconstructed to cum-dividend level from `yahoo/corp_actions/v1`.

**Factor and vol-index switchboards.** Equities use E-mini S&P futures (`ES=F`) and VIX; MSTR pivots to BTC-USD from 2020-08-01 (its first Bitcoin treasury purchase — a structural exposure break); GLD uses gold futures + GVZ; TLT uses 10Y T-note futures + MOVE (per-class vol indices lift the log-log σ̂ regression slope into the equity range). Full per-symbol mappings in the appendix.

**Regime labeler $\rho$.** A strict pre-publish priority cascade. *Weekend:* `high_vol` (VIX at Friday close in the top quartile of its trailing 252-day window) → `long_weekend` (`gap_days` $\ge 4$) → `normal`; sample shares $65.6 / 23.9 / 10.5\%$. *Overnight:* `long_weekend` is dropped, `high_vol` retained (252-trading-day lookback), and `earnings_night` added as top priority — a gap is `earnings_night` iff a scheduled release falls inside it by session timing (§4.3.1); shares $73.4 / 25.6 / 1.0\%$.

**Train/test split.** 2023-01-01: calibration = 4,186 weekend rows (2014–2022), held-out OOS = 1,730 rows (2023-01 → 2026-04). The split is conservative — it places the 2023 banking turbulence and the 2024–2025 macro transition in the holdout. Trained per-regime quantiles are fixed throughout OOS evaluation; provenance and one-command reproduction are in Appendix A.

# §9 — Limitations

We state the load-bearing limitations here; the full disclosure set — shock-tertile coverage ceiling, stationarity sub-period evidence, the σ̂-selection multi-test exposure, the τ-stratified CUSUM drift bank, MEV/execution-aware coverage, off-anchor interpolation, and the external-evidence dependencies in the §1/§2 framing — is in Appendix E. These are disclosures, not retractions; each has a concrete §10 follow-up.

**Per-anchor, not full-distribution, calibrated.** The served band passes Kupiec at every $\tau$ and Christoffersen at every served $\tau$, but pooled Berkowitz and Engle–Manganelli DQ reject when PITs/violations are concatenated across symbols. The residual is structural: a within-weekend cross-sectional cluster topology separating equities (positively correlated with the weekend equity factor, within-cluster $\hat\rho = 0.477$) from safe-haven assets (GLD, TLT, $\hat\rho \approx 0$), orthogonal to the per-symbol σ̂ standardisation and invisible to per-symbol time-ordered DQ. §6.4's joint-tail empirical distribution turns it into a measurable reserve-guidance threshold ($k^\ast = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$, 0/24 sub-period stability rejections) — the operational handle no incumbent oracle publishes — and §10.1 names the cluster-conditional + path-fitted full-distribution upgrade.

**Per-anchor SLA $\neq$ portfolio solvency.** The $k^\ast$ threshold and the per-anchor guarantee are *per-name, per-read* properties; they do not compose into a portfolio-level solvency guarantee. A protocol holding $m$ correlated xStock collateral types simultaneously inherits the within-weekend common-mode directly: the relevant object is the joint breach count $k_w$ across the held names, which over-disperses $2.34\times$ vs independence and whose worst observed realisation is the 2024-08-05 BoJ-unwind weekend ($k_w = 10$ at $\tau = 0.85$). Portfolio solvency under simultaneous breach therefore requires reserve architecture sized against the empirical $k_w$ distribution — for which §6.4 supplies the CDF and $k^\ast$ the circuit-breaker trigger — not the per-name band alone. The full reserve-sizing treatment is the subject of companion liquidation-policy work.

**OOS-tuned $c(0.95)$.** Of the four OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ scalars, three are essentially identity; only $c(0.95) = 1.079$ (a 7.9% widening) carries meaningful out-of-sample information. The nested temporal holdout fits it on 2023 alone and evaluates on a 2024–2026 slice neither the quantile nor the bump has seen — realised $0.9504$, Kupiec $p = 0.947$ — so the headline scalar is empirically held out at the time level. The residual provenance is a lower-$\tau$ over-coverage in held-out mode (a contract-favourable sharpness deficit, not under-coverage); the deployed full-OOS schedule is the production mitigation.

**Scope.** *Temporal:* the headline battery is the weekend gap, but §6.6 demonstrates the same architecture — only its gap selector changed — calibrates on the overnight panel, so we claim calibration-transparency across *closed-market hours* generally; ex-dividend mornings are dividend-adjusted from the corporate-actions feed, removing the only systematic price-level artefact. *Region:* all ten tickers are US-listed and the factor switchboard privileges US-session data; we make no claim about tokenised-JP/EU equities. *Asset class:* the methodology requires a continuous off-hours information set (futures + a vol index, or a correlated sub-daily signal) — satisfied by US equities, gold, treasuries, and major FX, but not by real estate or NAV-priced instruments. A per-class fit/no-fit table is in `docs/methodology_scope.md`.

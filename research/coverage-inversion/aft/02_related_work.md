# §2 — Related Work

Our contribution sits at the intersection of decentralised-oracle design, cross-venue price discovery, and conformal/calibration interval forecasting, with institutional model-risk-management guidance supplying the disclosure vocabulary. Appendix F gives the full survey; we state the gap here.

**Oracles publish integrity, not calibration.** Deployed RWA-oracle surfaces are organised around an *integrity* primitive — delivering a faithful value on-chain without a trusted intermediary — not a *calibration* one. Chainlink Data Streams (v8/v10/v11) expose a `marketStatus` flag but no realised-coverage claim [chainlink-streams; chainlink-v8]; Kamino Scope ($4B TVL) routes every xStock collateral through the v8 path with `Open`/`AllUpdates` feeds clamped to ±500 bps of the Friday close [scope-oracle-type; scope-mainnet-cfg]. Pyth emits `(price, conf)` and asserts ~95% coverage at the *publisher* level, not as a property of the aggregate [pyth-agg; pyth-conf]; CF Benchmarks' FCA-supervised xStocks Indices [cfb-xstocks-indices] and SEDA's session-aware composites [seda-benchmark] carry no on-wire uncertainty field; optimistic oracles [uma; tellor] guarantee *eventual* correctness, not a distributional statement conditional on time-of-week. The academic literature is likewise integrity-focused — CFMM-as-oracle incentive compatibility [angeris-cfmm], manipulation SoKs [sok-oracles], adversarial ordering [flashboys-2]. **No deployed surface publishes a calibration receipt verifiable against public data**, so a coverage-vs-sharpness numerical comparison is not well-defined against incumbents and is not reported; the contribution is necessarily categorical. An oracle can be integrity-perfect and calibration-meaningless: faithfully reporting Friday's 16:00 close on Sunday is not asserted to be a 95%-coverage estimate of Monday's open. We invert the interface so $\tau$ is a request parameter and the served quantile is the receipt.

**Price discovery establishes the forecaster, not the novelty.** The point estimate scales Friday's close by a weekend futures return, applying Hasbrouck's information-share result that S&P/Nasdaq price discovery concentrates in E-mini futures [hasbrouck-1995; hasbrouck-2003]. The Friday 16:00 → Monday 09:30 ET window is the longest contiguous US non-trading interval [french-1980]; after-hours discovery is non-trivial and its effective spreads run 3–4× regular hours [barclay-hendershott-2003; barclay-hendershott-2004] — the window in which RWA collateral is most exposed. Cong et al. [cong-tokenized-2025] supply the empirical premise: tokenised prices track underliers near-one-for-one *during* US hours (adjusted $R^2 = 0.982$) but wedge substantially off-hours (TSLAx — a live Kamino collateral asset — deviates >1% from the last underlying close in 71% of weekday off-hours and 15% of weekend hours), and off-hour token returns predict the close-to-open underlying return ($\lambda = 0.903$, adjusted $R^2 = 0.839$) but regime-dependently — informative on weekends, reversal-driven on weekday overnights. That signal is conditional-*mean*; the conditional-*quantile* band that lets a consumer use it without inheriting prints that "likely would have triggered halts in regulated markets" is the primitive we deploy.

**Calibration supplies the evaluation.** We report Kupiec unconditional-coverage [kupiec-1995] and Christoffersen independence [christoffersen-1998] tests — the Basel traffic-light content [bcbs-backtest-1996] — with Berkowitz [berkowitz-2001] and Engle–Manganelli dynamic-quantile checks, under the Gneiting et al. objective of maximising sharpness subject to calibration [gneiting-calibration-2007]. The deployed architecture is a locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal predictor [vovk-mondrian-2003; vovk-book-2005] — per-regime quantiles on per-symbol $\hat\sigma_s$-standardised residuals — with Romano–Patterson–Candès CQR [romano-cqr-2019] the named full-distribution upgrade (§10). The validation form matches what a bank integrating an on-chain oracle into a collateral-valuation pipeline owes under SR 11-7 / SR 26-2 [sr11-7; sr26-2]. To our knowledge no prior work combines decentralised-oracle design with Christoffersen-style conditional-coverage evaluation — the gap this paper fills.

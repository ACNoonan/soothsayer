# §10 — Extensions

The §9 limitations are disclosures, not retractions; three extensions close the named residuals (lower-priority follow-ups — on-chain xStock TWAP forecaster, conditional EVT for the $\tau \to 1$ tail, denser anchor grid — are tracked in the roadmap).

**Full-distribution conformal + cluster-conditional partial-out.** The pooled Berkowitz / Engle–Manganelli DQ rejection is the deepest open methodological item. The upgrade replaces the four-anchor schedule with a single conformal correction over the residual CDF (Romano–Patterson–Candès CQR [romano-cqr-2019] the closest match); composed with a cluster-internal partial-out within the equity cluster — which reduces within-cluster $\hat\rho$ from $0.477$ to $0.077$, preserves per-symbol Kupiec, and tightens $\tau = 0.95$ width by $11.6\%$ — and a path-fitted score, the recommendation is τ-conditional, empirically calibrated at $\tau \ge 0.95$ on the equity-cluster + CME-projected subset.

**Path-fitted conformity score.** Continuous-consumption AMM use cases require the path-fitted variant rather than the endpoint score; the methodology is library-grade and unit-tested, and the binding empirical question — does path-fitting close the §6.5 perp/on-chain residual on consumer-experienced data — accumulates as the V5 forward-cursor tape crosses the path-coverage threshold.

**Off-hours and live-deployment validation.** An earnings-night passthrough bake-off against the Cong-implied tokenised baseline is the empirical bridge from this underlying-side result to the tokenised-side claim. A rolling forward-tape rebuild and a devnet-then-mainnet live window against a real consumer protocol close the external-validity gap. The downstream decision-theoretic question — given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol *do*? — requires an explicit borrower-book LTV distribution, a protocol cost model, and a realized-action semantics, and is the subject of companion liquidation-policy work.

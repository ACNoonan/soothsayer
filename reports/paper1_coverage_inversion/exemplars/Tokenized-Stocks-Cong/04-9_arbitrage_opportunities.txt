Figure 12. Daily maximum slippage (xStocks vs. underlying stocks). The figure presents the daily maximum absolute percentage
deviations of xStock tokenized stock prices from Yahoo closing prices of Tesla, NVIDIA, and Alphabet.

The largest deviations typically correspond to weekend closures where news or order imbalance occur. For
instance, Tesla’s 14% slippage, driven by the pre-market news on September 15, is attributed to the September 14
weekend interval. By contrast, overnight slippage from Monday to Thursday are generally minor. Overall, slippage is modest in most case, with extreme divergences tied to material news events, and any larger gaps are quickly
arbitraged away by the next trading session.
Notably, Tesla’s token exhibited both the highest volatility and the largest single-day slippage. This aligns with
Tesla’s reputation for significant news and trader interest. Nvidia and Alphabet tokens had low slippage, mostly
under 2%, reflecting more efficient pricing or fewer off-hour surprises. Our findings suggest that for blue-chip
stocks, tokenized markets generally stay in sync with underlying prices, with slippage on the order of a few percent
or less. Larger deviations only arise in exceptional circumstances, such as major off-hour news, and are corrected
when markets reopen.

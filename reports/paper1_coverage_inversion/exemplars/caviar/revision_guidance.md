# Revision guidance for paper 1, benchmarked against Engle & Manganelli, *CAViaR* (NBER WP 7341, 1999 / JBES 2004)

## Executive summary — five load-bearing changes

1. **Cut the introduction by ~40%; promote one sentence-level headline number to the abstract's first paragraph and to §1.1's opening.** CAViaR's abstract is six sentences and its §1 is two pages; paper 1's abstract is one ~700-word page and §1 sprawls over five sub-headings before stating a result. Hold the protocol/primitive framing, but stop trying to deliver every disclosure in the abstract. (See §1 below.)
2. **Demote the 16-scalar / 19-scalar / DOF accounting from a load-bearing claim to a one-table appendix entry.** It is referenced in nearly every section of paper 1 and is paid for in reader attention. CAViaR has more parameters than 16, never apologises, and lets the reader judge by Kupiec/DQ. The right calibration of confidence here is "we report the count for transparency" not "we re-derive it across §1, §3, §4, §5, §7, §9, §11."
3. **Promote DQ from §6.3.6 sub-section to a primary backtest, alongside Kupiec/Christoffersen, with a stand-alone DQ table per (symbol × τ) and per regime — and report the DQ regressor specification you actually used.** This is the single highest-leverage change you can make for q-fin.RM credibility. CAViaR built DQ specifically because Kupiec/Christoffersen are underpowered; right now paper 1 uses CAViaR's tools while keeping the weakest one (Kupiec) as the headline.
4. **Build a single CAViaR-Table-3-style master table per method** (this paper, GARCH-N, GARCH-t, unweighted Mondrian, constant buffer) **with a fixed row schema across every τ × symbol cell, then point every other claim back at it.** Right now the same numbers are restated in five places with mildly different framings (abstract, §1.3, §6.3.1, §6.3.3.1, §6.4.2, §6.8, §11.1). One canonical table per method, then pointer references.
5. **Cut §10 by ~50% and merge §9.6 / §9.10 / §9.11 into §9 proper.** CAViaR's §10 is one paragraph. Paper 1's §10 has four subsections, ~12 named follow-ups, and several "library-grade, unit-tested but not yet empirically pursued" extensions. For a first paper, the future-work section is signalling research overhang, not contribution.

## Body — ten structured comparisons

### 1. §1 / Introduction: density and discipline

CAViaR's §1 (pp. 1–3 of the working paper) does five things in two pages: motivates VaR with one sentence on disasters and Basel; sets up Eqn (1) — the formal VaR definition; states the three modelling questions any VaR system must answer; states what CAViaR contributes against each; and previews the section structure. It does not gesture at headline numbers because the paper's contribution is methodological. Paper 1's §1 (`01_introduction.md` lines 1–62) does too much: it has four sub-headings (1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4), reproduces the full deployment formula on line 27, lists nine headline empirical claims, and burns a paragraph on the JELLYJELLY post-mortem before getting to the contribution. **The intro is doing the abstract's job and the §3 problem-statement's job simultaneously.** Concrete cuts: (i) drop the formula from §1.2 (it belongs only in §3 / §4); (ii) the bullet list in §1.3 lines 39–51 should be at most three bullets, with the held-out-LOSO number as one and "vs GARCH-t 40/40 vs 31/40" as another, and the rest deferred to §6; (iii) merge §1.4's contribution list (C1–C5) into a single paragraph or into the §3 problem statement. The right target is two pages: motivation, gap, contribution, headline number, structure preview. Aim for CAViaR's density (1 page = ~350 words, paper 1 currently runs ~1100 words/page in §1).

### 2. Methodology: structural clarity

CAViaR's §3 specifies the model in equations (2)–(3) on pp. 7–8 — a single general autoregressive form plus six concrete instantiations (Adaptive, Symmetric Absolute Value, Asymmetric Slope, Indirect GARCH, etc.) listed in roughly half a page. Theory follows in §§5–6 (regression-quantile asymptotics, hypothesis testing) but the *model* is communicated in <2 pages. Paper 1's §4 (`04_methodology.md`) is structurally clear but has earned-vs-unearned length. The four ingredients (point estimator, σ̂, Mondrian conformal quantile, c(τ) bump) and the central serving formula on §4.6 lines 60–66 are CAViaR-tight. What's not earned is the §4.5 walk-forward δ(τ) explanation (a six-paragraph justification of why a vector is identically zero — it should be one sentence: "The δ(τ) schedule is identically zero on this panel; see §7.2.3 for ablation evidence"); §4.3.1 (within-bin exchangeability — belongs in an appendix, not in the model spec); the §4.2 paragraph on the σ̂ five-variant ladder (belongs entirely in §7.4). **Compress §4 from its current ~5 pages to ~2.5 pages by hoisting the ablation-and-disclosure material out into §7 and §9, leaving §4 as: estimator, σ̂, conformal step, c(τ), serve formula, receipt.** The conformal-prediction setup *does* require more space than CAViaR's pure quantile-regression specification (you need to state the standardisation of the score, the per-regime split, and the c(τ) correction, none of which CAViaR needed) — but the extra length should be the math, not the methodology-history asides.

### 3. Data section: discipline about windows and cleaning

CAViaR's §9 opens (p. 32) with one paragraph: "3392 daily prices from Datastream for General Motors, IBM and S&P 500, 7 April 1986 – 7 April 1999, log-returns, first 2892 observations for estimation, last 500 out-of-sample, Figure 1 plots the returns." Three sentences, three assets, ~3,400 days, sharp split, plot. Paper 1's §5 (`05_data_and_regimes.md`) is more disciplined than the rest of the paper: §5.1–§5.3 specify universe, panel construction, and pre-publish features in roughly two pages; §5.4's switchboard table is genuinely good; §5.6's split table (lines 53–58) is the right CAViaR-style summary. Two improvements: (i) add a CAViaR-Figure-1 analogue — a single figure with 10 sub-panels showing each symbol's weekend log-return series 2014–2026, with the 2023-01-01 split as a vertical line, plus marginal histograms. This is the strongest single piece of "we know our data" signal for a q-fin reviewer and is missing. (ii) The §5.5 paragraph on "Cross-asset regime-index sensitivity" (lines 49 onward) is a result, not data description — move to §7.

### 4. Real-data results: density and table design

CAViaR's Tables 3–8 are the canonical reference — one table per CAViaR variant, one column per asset (GM, IBM, S&P 500), four sub-blocks per anchor (0.1%, 1%, 5%, 25%), and within each sub-block: parameter estimates, standard errors, p-values, RQ-in/out-of-sample, hits-in/out-of-sample, and four DQ p-values (one per regressor specification — §9 p. 33). Roughly twelve numbers per (variant, asset, anchor) cell. Tables 3–8 are dense, single-page, and let you compare any two of CAViaR's six methods at a glance. Paper 1's §6 (`06_results.md`) by contrast has 13 distinct tables across §6.2–§6.7, each formatted differently, several of them carrying narrative inline ("$c(\tau)=1.079$ working as designed" etc). The headline LOSO number ($0.9497 \pm 0.0128$) appears in §1, §6.3.3, §6.8, §11; the 40/40 vs 31/40 GARCH-t comparison appears in §1, §6.4.2, §11. **The single biggest §6 win is collapsing this into a CAViaR-Table-3-style master table with the schema {symbol × τ × method → (violation rate, Kupiec p, Christoffersen p, DQ p with explicit regressor list, mean half-width)}.** That is one table, comprehensive, comparable across methods, ~80 rows. Then §6.3.1 / §6.3.2 / §6.4.1 / §6.4.2 / §6.4.3 become a paragraph each that point at sub-blocks of the master table. CAViaR's claim economy: §9 of CAViaR is roughly four pages of prose plus six tables. Paper 1's §6 is roughly fifteen pages of prose plus the tables.

### 5. Backtest framing: is DQ a primary backtest or a footnote?

CAViaR §4 (pp. 9–11) builds DQ specifically because of an explicit critique of Kupiec/Christoffersen (p. 10): "All these tests can detect the presence of serial correlation in the sequence of indicator functions. However, this is not enough to assess the performance of a VaR estimate." The proof example on p. 10 is sharp: a VaR sequence $K \cdot z_t$ for $z_t$ Bernoulli has correct Kupiec, no serial correlation in hits, and yet the conditional probabilities are 0 or 1. **Kupiec/Christoffersen do not have power against conditional bias.** That is the single most important sentence in CAViaR's testing chapter and it is the reason paper 1's §6.3.6 DQ rejection — currently buried — should be promoted. Right now paper 1 uses Kupiec as the lead test in 40 of 40 cells; reports DQ only as a pooled p-value rejection for one (presumably $\tau = 0.95$) anchor in §6.3.6 lines 147–149; never specifies which DQ regressor list it used; never reports DQ per-symbol or per-regime. CAViaR reports DQ across four regressor specifications {(1) constant + 5 lagged hits; (2) VaR; (3) constant + 1 lagged hit + VaR; (4) constant + 5 lagged hits + VaR} for every (variant × asset × anchor) — see Tables 3–8 column "DQ in sample (p-values)" / "DQ out of sample (p-values)". **Concrete fix:** specify the DQ regressor list (you should use CAViaR's same four for direct comparability), report DQ p-values per (symbol × τ × method) in the master table from item 4, and reframe §6.3.6's pooled-DQ rejection as one row of that grid. The current treatment understates the most important diagnostic in this paper's evidence base.

The defensibility argument *for* keeping DQ subordinate is "we are doing conformal prediction, not GARCH-style quantile regression, and Kupiec/Christoffersen are the institutional banking-validation standard." That is not wrong — SR 11-7 and Basel speak Kupiec — but the q-fin.RM reviewer reads CAViaR. The right move is to elevate DQ as the *finer* test and keep Kupiec/Christoffersen as the *regulatory-floor* test, with the framing made explicit in one sentence.

### 6. Baseline comparison: even-handedness

CAViaR §9 (pp. 33–35 + Tables 3–9) compares six CAViaR variants against each other plus a plain GARCH(1,1) baseline (Table 9 on p. 51). It is even-handed about where its own methods fail: explicitly states that the Adaptive model has correct unconditional coverage but autocorrelated hits (p. 33–34); the Proportional Symmetric Adaptive is "consistently rejected at the 1% and 5% confidence levels" (p. 33); the 5% S&P 500 OOS DQ is rejected for *every* CAViaR specification (p. 34); the 0.1% estimates "must be taken with extreme caution" (p. 35). Paper 1's §6.4.3 (`06_results.md` lines 200–219) compares against GARCH-Gaussian, GARCH-t, and the unweighted Mondrian — three baselines, well-chosen, with the GARCH-t NVDA fallback honestly disclosed. The framing is mostly fair: §6.4.3 line 215 admits GARCH-t "ties" the deployed architecture on width-at-matched-coverage at $\tau = 0.95$. What's *less* even-handed: the §6.3.6 / §9.4 Berkowitz/DQ pooled rejection — paper 1 has its own evidence that the deployed bands are *not* full-distribution calibrated, but in §1.3 / §11.1 / §6.8 the framing returns to "10/10 Kupiec passes" and the rejection is consistently described as "cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode" rather than as a backtest failure that downstream protocols should know about. CAViaR would say plainly: "DQ rejects at $\tau = 0.95$; this is a real failure mode, the residual is identified, the fix is in §10." The current text comes close to that in §9.4 but the headline framing in the abstract / §1 / §11 still papers over the DQ rejection. **One clean fix: add a sentence to the abstract "DQ rejects at $\tau = 0.95$ pooled; the residual is cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode (§9.4)" — same phrasing CAViaR would use.** Right now the abstract is silent on the DQ rejection.

### 7. Honesty about negative results

This is the closest dimension on which paper 1 is *already* strong, with two caveats. Paper 1 explicitly discloses: §6.3.6 DQ rejection (though buried); §6.5 / §6.6 endpoint-vs-path gap of $+16.1$ pp at $\tau = 0.95$; the §9.10 off-anchor interpolation disclosure; the §6.4.3 NVDA GARCH-t fallback; the §6.7 forward-tape harness $N = 1$ caveat; the §7.4 multi-test correction admission that under BH the σ̂ ranking does not hold. CAViaR's negative-result discipline (§9 on Adaptive autocorrelation, on 0.1% noise, on 5% S&P 500 OOS rejection) is matched here. Two caveats: **(i) the LOSO-vs-pooled distinction.** Paper 1 currently presents both numbers, but the "$0.9497 \pm 0.0128$" leads in the abstract while the "$0.950$ pooled is by construction" caveat lives in a parenthetical. CAViaR would put the pooled-by-construction admission before the LOSO claim. (ii) **The 16-scalar / "3 of 16 carry meaningful OOS information" framing.** This is technically honest but rhetorically defensive — repeated in §1, §3, §4, §5, §7, §9, §11. The reader notices the over-disclosure and mentally docks the paper. CAViaR has many more scalars than 16 (six per CAViaR variant × three assets × four τ-anchors = ~72 estimated scalars in Tables 3–8), reports them all in tables, and writes in §9 simply "the results are reported in Tables 3 to 8." The right calibration is to put the 16-scalar disclosure *once* (in §A.2 of the appendix where it already lives) with one cross-reference from §4.

### 8. Conclusion / contribution framing: tightness

CAViaR's §10 (p. 36) is exactly two paragraphs and ~250 words. It says: we propose a new approach; we use regression quantiles + a genetic algorithm; we introduce a new test (DQ); applications support the methodology; that's it. Paper 1's §11 (`11_conclusion.md`) is ~1500 words across four sub-sections (11.1 properties revisited, 11.2 ablation summary, 11.3 honest framing). §11.1 (lines 7–11) restates each of P1, P2, P3 in roughly the same depth as §3.4 — pure repetition. §11.3 (lines 26–35) restates the disclosures from §9 with bullet pointers — also pure repetition. **Cut §11 to two paragraphs: (a) what the paper did (interface + architecture + 12-year evidence), (b) what the empirical bottom line is (one number — pick LOSO or 40/40 — and one residual — DQ rejection with §10 pointer).** A reader who reaches §11 has already read §1, §6, §9, §10; the conclusion exists to give them the takeaway, not to summarise. Length cap: 250 words to match CAViaR.

### 9. What CAViaR is missing that paper 1 correctly adds

Paper 1's structural additions over CAViaR that *are* earned and should not be cut:

- **The product-primitive framing (§3 problem statement, §4.7 receipt, §8 serving layer).** CAViaR is methodology only; paper 1 ships an oracle interface with a per-read cryptographic receipt, a Python reference, a Rust port with byte-exact parity, and an Anchor program. The (a)-shaped contribution (the primitive) is genuinely a new artefact and CAViaR does not have an analogue. For the AFT audience this is the contribution. *Do not weaken this; if anything strengthen the §3 → §4 → §8 thread by writing one paragraph at the top of §3 framing the artefact as a software contract that downstream protocols can enforce.*
- **The reproducibility appendix §A.** CAViaR has nothing comparable — its §7 mentions a Matlab implementation by Rick Baker as a footnote (p. 28). Paper 1's §A.1 algorithm boxes, §A.2 hyperparameter table, §A.3 code/data availability, and §A.5 runnable command sequence are at the bar of modern ML/conformal-prediction papers, well above CAViaR's bar. Keep.
- **Joint-tail empirical distribution (§6.3.4) + worst-weekend vignette (§6.3.5).** CAViaR is single-asset; paper 1 has 10 symbols and characterises the joint breach distribution under empirical, Binom, Gaussian copula, and t-copula references. The reserve-guidance threshold $k^* = 3$ is a genuinely novel consumer-facing handle that no incumbent oracle publishes. Keep — though tighten §6.3.4 prose; it currently runs three pages where two would suffice.
- **The four-DGP simulation study (§6.7).** CAViaR has a Monte Carlo (§8 of CAViaR, p. 29) that is one DGP testing whether the genetic algorithm recovers GARCH parameters. Paper 1's four-DGP study isolates the per-symbol bimodality mechanism on synthetic data with known ground truth and confirms σ̂ standardisation closes it. This is a strictly stronger structural-claim discipline than CAViaR's. Keep.
- **The held-out forward-tape harness (§6.6).** Whatever its current $N = 1$ status, the *commitment* to ongoing forward-tape evaluation against a content-addressed frozen artefact is an institutional-validation discipline CAViaR does not have. Keep — but be honest at submission that $N = 1$ is operational signal, not load-bearing held-out evidence (you already are; just don't dilute this).
- **Per-symbol Berkowitz LR range as a dispersion summary.** §6.4.1's "$[3.2, 16.7]$ — a 5.2× spread" is a sharper structural claim than anything CAViaR makes about cross-asset uniformity. Keep.

### 10. What paper 1 has that's bloat or premature

- **The §4.5 walk-forward δ(τ) is identically zero discussion.** Six paragraphs (`04_methodology.md` lines 51–57) to justify a vector of zeros. Compress to one sentence with a §7.2.3 cross-reference.
- **§4.3.1 within-bin exchangeability permutation test.** Belongs in §A or in §9 as a one-paragraph disclosure, not in the methodology spec.
- **§5.5 "cross-asset regime-index sensitivity."** This is an ablation result (lines 49 onward of `05_data_and_regimes.md`), not a data-description. Move to §7.
- **§7.4 σ̂ selection procedure section.** This is currently ~9 sub-sections (7.4.1 through 7.4.10) and runs longer than §6.3 + §6.4 combined. The σ̂ choice does need to be defended (CAViaR-style: a Monte Carlo + Tables 3–8 settles which CAViaR variant to use), but you can do the same job in 1.5 pages: (i) the five-variant ladder with one summary table, (ii) the three-gate criterion stated once, (iii) the BH-corrected admission, (iv) the held-out forward-tape pointer. The current §7.4 is the strongest place where the paper's "ML-engineer dialect" shows up vs CAViaR's "econometrician dialect" — CAViaR would handle σ̂ selection by minimising the regression-quantile RQ criterion out-of-sample (CAViaR §9 p. 35: "Among the surviving models, we choose the one with the lowest out-of-sample RQ criterion") and would not write an entire sub-section on multiple-testing correction. The honest tension here: paper 1's three-gate approach with BH is *more* statistically rigorous than CAViaR's, but it also reads as defensive. The compromise is to keep BH but compress the prose.
- **§9.11 "asymmetric failure modes — a coherent architectural property."** This is rhetorical packaging — the failure modes are real and disclosed, but the framing as "a design property, not an accident" reads like over-explanation. Cut the sub-section title; fold the content into §9.1 or §9.3 where the failure modes are first discussed.
- **§10 future work.** Currently four sub-sections, ~12 named follow-ups, including library-grade-but-not-pursued path-fitted scoring, a denser anchor grid, full-distribution conformal, conditional EVT, cluster-conditional partial-out, MEV-conditional variant, etc. CAViaR's §10 (the conclusion, since CAViaR has no separate future-work section) suggests one extension: "introducing extreme value theory in the CAViaR framework" (p. 31). For a first paper at arXiv + AFT, your §10 should name three follow-ups maximum, each with a one-sentence motivation. The current §10 is signalling research overhang.
- **The "16 / 19 deployment scalar" accounting.** Mentioned in `00_abstract.md`, `01_introduction.md` (§1.2 lines 27–29 and §1.4), `problem_statement.md` §3.4 P3, `04_methodology.md` §4 line 5, `05_data_and_regimes.md` §5.6, `07_ablation.md` §7.4.10, `09_limitations.md` §9.3, `11_conclusion.md` §11.1. That's eight repetitions. Drop seven of them; keep §A.2 plus one cross-reference.
- **The synthetic-marker `.01`-suffix Chainlink v11 finding** (mentioned in §1.1 and §2.1). It's a real and useful empirical finding but it does not belong in a calibration paper at the level of detail given. One sentence in §2.1 is enough; the deeper analysis lives at `reports/v11_cadence_verification.md`.

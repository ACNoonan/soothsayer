# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets settle on-chain around the clock, but the venues that price their underlying assets do not: a US equity trades 6.5 hours per weekday, and for the remaining two-thirds of wall-clock time — every night and every weekend — lending protocols must value tokenised collateral against a closed reference market. No oracle deployed at scale publishes a verifiable statement of how uncertain its served price is during that window. We invert the conventional oracle interface: instead of a point and a band whose coverage is implicit, target coverage $\tau$ is the consumer-chosen input, the served band is the output, and every read carries a calibration receipt that any third party can re-derive from public data. The deployed architecture is a locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal predictor — per-regime conformal quantiles on per-symbol volatility-standardised residuals. On 5,996 weekend and 22,624 overnight prediction windows across ten US-listed tickers (2014–2026), the headline claim is held out on two orthogonal axes: leave-one-symbol-out coverage at $\tau = 0.95$ of $0.9497 \pm 0.0128$ with all ten held-out symbols passing Kupiec, and a nested temporal holdout realising $0.9504$. Across a 40-cell (symbol × $\tau$) grid the architecture passes **40/40 Kupiec, 38/40 Christoffersen, and 39/40 DQ**, leading the strongest practitioner baseline, GARCH-$t$, on every metric — and it calibrates on overnight gaps with only its gap selector changed, making calibration-transparency a property of closed-market hours generally, not of weekends. The residual is disclosed and measured: pooled DQ rejects — bands are *per-anchor*, not full-distribution, calibrated — and the failure mode is within-weekend cross-sectional co-breach, quantified into an operational reserve-guidance threshold ($k^\ast = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$). Finally, because an earnings release is a scheduled, publicly dated event, the overnight band widens deterministically *ahead* of it and stays calibrated against a tail roughly $8\times$ the normal-night scale — **calendar-conditioned coverage**, which no incumbent oracle provides. Reference implementation in Python, Rust, and Anchor.

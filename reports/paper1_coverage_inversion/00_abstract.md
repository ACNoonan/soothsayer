# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface so that target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a consumer request and the served claimed quantile $q_\mathrm{served} = \tau + \delta(\tau)$ is a per-read receipt, and we build a Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime band whose lookup function maps an asset $s$, a regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, and a target $\tau$ to a calibrated symmetric band around a factor-adjusted point. The resulting primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact against public data ($P_1$), conditional empirical coverage of the served band at $\tau$ ($P_2$), and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$). We evaluate on 5{,}996 weekend prediction-window rows across 10 US-listed tickers (639 distinct weekends, with HOOD partial post-2021) from 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24, with a temporally disjoint 2023+ held-out slice of 1{,}730 rows over 173 weekends; at the consumer-facing target $\tau = 0.95$ the deployed serving configuration — twelve per-regime conformal quantiles trained on the pre-2023 calibration set, four OOS-fit multiplicative bumps $c(\tau)$, and four walk-forward-fit $\delta(\tau)$ shifts — realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.912$ at a mean half-width of $354.5$ bps (a 20% reduction over the v1 hybrid forecaster Oracle on the same slice, with block-bootstrap CI excluding zero on width and straddling zero on coverage). At $\tau = 0.99$ the deployed band hits the consumer's nominal target (realised $0.990$, Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.942$), closing the v1 finite-sample tail ceiling at $0.972$ at the cost of a 22% wider band. A six-split expanding-window walk-forward ratifies the deployed schedule at every anchor (per-anchor Kupiec $p$ = 0.43, 0.37, 0.36, 0.32 at $\tau \in \{0.68, 0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$), so the operating result is deployment-calibrated and walk-forward-stable rather than purely held-out end-to-end. Berkowitz and DQ both reject, which narrows the empirical claim to per-anchor calibration rather than full-distribution calibration and identifies a concrete v3 research agenda: full-distribution conformal, richer regime classifiers, and rolling live rebuilds. An eight-rung forecaster ladder, five-cell serving-layer matrix, constant-buffer width-at-coverage stress test, and Mondrian conformal-by-regime ablation isolate the load-bearing components with block-bootstrap confidence intervals. The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack and a Rust + Anchor production stack with byte-for-byte parity (90/90 cases) on a Solana on-chain publisher whose `PriceUpdate` Borsh wire format was preserved through the v1 → M5 migration. We do not claim point-accuracy state-of-the-art, integrity against adversarial feeds, or an optimal liquidation-policy default; those concerns sit in adjacent companion papers.

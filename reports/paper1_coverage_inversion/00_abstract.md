# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface: target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a consumer request, the served claimed quantile $q_\mathrm{served} = \tau + \delta(\tau)$ is a per-read receipt, and a Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime band maps an asset $s$, regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, and target $\tau$ to a calibrated band around a factor-adjusted point. The primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact ($P_1$), conditional empirical coverage at $\tau$ ($P_2$), and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$).

We evaluate on 5{,}996 weekend prediction-window rows across ten US-listed tickers (639 weekends, 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24) with a temporally disjoint 2023+ held-out slice of 1{,}730 rows. The deployed configuration carries twenty scalars: 12 per-regime conformal quantiles trained on pre-2023 data, four OOS-fit multiplicative bumps $c(\tau)$, and four walk-forward-fit shifts $\delta(\tau)$ — the same parameter budget as the prior hybrid Oracle's `BUFFER_BY_TARGET`. At $\tau = 0.95$ the served band realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.912$ at a mean half-width of $354.5$ bps — a 20% reduction over the prior hybrid Oracle on the same slice (block-bootstrap CI excludes zero on width, straddles zero on coverage). At $\tau = 0.99$ the band hits the consumer's nominal target (realised $0.990$, Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.942$), closing the prior baseline's finite-sample tail ceiling at $0.972$ at the cost of a 22% wider band. A six-split expanding-window walk-forward and a four-anchor split-date sensitivity (2021–2024) both ratify the deployed schedule. Berkowitz and DQ both reject, narrowing the empirical claim to per-anchor calibration; the rejection mechanism localises to cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode residual rather than a coarser-classifier story (a vol-tertile sub-split of the `normal` regime fails to reduce the joint LR), pointing the next-generation agenda at full-distribution conformal calibration and rolling rebuilds. Per-symbol calibration is bimodal — large-cap equities and the RWA anchors are over-covered (variance compression) while heavy-tail tickers (TSLA, HOOD, MSTR) are under-covered (variance expansion) at $\tau \le 0.95$; HOOD is the load-bearing per-symbol disclosure. The primary calibration claim is *endpoint*-conditional (the realised Monday open lies inside the band at rate $\tau$); a complementary intra-weekend *path-coverage* read on a 24/7 stock-perp reference ($n = 118$ symbol-weekends, 2025-12 to 2026-04) shows a $14.4$pp raw gap at $\tau = 0.95$ between endpoint and path coverage, decomposing after three robustness checks (perp-spot basis at the Friday-close anchor, volume floor, sustained-crossing definition) to a residual ${\sim}7$–$10$pp of genuine intra-weekend shortfall — approximately closed by stepping up to $\tau = 0.99$ on this slice.

The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack and a Rust + Anchor production stack with byte-for-byte parity (90/90) on a Solana on-chain publisher whose `PriceUpdate` Borsh wire format was preserved through the migration from the prior hybrid Oracle to M5. We do not claim point-accuracy state-of-the-art, integrity against adversarial feeds, or an optimal liquidation-policy default; those concerns sit in companion papers.

# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface so that target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a consumer request and the served claimed quantile $q_\mathrm{served}$ is a per-read receipt, and we build the empirical calibration surface $S^f(s, r, q)$ that maps a base forecaster $f$, an asset $s$, and a regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$ to its historical realised coverage at each grid quantile. The resulting primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the surface against public data ($P_1$), conditional empirical coverage of the served band at $\tau$ ($P_2$), and per-regime serving efficiency over a candidate forecaster set $\mathcal{F} = \{\texttt{F0\_stale}, \texttt{F1\_emp\_regime}\}$ ($P_3$). We evaluate on 5{,}986 weekend prediction-window rows across 10 US-listed tickers (638 distinct weekends, with HOOD partial post-2021) from 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-17, with a temporally disjoint 2023+ held-out slice of 1{,}720 rows over 172 weekends; at the consumer-facing target $\tau = 0.95$ the deployed serving configuration — calibration surface frozen on the pre-2023 calibration set and a per-anchor empirical buffer schedule deployment-tuned on the held-out slice — realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 1.000$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.485$; a six-split expanding-window walk-forward ratifies the deployed buffer at $\tau = 0.95$ as the cross-split mean ($0.020$ deployed vs $0.019$ mean, $\sigma = 0.017$), so the operating result is deployment-calibrated rather than purely held-out end-to-end. Berkowitz and DQ still reject, which narrows the empirical claim to per-anchor calibration rather than full-distribution calibration and, more importantly, identifies a concrete V2 research agenda: rolling live rebuilds, richer on-chain signals, and finer regime structure. An eight-rung forecaster ladder plus five-cell serving-layer matrix isolate the load-bearing components with block-bootstrap confidence intervals. The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack and a Rust + Anchor production stack with byte-for-byte parity (75/75 cases) on a Solana on-chain publisher. We do not claim point-accuracy state-of-the-art, integrity against adversarial feeds, or an optimal liquidation-policy default; those concerns sit in adjacent companion papers.

# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface: target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a published, parametric product input rather than an implicit by-product of the model, the served band carries a per-read calibration receipt whose realised rate is independently verifiable from public data, and a locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal architecture — referred to as **M6** throughout — maps an asset $s$, regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, per-symbol pre-Friday scale $\hat\sigma_s(t)$, and target $\tau$ to a calibrated band around a factor-adjusted point. The primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact ($P_1$); conditional empirical coverage at $\tau$ per symbol and per regime ($P_2$); and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$).

We evaluate M6 on 5,996 weekend prediction-window rows across ten US-listed tickers (639 weekends, 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24) with a 1,730-row 2023+ held-out slice. The deployment carries **16 scalars**: 12 per-regime conformal quantiles trained on standardised residuals on pre-2023 data, four near-identity OOS-fit multiplicative bumps $c(\tau) \in \{1.000,\,1.000,\,1.079,\,1.003\}$, and zero walk-forward shifts. At $\tau = 0.95$ the served band realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.603$ at $370.6$ bps half-width; **all 10 symbols pass per-symbol Kupiec**; calendar sub-period coverage holds across {2023, 2024, 2025, 2026-YTD} with Kupiec $p \ge 0.054$ in every cell. Against parametric baselines on the same slice, M6 carries **39/40 Kupiec passes** across the (symbol $\times \tau$) grid versus 31/40 for GARCH-$t$ and 23/40 for GARCH-Gaussian; at matched 95% realised coverage M6 ties GARCH-$t$ on width.

The architectural prediction that per-symbol scale standardisation closes per-symbol calibration error is confirmed *in advance* under a four-DGP simulation study with known ground truth (per-symbol Kupiec pass-rate $97.6$–$99.6\%$ across stationary, regime-switching, drift, and structural-break specifications) before real-data confirmation. Joint-tail behaviour for portfolio consumers is empirically characterised, not assumed: across 173 OOS weekends at $\tau = 0.95$, empirical $P(k_w \ge 3)$ on a 10-symbol panel is $5.20\%$ (95% bootstrap CI $[2.31,\,8.67]$) versus the binomial-independence reference of $1.15\%$; the worst-observed weekend (Friday 2024-08-02 $\to$ Monday 2024-08-05, the BoJ yen-carry-unwind) sat at $k_w = 10$ across all 10 symbols at $\tau = 0.85$. The standard *per-anchor calibrated, not full-distribution calibrated* disclosure is consequently turned into a measurable reserve-guidance threshold ($k^* = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$, with 0/24 sub-period $\times$ threshold-convention stability rejections under M6).

The $\hat\sigma_s$ rule (per-symbol EWMA, weekend half-life 8) was selected from a five-variant ladder under a pre-registered three-gate criterion with Benjamini–Hochberg multi-testing correction across an 80-cell split-date Christoffersen grid; the deployment decision rests on the bootstrap CI on width (no multi-test exposure) plus a held-out forward-tape re-validation harness running weekly against a content-addressed frozen artefact. The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack with a Rust + Anchor production stack; M6's on-chain wire-format slot is reserved (`forecaster_code = 3`, `FORECASTER_LWC`) ahead of the productionisation milestone.

# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface: target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a consumer request, the served claimed quantile $q_\mathrm{served} = \tau + \delta(\tau)$ is a per-read receipt, and a Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime band maps an asset $s$, regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, and target $\tau$ to a calibrated band around a factor-adjusted point. The primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact ($P_1$), conditional empirical coverage at $\tau$ ($P_2$), and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$).

We evaluate on 5{,}996 weekend prediction-window rows across ten US-listed tickers (639 weekends, 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24) with a temporally disjoint 2023+ held-out slice of 1{,}730 rows. The deployed configuration carries twenty scalars: 12 per-regime conformal quantiles trained on pre-2023 data, four OOS-fit multiplicative bumps $c(\tau)$, and four walk-forward-fit shifts $\delta(\tau)$ — the same parameter budget as the v1 hybrid Oracle's `BUFFER_BY_TARGET`. At $\tau = 0.95$ the served band realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.912$ at a mean half-width of $354.5$ bps — a 20% reduction over v1 on the same slice (block-bootstrap CI excludes zero on width, straddles zero on coverage). At $\tau = 0.99$ the band hits the consumer's nominal target (realised $0.990$, Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.942$), closing the v1 finite-sample tail ceiling at $0.972$ at the cost of a 22% wider band. A six-split expanding-window walk-forward ratifies the deployed schedule at every anchor. Berkowitz and DQ both reject, narrowing the empirical claim to per-anchor calibration and identifying a v3 agenda: full-distribution conformal, richer regime classifiers, and rolling rebuilds.

The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack and a Rust + Anchor production stack with byte-for-byte parity (90/90) on a Solana on-chain publisher whose `PriceUpdate` Borsh wire format was preserved through the v1 → M5 migration. We do not claim point-accuracy state-of-the-art, integrity against adversarial feeds, or an optimal liquidation-policy default; those concerns sit in companion papers.

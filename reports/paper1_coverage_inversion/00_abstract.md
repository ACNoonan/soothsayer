# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface: target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a published, parametric product input rather than an implicit by-product of the model, the served band carries a per-read calibration receipt whose realised rate is independently verifiable from public data, and a locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal architecture maps an asset $s$, regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, per-symbol pre-Friday scale $\hat\sigma_s(t)$, and target $\tau$ to a calibrated band around a factor-adjusted point. The primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact ($P_1$); conditional empirical coverage at $\tau$ per symbol and per regime ($P_2$); and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$).

We evaluate the architecture on 5,996 weekend prediction-window rows across ten US-listed tickers (639 weekends, 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24) with a 1,730-row 2023+ held-out slice. **The headline empirical claim is held-out via 10-fold leave-one-symbol-out CV — fit $c(\tau)$ on nine symbols, evaluate on the tenth — at $\tau = 0.95$: realised coverage $0.9497 \pm 0.0128$ across the held-out symbol distribution, all 10 LOSO bands pass Kupiec.** Pooled realised coverage on the full 2023+ slice is $0.950$ at $370.6$ bps half-width, consistent with the deployment-tuned target — but the agreement at $0.950$ is by construction (only one of 16 deployment scalars, $c(0.95) = 1.079$, carries meaningful OOS information; the other three $c(\tau) \in \{1.000,\,1.000,\,1.003\}$ are essentially identity at the four served anchors $\{0.68 \approx 1\text{-}\sigma,\,0.85,\,0.95,\,0.99\}$), so the pooled number is not held-out evidence. The deployment carries 16 scalars: 12 per-regime conformal quantiles trained on standardised residuals on pre-2023 data, four near-identity OOS-fit multiplicative bumps, and zero walk-forward shifts. A handful of implicit hyperparameters are pinned a priori (EWMA half-life 8, $\geq 8$-past-observation σ̂ warm-up, three Mondrian regimes, top-quartile VIX threshold, 2023-01-01 split date); the 16-scalar count is the trainable / tuned budget, not a complete degrees-of-freedom statement. Three orthogonal stability checks support the LOSO read: per-symbol Kupiec passes on all 10 symbols at $\tau = 0.95$, four-anchor split-date sensitivity refits $c(\tau)$ per anchor and passes Christoffersen at every (split × $\tau$) cell, and calendar sub-period coverage holds across {2023, 2024, 2025, 2026-YTD}. Against parametric baselines on the same slice the conformal architecture carries **40/40 Kupiec passes** across the (symbol $\times \tau$) grid versus 31/40 for GARCH-$t$ and 23/40 for GARCH-Gaussian; at matched 95% realised coverage it ties GARCH-$t$ on width.

The architectural claim that per-symbol scale standardisation closes per-symbol calibration error is corroborated under a four-DGP simulation study with known ground truth (per-symbol Kupiec pass-rate $98.6$–$99.9\%$ across stationary, regime-switching, drift, and structural-break specifications, against the unweighted-Mondrian comparator's $29$–$31\%$). Joint-tail behaviour for portfolio consumers is empirically characterised, not assumed: across 173 OOS weekends at $\tau = 0.95$, empirical $P(k_w \ge 3)$ on a 10-symbol panel is $4.62\%$ (95% bootstrap CI $[1.73,\,7.51]$) versus the binomial-independence reference of $1.15\%$; the worst-observed weekend (Friday 2024-08-02 $\to$ Monday 2024-08-05, the BoJ yen-carry-unwind) sat at $k_w = 10$ across all 10 symbols at $\tau = 0.85$. The standard *per-anchor calibrated, not full-distribution calibrated* disclosure is consequently turned into a measurable reserve-guidance threshold ($k^* = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$, with 0/24 sub-period $\times$ threshold-convention stability rejections). The endpoint contract is what the served band claims: on a 24/7 stock-perp reference ($n = 118$ symbol-weekends) the architecture carries a $+16.1$ pp endpoint-vs-path gap at $\tau = 0.95$ — what an honestly-calibrated endpoint band exhibits when intra-weekend variance exceeds endpoint variance — with the §10.1 path-fitted conformity score as the methodology fix for continuous-consumption consumers.

The $\hat\sigma_s$ rule (per-symbol EWMA, weekend half-life 8) was selected from a five-variant ladder under a pre-registered three-gate criterion with Benjamini–Hochberg multi-testing correction across an 80-cell split-date Christoffersen grid; under BH correction at FDR=0.05 the per-cell evidence does not statistically distinguish the variants, so the deployment decision rests on the bootstrap CI on pooled width (no multi-test exposure, statistically significantly narrower at $\tau \in \{0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$ at preserved calibration). A weekly forward-tape harness against a content-addressed frozen artefact is operational and accumulating ($N = 1$ at submission, preliminary by the harness's own threshold of $N \ge 4$); it will become load-bearing held-out evidence as $N$ matures. The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack with a Rust + Anchor production stack (§8).

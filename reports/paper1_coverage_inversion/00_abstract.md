# Abstract

Tokenised real-world assets on public blockchains trade continuously, but their underlying price-discovery venues do not, leaving roughly two-thirds of wall-clock time during which on-chain protocols must value collateral against a closed reference market. We invert the conventional point-plus-confidence oracle interface: target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ is a published, parametric product input rather than an implicit by-product of the model, the served band carries a per-read calibration receipt whose realised rate is independently verifiable from public data, and a locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal architecture maps an asset $s$, regime label $r = \rho(\mathcal{F}_t)$, per-symbol pre-Friday scale $\hat\sigma_s(t)$, and target $\tau$ to a calibrated band around a factor-adjusted point. The primitive carries a three-property contract: auditability of the deployment artefact ($P_1$); conditional empirical coverage at $\tau$ per symbol and per regime ($P_2$); and per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets ($P_3$).

We evaluate on 5,996 weekend prediction-window rows across ten US-listed tickers (639 weekends, 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24) with a 1,730-row 2023+ held-out slice. The deployed configuration carries 16 scalars: 12 per-regime conformal quantiles trained on pre-2023 data on standardised residuals, four near-identity OOS-fit multiplicative bumps $c(\tau) \in \{1.000,\,1.000,\,1.079,\,1.003\}$, and zero walk-forward shifts (the structural-conservatism shim collapses under per-symbol scale standardisation). At $\tau = 0.95$ the served band realises $0.950$ coverage with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.603$ at $370.6$ bps half-width, with **all 10 symbols passing per-symbol Kupiec** (vs 2/10 under the predecessor v1 architecture) and the per-symbol Berkowitz LR range collapsing from $0.9$–$224$ to $3.2$–$16.7$. The architectural fix — adding a strictly pre-Friday per-symbol EWMA scale (half-life 8 weekends) to the conformity score — is *predicted from architecture* under a four-DGP simulation study with known ground truth (per-symbol Kupiec pass-rate $\sim 31\% \to \sim 99\%$ across stationary, regime-switching, drift, and structural-break specifications) before real-data confirmation. The σ̂ rule was selected from a five-variant ladder under a pre-registered three-gate criterion with Benjamini-Hochberg multi-testing correction across an 80-cell split-date Christoffersen grid; the deployment decision rests on the bootstrap CI on width (no multi-test exposure) plus a held-out forward-tape re-validation harness running weekly against a content-addressed frozen artefact.

Berkowitz still rejects pooled — bands are *per-anchor calibrated*, not full-distribution calibrated — and the residual rejection localises to cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode (orthogonal to σ̂; deferred to companion-paper roadmap). The reference implementation is open-sourced as a Python research stack with a Rust + Anchor production stack on the v1 architecture; the v2 Rust port is gated to a productionisation milestone with the on-chain wire format slot reserved (`forecaster_code = 3`, FORECASTER_LWC). We do not claim point-accuracy state-of-the-art, integrity against adversarial feeds, an optimal liquidation-policy default, or σ̂-rule optimality among locally-weighted variants.

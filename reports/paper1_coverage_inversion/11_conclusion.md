# §11 — Conclusion

This paper specified a calibration-transparent oracle primitive for tokenised real-world assets, evaluated it on 12 years of US-listed weekend data, and shipped a reference implementation. The contribution is the *interface* (consumer-selects-coverage with a per-read receipt) and the *deployable architecture* (locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal under EWMA HL=8 σ̂). The §7 ablation isolates three load-bearing components: (a) regime stratification, validated against the constant-buffer baseline; (b) per-symbol σ̂ standardisation, validated against an unweighted Mondrian comparator; (c) the near-identity OOS $c(\tau)$ bump, of which only $c(0.95) = 1.079$ carries meaningful out-of-sample information. The §6.8 simulation study corroborates the architectural mechanism on synthetic data with known ground truth (the per-symbol bimodality reproduces under controlled DGPs and per-symbol σ̂ standardisation closes it on every DGP tested); the §6.7 forward-tape harness against a content-addressed frozen artefact is operational and accumulating weekly.

## 11.1 The three properties, revisited

- **(P1) Auditability.** The deployment artefact (per-Friday parquet + 16-scalar JSON sidecar) is reconstructible from public data — Soothsayer consumes Scryer parquet for Yahoo daily prices, the per-symbol factor returns (ES / NQ / GC / ZN / BTC), the regime classifier inputs, and computes σ̂ as a deterministic function of past Fridays. The per-read `PricePoint` exposes the eight-field receipt of §4.7, and the on-chain `PriceUpdate` PDA mirrors that receipt under the byte-for-byte parity contract of §8.5. A third party with the sidecar and the per-Friday artefact can reconstruct any served band from the receipt alone.

- **(P2) Conditional empirical coverage — held-out via leave-one-symbol-out CV.** The leading calibration claim is **10-fold LOSO CV at $\tau = 0.95$: realised $0.9497 \pm 0.0128$ across the held-out symbol distribution, all 10 bands pass Kupiec.** Each held-out symbol's $c(\tau)$ is fit on the other nine symbols' OOS slice and never sees the held-out symbol's data, so the realised coverage is genuinely out-of-fit. On the 2023+ deployment-tuned slice (1,730 rows × 173 weekends) the served band at $\tau = 0.95$ realises $0.950$ at $370.6$ bps half-width; this is consistent with the deployment-tuned target but follows by construction (so the pooled Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ is not independent held-out evidence). Three orthogonal stability checks complement LOSO on the deployed-fit slice: per-symbol Kupiec (all 10 symbols pass at $\tau = 0.95$, per-symbol Berkowitz LR $\in [3.2, 16.7]$); four-anchor split-date sensitivity (Christoffersen passes every (split × $\tau$) cell across {2021, 2022, 2023, 2024}); and four-year calendar sub-period stability (Kupiec $p \ge 0.054$ in every cell across {2023, 2024, 2025, 2026-YTD}). At $\tau = 0.99$ the band realises $0.990$. Joint-tail behaviour for portfolio consumers is empirically characterised: $P(k_w \ge 3) = 4.62\%$ at $\tau = 0.95$ vs the binomial reference of $1.15\%$, with reserve-guidance threshold $k^\ast = 3$ stable across the 24-cell sub-period grid (0/24 stability rejections). Against parametric baselines on the same slice the deployed architecture carries 40/40 per-symbol Kupiec passes versus GARCH-$t$'s 31/40 — Kupiec at every (symbol × $\tau$) cell. The empirical content of P2 remains *per-anchor calibration*, not full-distribution calibration (Berkowitz still rejects pooled — the residual is cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode, orthogonal to σ̂; §9.4); the §6.3.4 joint-tail measurement turns this disclosure into a measurable, reportable shape. The §6.7 forward-tape harness is operational ($N = 1$ at submission, preliminary by its own threshold of $N \ge 4$); it will become load-bearing held-out evidence as $N$ matures.

- **(P3) Per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets.** The deployment carries 16 trainable / tuned scalars: 12 trained per-regime quantiles + 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ + 0 walk-forward (the $\delta(\tau)$ schedule is identically zero under per-symbol σ̂ standardisation), with implicit hyperparameters (EWMA HL=8, σ̂ warm-up minimum, regime cardinality, top-quartile VIX threshold, four served anchors, 2023-01-01 split) pinned a priori outside this count. Of the 4 $c(\tau)$ scalars, three are essentially identity ($c \in \{1.000,\,1.000,\,1.003\}$ at $\tau \in \{0.68,\,0.85,\,0.99\}$); only $c(0.95) = 1.079$ carries meaningful OOS information. **3 of 16 deployment scalars carry meaningful OOS-fit information.** The σ̂ multiplier delivers per-symbol width allocation: heavy-tail equities receive bands matched to their relative-residual scale; the defensive class (GLD, TLT) receives narrower bands without losing coverage (§7.5).

## 11.2 What the ablation says is doing the work

The §7 ablation reduces to three load-bearing components plus the underlying point estimator:

1. **Factor-switchboard point estimator.** Tightens pooled bands $\sim 39\%$ vs a stale-hold reference; the input whose residual distribution the conformal quantile is fit on.

2. **Regime stratification.** §7.1 established that a deployable architecture without regime structure (the constant-buffer baseline) cannot deliver coverage on the 2023+ slice. The regime model concentrates width in `high_vol` weekends where it earns coverage and releases width in `normal` weekends without losing it.

3. **Per-symbol pre-Friday σ̂ standardisation.** Adding a per-symbol $\hat\sigma_s(t)$ multiplier to the conformity score, with no other architectural change relative to an unweighted Mondrian comparator, takes per-symbol Kupiec at $\tau = 0.95$ from 2/10 → 10/10, collapses the Berkowitz LR range from $0.9$–$224$ to $3.2$–$16.7$, and tightens LOSO realised-coverage std by $5.9\times$. The §6.8 simulation study corroborates the mechanism — the per-symbol bimodality reproduces under four DGPs with known ground truth and σ̂ standardisation closes it on every DGP tested.

4. **Near-identity OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ bump.** Meaningful only at $\tau = 0.95$ (a 7.9% widening); at the other three anchors essentially identity. The walk-forward $\delta(\tau)$ schedule is identically zero — per-symbol σ̂ standardisation tightens cross-split realised-coverage variance enough that no structural-conservatism shift is required.

## 11.3 Honest framing

- **Per-anchor calibration, not full-distribution calibration.** Berkowitz and DQ both reject pooled. §6.3.6 / §9.4 localise the residual to *cross-sectional within-weekend* common-mode ($\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$), orthogonal to σ̂ standardisation. §6.3.4's joint-tail empirical distribution turns the residual into a measurable reserve-guidance threshold ($k^\ast = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$, stable across the 24-cell sub-period grid); the §10.2 cross-sectional partial-out track is the candidate methodology fix, gated on a Friday-observable predictor not yet available.

- **σ̂ selection multi-test exposure (mitigated).** The §7.4 σ̂ selection ran 80 split-date Christoffersen tests across 5 variants × 16 cells; under BH correction at FDR=0.05, no variant has any rejected cell. The deployment decision rests on Gate 3 (bootstrap CI on width — no multi-test issue, statistically significantly narrower at $\tau \in \{0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$ at preserved calibration). The §6.7 / §7.4.6 forward-tape variant comparison harness is operational; at $N = 1$ it does not yet contribute held-out re-validation. We do not claim the σ̂ rule is optimal among all locally-weighted variants.

- **OOS-fit $c(0.95) = 1.079$.** One scalar of the 16 is meaningfully OOS-fit. §9.3 reports the three independent provenance checks (4-split powered walk-forward, 4-anchor split-date sensitivity, 10-fold leave-one-symbol-out); all pass. The forward-tape harness is operational and accumulating; it does not yet add held-out re-validation at $N = 1$.

- **Shock-tertile floor.** Realised $\tau = 0.95$ coverage on the heaviest-move tertile is $87.8\%$ — the residual is cross-sectional common-mode, not per-symbol scale, and does not yield to σ̂ choice. The §6.3.4 reserve-guidance threshold and the §6.3.5 worst-weekend vignette (2024-08-05 BoJ unwind) make this disclosure operationally addressable through a circuit-breaker handle.

- **Forward-tape harness operational; held-out evidence accumulating.** $N = 1$ at submission, preliminary by the harness's own threshold ($N \ge 4$ to clear the preliminary banner; $N \ge 13$ for moderate forward-Christoffersen power). The harness will become load-bearing as $N$ matures. A live integrator window remains an open external-validity gap.

The textbook econometric default — per-symbol GARCH(1,1) on log Friday-to-Monday returns — was extended to GARCH-$t$ (the practitioner standard for fat-tailed returns) on the same OOS slice. GARCH-$t$ closes the $\tau = 0.99$ Kupiec gap that GARCH-Gaussian leaves on the table but still under-covers Kupiec at every $\tau < 0.99$; at matched 95% realised coverage GARCH-$t$ widens to $\approx 378$ bps — statistically tied with the deployed architecture's $370.6$. Across the 40-cell (symbol × $\tau$) grid the architecture carries 40/40 per-symbol Kupiec passes versus GARCH-$t$'s 31/40. The conformal route fixes both Kupiec and Christoffersen because it is calibrated to the data, not to a fitted parametric distribution.

Each residual identified above carries a proposed methodological response in §10: full-distribution conformal calibration for the Berkowitz / DQ rejection, a Friday-observable cross-sectional partial-out for the within-weekend common-mode, McNeil-Frey conditional EVT for the $\tau \to 1$ tail, a path-fitted conformity score for continuous-consumption consumers, and a denser anchor grid for the off-anchor interpolation disclosure. They name the boundary of what a per-anchor-calibrated locally-weighted-Mondrian interval oracle can claim, with concrete extensions for what the next architecture would do differently. §6.7 is the held-out re-validation mechanism whose evidence accumulates as forward weekends arrive.

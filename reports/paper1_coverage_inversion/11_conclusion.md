# §11 — Conclusion

This paper specified a calibration-transparent oracle primitive for tokenised real-world assets, evaluated it on 12 years of US-listed weekend data, and shipped a reference implementation. The contribution is the *interface* (consumer-selects-coverage with a per-read receipt) and the *deployable architecture* (M6 — locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal under EWMA HL=8 σ̂). The architecture was arrived at through the §7.1 constant-buffer stress test that ruled out the deployable simpler baseline; the §7.2 ladder that isolated the per-regime conformal lookup as the load-bearing replacement for the legacy Soothsayer-v0 hybrid forecaster machinery; and the §7.2.4 isolation of σ̂ standardisation as the per-symbol-bimodality fix. The §6.8 simulation study predicted the per-symbol fix from architecture under four DGPs *before* real-data confirmation; the §6.7 forward-tape harness carries the held-out evidence forward weekly against a content-addressed frozen artefact.

## 11.1 The three properties, revisited

- **(P1) Auditability.** The deployment artefact (per-Friday parquet + 16-scalar JSON sidecar) is reconstructible from public data — Soothsayer consumes Scryer parquet for Yahoo daily prices, the per-symbol factor returns (ES / NQ / GC / ZN / BTC), the regime classifier inputs, and computes σ̂ as a deterministic function of past Fridays. The per-read `PricePoint` exposes the eight-field receipt of §4.7, and the on-chain `PriceUpdate` PDA mirrors that receipt under the byte-for-byte parity contract of §8.5 (M5 path live; M6 Rust port reserved). A third party with the sidecar and the per-Friday artefact can reconstruct any served band from the receipt alone.

- **(P2) Conditional empirical coverage.** On the 2023+ slice (1,730 rows × 173 weekends), the served M6 band at $\tau = 0.95$ realises $0.950$ with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.603$ at $370.6$ bps half-width. **All 10 symbols pass per-symbol Kupiec**; per-symbol Berkowitz LR sits in $[3.2, 16.7]$. At $\tau = 0.99$ the band realises $0.990$ ($p_{uc} = 0.942$). Kupiec passes at all four anchors; Christoffersen passes at every anchor on the deployed split *and* at every (split × $\tau$) cell across the four split-date anchors {2021, 2022, 2023, 2024}. Calendar sub-period stability holds across {2023, 2024, 2025, 2026-YTD} (Kupiec $p \ge 0.054$ in every cell). Joint-tail behaviour for portfolio consumers is empirically characterised: $P(k_w \ge 3) = 5.20\%$ at $\tau = 0.95$ vs the binomial reference of $1.15\%$, with reserve-guidance threshold $k^\ast = 3$ stable across the 24-cell sub-period grid (0/24 stability rejections). Against parametric baselines on the same slice M6 carries 39/40 per-symbol Kupiec passes versus GARCH-$t$'s 31/40. The empirical content of P2 remains *per-anchor calibration*, not full-distribution calibration (Berkowitz still rejects pooled — the residual is cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode, orthogonal to σ̂; §9.4); the §6.3.4 joint-tail measurement turns this disclosure into a measurable, reportable shape. The deployed schedule is *deployment-calibrated and walk-forward-stable*, with §6.7 forward-tape held-out re-validation accumulating weekly.

- **(P3) Per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets.** M6 deploys 16 scalars: 12 trained per-regime quantiles + 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ + 0 walk-forward (the $\delta(\tau)$ schedule is identically zero under per-symbol σ̂ standardisation). Of the 4 $c(\tau)$ scalars, three are essentially identity ($c \in \{1.000,\,1.000,\,1.003\}$ at $\tau \in \{0.68,\,0.85,\,0.99\}$); only $c(0.95) = 1.079$ carries meaningful OOS information. **3 of 16 deployment scalars carry meaningful OOS-fit information.** The σ̂ multiplier delivers per-symbol width allocation: heavy-tail equities receive bands matched to their relative-residual scale; the defensive class (GLD, TLT) receives narrower bands without losing coverage (§7.4).

## 11.2 What the ablation says is doing the work

The §7 ladder reduces to four load-bearing components:

1. **Factor-switchboard point.** Tightens pooled bands $\sim 39\%$ vs a stale-hold reference; the input whose residual distribution the conformal quantile is fit on.

2. **Regime classifier $\rho$.** §7.1 established that a deployable architecture without regime structure cannot deliver coverage on the 2023+ slice; §7.2 isolated the M2 → M5 narrowing as the per-regime conformal lookup replacing Soothsayer-v0's hybrid forecaster ladder.

3. **Per-symbol pre-Friday σ̂ standardisation.** Adding a per-symbol $\hat\sigma_s(t)$ multiplier to the conformity score, with no other architectural change relative to an unweighted Mondrian comparator, takes per-symbol Kupiec at $\tau = 0.95$ from 2/10 → 10/10, collapses the Berkowitz LR range from $0.9$–$224$ to $3.2$–$16.7$, and tightens LOSO realised-coverage std by $5.7\times$. The §6.8 simulation study confirms this fix is structural — predicted in advance under four DGPs with known ground truth.

4. **Near-identity OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ bump.** Meaningful only at $\tau = 0.95$ (a 7.9% widening); at the other three anchors essentially identity. The walk-forward $\delta(\tau)$ schedule is identically zero — per-symbol σ̂ standardisation tightens cross-split realised-coverage variance enough that no structural-conservatism shift is required.

The remaining components from earlier ablation rungs (Soothsayer-v0's VIX-scaled standardisation, log-log vol regression, per-symbol vol index, long-weekend regressor, earnings flag, hybrid regime-to-forecaster policy; the M5 walk-forward $\delta(\tau)$ schedule) are dropped at M6; the simplification is what makes the §11.1 P3 contraction possible.

## 11.3 Honest framing

- **Per-anchor calibration, not full-distribution calibration.** Berkowitz and DQ both reject pooled. §6.3.6 / §9.4 localise the residual to *cross-sectional within-weekend* common-mode ($\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$), orthogonal to σ̂ standardisation. §6.3.4's joint-tail empirical distribution turns the residual into a measurable reserve-guidance threshold ($k^\ast = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$, stable across the 24-cell sub-period grid); the §10.2 cross-sectional partial-out track is the candidate methodology fix, gated on a Friday-observable predictor not yet available.

- **σ̂ selection multi-test exposure (mitigated).** The §7.3 σ̂ selection ran 80 split-date Christoffersen tests across 5 variants × 16 cells; under BH correction at FDR=0.05, no variant has any rejected cell. The deployment decision rests on Gate 3 (bootstrap CI on width — no multi-test issue) plus the §6.7 / §7.3.6 forward-tape variant comparison harness (held-out re-validation, never used to re-select). We do not claim the σ̂ rule is optimal among all locally-weighted variants.

- **OOS-fit $c(0.95) = 1.079$.** One scalar of the 16 is meaningfully OOS-fit. §9.3 reports the three independent provenance checks (4-split powered walk-forward, 4-anchor split-date sensitivity, 10-fold leave-one-symbol-out); all pass. Forward-tape evidence accumulates weekly.

- **Shock-tertile floor.** Realised $\tau = 0.95$ coverage on the heaviest-move tertile is $87.8\%$ — the residual is cross-sectional common-mode, not per-symbol scale, and does not yield to σ̂ choice. The §6.3.4 reserve-guidance threshold and the §6.3.5 worst-weekend vignette (2024-08-05 BoJ unwind) make this disclosure operationally addressable through a circuit-breaker handle.

- **Forward-tape held-out window operational, accumulating.** Live integrator window remains an open external-validity gap; forward-tape carries the held-out coverage statement weekly until that window opens.

The textbook econometric default — per-symbol GARCH(1,1) on log Friday-to-Monday returns — was extended to GARCH-$t$ (the practitioner standard for fat-tailed returns) on the same OOS slice. GARCH-$t$ closes the $\tau = 0.99$ Kupiec gap that GARCH-Gaussian leaves on the table but still under-covers Kupiec at every $\tau < 0.99$; at matched 95% realised coverage GARCH-$t$ widens to $\approx 378$ bps — statistically tied with M6's $370.6$. Across the 40-cell (symbol × $\tau$) grid M6 carries 39/40 per-symbol Kupiec passes versus GARCH-$t$'s 31/40. The conformal route fixes both Kupiec and Christoffersen because it is calibrated to the data, not to a fitted parametric distribution.

The remaining statistical gaps are useful bad news rather than project-fatal news. They name the boundary of what a per-anchor-calibrated locally-weighted-Mondrian interval oracle can claim. §10 names the candidate paths forward; §6.7 carries the held-out evidence weekly.

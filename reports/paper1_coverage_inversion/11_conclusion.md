# §11 — Conclusion

This paper specified a calibration-transparent oracle primitive for tokenised real-world assets, evaluated it on 12 years of US-listed weekend data, and shipped a reference implementation with end-to-end Python ↔ Rust ↔ on-chain parity. The contribution is the *interface* (consumer-selects-coverage with a per-read receipt) and the *deployable architecture* (Mondrian split-conformal by regime + factor-adjusted point + δ-shifted $c(\tau)$), arrived at through the precursor v1 forecaster ablation (`reports/methodology_history.md`), the §7.1 constant-buffer stress test, and the §7.2 Mondrian head-to-head that produced the deployed v2 / M5 schedule.

## 11.1 The three properties, revisited

The contract of §3.4 holds on the deployed v2 / M5 system as follows.

- **(P1) Auditability.** The deployment artefact (`data/processed/mondrian_artefact_v2.parquet` per-Friday rows + `data/processed/mondrian_artefact_v2.json` 20-scalar sidecar) is reconstructible from public data — Soothsayer consumes Scryer parquet for Yahoo daily prices, the per-symbol factor returns (ES / NQ / GC / ZN / BTC), and the regime classifier inputs (gap-days + VIX). The per-read `PricePoint` exposes the eight-field receipt of §4.5 (target coverage, $\delta(\tau)$ shift applied, claimed coverage served, forecaster used = "mondrian", regime, sharpness, $c(\tau')$ bump, per-regime quantile $q_r(\tau')$, effective $q_\text{eff}$), and the on-chain `PriceUpdate` PDA mirrors that receipt under the byte-for-byte parity contract of §8.5. A third party in possession of the audit-trail sidecar and the per-Friday artefact can reconstruct any served band from the receipt alone.

- **(P2) Conditional empirical coverage.** On the temporally disjoint 2023+ slice (1{,}730 rows × 173 weekends; 12 trained quantiles frozen on the 4{,}266 pre-2023 rows; 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ bumps and 4 walk-forward-fit $\delta(\tau)$ shifts deployment-tuned on the same slice and walk-forward-stable per §6.4), the served band at $\tau = 0.95$ realises coverage $0.950$ with Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ and Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.912$ at a mean half-width of $354.5$ bps (§6.4) — a 20% reduction over the v1 hybrid Oracle on the same slice (block-bootstrap CI excludes zero on width, straddles zero on coverage). At $\tau = 0.99$ the deployed band hits the consumer's nominal target (realised $0.990$, Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.942$), closing the v1 finite-sample tail ceiling at the cost of a 22% wider band. Kupiec passes at all four anchors; Christoffersen passes at the three deployment-relevant anchors ($\tau \in \{0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$) and rejects at $\tau = 0.68$ via the same coarse-regime-classifier mechanism that drives the §6.4.1 Berkowitz / DQ rejections. The empirical content of P2 is therefore *per-anchor calibration* in the Kupiec sense at every anchor, in the Christoffersen sense at the three deployment-relevant anchors, and *not full-distribution calibration* (Berkowitz LR $= 173.1$, DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ $p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$). The number is *deployment-calibrated and walk-forward-stable* rather than purely held-out end-to-end; §10.1's V3.2 (rolling artefact rebuild on a live deployment window) is the upgrade that would close the remaining gap.

- **(P3) Per-regime serving efficiency at deployment-tuned parameter budgets.** The v2 / M5 deployment matches the v1 Oracle's parameter budget — 4 OOS-fit scalars in `BUFFER_BY_TARGET` are replaced by 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ bumps + 4 walk-forward-fit $\delta(\tau)$ shifts, with the latter as the structural-conservatism analogue of the v1 buffer's deployment-tuned overshoot. The 12 trained per-regime quantiles replace the v1 surface (which carried per-(symbol, regime, claimed) cells but at $N \approx 50$–$300$ per cell). At identical OOS parameter budget, the v2 / M5 architecture is 19–20% narrower at indistinguishable realised coverage at every $\tau \le 0.95$ (block-bootstrap CIs exclude zero on width, straddle zero on coverage; §7.2.2). The §7.2 ablation establishes the deployable simpler baseline that survives both the §7.1 constant-buffer stress test (which the v1 architecture also survived) and the §7.2 head-to-head against v1 (which the v1 architecture, by definition, didn't have to face).

## 11.2 What the ablation says is doing the work

The §7 ladder reduces to three load-bearing components in the deployed v2 / M5 architecture.

1. **Factor-switchboard point.** The A0 → A1 v1 ladder rung tightens pooled bands by 39.3% (CI [37.3%, 41.3%]) at $n = 5{,}996$. The factor switchboard is inherited by v2 / M5 as the conformal lookup's input estimator $\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}$ (§4.1).

2. **Regime classifier $\rho$.** The §7.1 constant-buffer stress test established that a deployable architecture without regime structure cannot deliver coverage on the 2023+ slice (train-fit constant buffer undercovers OOS by 5–14pp at $\tau \le 0.95$; Kupiec rejects at $p_{uc} < 10^{-6}$). The §7.2 Mondrian ablation established that a deployable architecture *with* the regime classifier and *without* the v1 hybrid forecaster machinery (factor switchboard already kept; log-log VIX, per-symbol vol indices, earnings flag, long-weekend flag dropped) is the v2 / M5 deployment.

3. **OOS-tuned $c(\tau)$ + $\delta(\tau)$ schedules.** The 4 + 4 deployment-tuned scalars play the same role under v2 / M5 that the 4 BUFFER_BY_TARGET scalars played under v1: closing the train-OOS distribution-shift gap that the trained quantile (or surface) leaves on its own. §7.2.4 establishes that the $\delta$ schedule is load-bearing for walk-forward Kupiec attainment at $\tau \le 0.85$ where the bare $c(\tau)$ undercovers by 0.7–6.6pp.

The remaining v1 components (VIX-scaled standardisation, log-log vol regression, per-symbol vol index, long-weekend regressor, earnings-next-week flag, hybrid regime-to-forecaster policy) are dropped by v2 / M5; the load-bearing-vs-cosmetic taxonomy in `reports/methodology_history.md` marks each as cosmetic-under-M5. The simplification is what enabled the §7.2 width reduction.

## 11.3 Honest framing

Three scoping points warrant restatement at the close of the paper. First, the served band is *per-anchor calibrated*, not *full-distribution calibrated*. The Berkowitz joint LR on M5's walk-forward PITs rejects ($\text{LR} = 173.1$) and DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ rejects ($p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$); the rejections share the v1 architecture's diagnosis (§7.2.5) — the regime classifier $\rho$ is a coarse three-bin index that absorbs first-moment variation across regimes but leaves residual autocorrelation through high-vol weekend clusters. The product contract was always per-anchor; the Berkowitz / DQ disclosure makes the boundary explicit and identifies the v3 research target (§10.2's full-distribution conformal upgrade).

Second, the $\tau = 0.99$ tail ceiling under v1 is closed under v2 / M5 (§6.4, §7.2.2). The trade is a 22% wider band ($677.5$ vs $522.8$ bps) for honest coverage attainment at the consumer's nominal target. A protocol that prefers the v1 trade — narrower band at the cost of disclosed under-coverage — can recover it by requesting $\tau = 0.97$ from v2 / M5 instead. The customer-selects-coverage interface is what makes this trade a consumer choice.

Third, the v2 / M5 deployment is *deployment-calibrated* on the same OOS slice that §6 evaluates the served band on, via 4 + 4 OOS-fit scalars. The 6-split walk-forward of §6.4 + §7.2.4 ratifies the deployed schedule (per-anchor Kupiec $p$ = 0.43, 0.37, 0.36, 0.32 at $\tau \in \{0.68, 0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$) but does not upgrade the result to a purely held-out end-to-end validation. §9.3 carries the wider disclosure of OOS-tuning provenance and §10.1's V3.2 (rolling artefact rebuild) is the upgrade path.

The remaining statistical gaps are useful bad news rather than project-fatal news. Berkowitz and DQ do not say that the coverage-inversion primitive is void; they say that the present implementation has reached the boundary of what an anchor-calibrated interval oracle can claim, and the §10.1 v3 roadmap names the path forward.

## 11.4 What sits in the companion papers

The §3.5 non-claims and the §9.7 disclosure bracket one decision-theoretic question that the present paper cannot answer with the evidence it presents. *Paper 3* (optimal liquidation-policy defaults) addresses the mapping from a calibrated band to a $\{\texttt{Safe}, \texttt{Caution}, \texttt{Liquidate}\}$ action under an explicit borrower-book LTV distribution and a protocol-specific cost model. The active research structure is therefore methodology (this paper) → policy (Paper 3). A reviewer of the present paper need not commit to that framing; the coverage-inversion primitive stands alone as an oracle contract, regardless of how downstream consumers use the band.

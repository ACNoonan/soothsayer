# §10 — Future Work

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in five categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3), downstream protocol-policy work (§10.4), and scope-expansion paths beyond US-equity weekends (§10.5). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`; this section reproduces the items that are paper-relevant and explains the precondition each one waits on.

The deployed architecture is **v2 / M5** (Mondrian split-conformal by regime + factor-adjusted point + δ-shifted $c(\tau)$, paper §4 + §7.7). The v1 hybrid forecaster Oracle is the architecture this paper's §7.1–§7.5 ablation produced and that v2 superseded. The upgrades below are therefore **v3** items relative to the deployed v2 architecture, organised by the §9 boundary they aim to close. Berkowitz rejection marks the boundary between per-anchor calibration and full-distribution calibration. DQ rejection marks the boundary between simple violation de-clustering and a richer conditional-dynamics model. The remaining items below should therefore be read as a deliberately gap-driven v3 agenda rather than as an unprioritised wishlist.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

Five upgrades are blocked only on data history. Each becomes runnable as soon as its trigger fires.

**V3.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP).** The most likely reviewer ask, and the most-watched §9 item. Cong et al. show that on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens during closed-market intervals; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch on-chain history through the cutoff of this paper, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (`docs/v5-tape.md`, continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA is Q3–Q4 2026 at current accumulation. The deliverable is a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of the §6 OOS results admitting it into the candidate pool $\mathcal{F}$ alongside $\texttt{F1\_emp\_regime}$ and $\texttt{F0\_stale}$.

**V3.2 — Rolling artefact rebuild.** The deployed v2 / M5 artefact is a one-shot fit on pre-2023 data. A production deployment will see the per-regime quantile and the $c(\tau)$ schedule drift as the calibration target ages relative to the live regime. The fix is a monthly cron that re-runs `scripts/build_mondrian_artefact.py` against an updated panel, re-measures the 12 trained quantiles and the 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ scalars, and writes the fresh values to a versioned `mondrian_artefact_v2.parquet` for hot-reload. The trigger is the live-deployment window (§9.7); until then, the static artefact is correct. Paper-side, the deliverable is a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across ≥ 6 monthly rebuilds.

**V3.3 — MEV-aware consumer-experienced coverage.** §9.12's open question. Requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events, not just the mid we measure against $P_t$. The V5 tape supplies on-chain prints; Jito-bundle reconstruction supplies the ordering. ETA is Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. The deliverable is a §9 disclosure of the gap $\Delta = \text{reported} - \text{consumer-experienced}$ in basis points, not a methodology change.

**V3.4 — Intra-weekend forward-signal updating.** The current served band is fixed at the Friday-close publish event and not refreshed through the weekend. CME Globex reopens Sunday 18:00 ET on the E-mini S&P (`ES=F`) and gives ~3.5 hours of directly-observed forward signal on the Monday open before the publisher's next firing. A consumer reading the Soothsayer band at Sunday 19:00 ET sees the Friday-close band, not a band recentred on the realised ES weekend basis. This V3.4 architectural workstream is therefore distinct from the F\_tok upgrade of V3.1 (which adds an *on-chain* signal): Sunday-evening updating consumes an *off-chain*, already-public, free signal that exists today but the publish protocol does not currently consume. The three sub-deliverables are (i) a Sunday 18:30–22:00 ET republish window in the publisher CLI that conditions the served point on observed ES open and re-runs the empirical-quantile inversion, (ii) a `published_at` field in the receipt distinguishing the Friday-close versus Sunday-evening publish event, and (iii) a §6-shaped backtest of "Sunday-evening updated band" coverage versus the Friday-close-only baseline on the same OOS slice. The mechanical addition to F1 is a single weekend-basis term — replacing the F1 point's reliance on Friday's stale futures basis with the realised Sunday-evening basis — and does not require new data. The reviewer-asked baseline of §7.1 (the F0_VIX rung) showed that index-level VIX cannot price single-stock weekends; the natural complement is that the directly-observed ES Sunday-evening reopen *can* price the index basis, and a published band should consume it. The data trigger here is operational rather than longitudinal: a six-month Sunday-evening capture window — supplied by the existing scryer Yahoo-equity ingest at 1-hour cadence around the reopen — to support OOS evaluation of the updated-band methodology. Until that capture accumulates, the workstream is specified but not measured.

**V3.5 — Full-distribution conformal calibration.** §6.4.1 reports Berkowitz on M5's walk-forward PITs at LR $= 173.1$ and DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ at $p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$; the per-anchor-only calibration profile is shared with v1 (§7.7.5) and is the deepest open methodological item. The v2 / M5 architecture *partially* realises a conformal-prediction upgrade — the per-regime quantile $q_r(\tau)$ is the standard Mondrian split-conformal quantile under exchangeability within a regime, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is the OOS-fit conservatism that closes the train-OOS distribution-shift gap. What remains for v3 is a *full-distribution* conformal procedure: rather than four discrete anchor τ each calibrated separately by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage guarantees at every τ simultaneously. The conformalised quantile-regression variant of Romano-Patterson-Candès (2019) is the closest methodological match (the per-regime quantile becomes a quantile-regression model, the $c(\tau)$ overshoot becomes a conformal correction). The deliverable is a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on the v3 walk-forward PITs at the cost of the conformal procedure's wider per-anchor bands.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

Five upgrades require coding effort, not data accumulation. All are v3 territory relative to the deployed v2 / M5 architecture.

**[Superseded — v1.5 BMA-CAViaR proposal closed by v2 / M5].** A pre-M5 work package documented in `reports/methodology_history.md` (2026-04-28 entries) proposed a "v1.5 deployment" combining pooled-tail cross-symbol quantile (Trial 3) + multi-horizon volatility regressor (Trial 6) + a Bayesian Model Averaging of three forecasters at τ = 0.99 to close the v1 finite-sample tail ceiling. The v2 / M5 deployment closes the same gap by a different mechanism (per-regime conformal quantile pooled across symbols, §4.2; passes Kupiec at τ = 0.99 with realised $0.990$, §6.4) at a tighter parameter budget (16 deployed scalars vs the v1.5 BMA's per-symbol weights + per-component fit) and at substantially lower production-port effort. The v1.5 BMA-CAViaR plan is therefore retired; its empirical exploration informs §7.5 on what the v1 architecture's tail ceiling was *not* about (parametric tail estimation alone) and its methodology lineage is preserved in `reports/methodology_history.md` for context.

**EVT for the τ = 0.99 ceiling — Trial 1 disposition and the CEVT v2 swing.** The textbook reviewer ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold extreme value theory (POT-EVT) per Pickands-Balkema-de Haan; we treat this explicitly. Trial 1 of the family-A program (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with an unconditional generalized Pareto fit on the upper-decile exceedances of the standardised residuals (~15-16 exceedances per fit, comfortably above the 8-exceedance stability floor for GPD MLE). The trial is therefore *diagnostic*: the per-symbol DQ rejection pattern at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical under GPD and empirical (same six rejecting symbols, reject-count 6 / 10 for both), and bandwidth widened from 449 bps to 484 bps at no improvement on the headline DQ diagnostic. The reading is not that EVT-POT failed; it is that the $\tau = 0.99$ miscalibration *is not a tail-quantile-noise mechanism* — if it were, parametric tail extrapolation from the same exceedances would visibly help. The implied mechanism is GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence in the standardised residuals, which the unconditional GPD does not address. The technically-correct EVT response to this diagnosis is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on the standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000], a two-stage estimator that targets exactly the conditional-persistence mechanism Trial 1 surfaces. We park CEVT in v2 calibration-surface revision territory rather than the v1.5 BMA scope above: per-symbol GARCH fits refit on every rolling-window step are heavier than the Trial 3 + Trial 6 + BMA combination that already passes the v1.5 scorecard at 2 / 10 DQ rejects. Both EVT framings remain on the methodology record — the unconditional fit empirically falsified as the headline-DQ fix, the conditional fit named as the next swing once the v2 budget admits the per-window GARCH cost.

**Full-distribution conformal upgrade (v3).** The v2 / M5 architecture is *partially* a conformal upgrade — the per-regime quantile $q_r(\tau)$ is a Mondrian split-conformal quantile under exchangeability within a regime, and the §7.7 ablation showed it dominates the v1 surface inversion on width-at-coverage. What remains is to extend Mondrian conformal from four discrete anchor τ to a full-distribution procedure. The v1-era conformal-comparison study (`reports/v1b_archive_conformal_comparison.md` after archive) tested vanilla split-conformal, Barber et al. (2022) nexCP at 6/12-month half-lives, and block-recency baselines under the v1 surface architecture; the v2 swap to Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime largely realises that work's promise (the §7.7 width reduction is the load-bearing improvement). The remaining v3 question is whether full-distribution conformal — calibrating against the entire residual CDF rather than four discrete τ — passes Berkowitz / DQ at the cost of wider per-anchor bands. The conformalised quantile-regression variant of Romano-Patterson-Candès (2019) is the closest methodological match.

**Sub-regime granularity and richer state.** The §6.4.1 DQ-test pooled-$\chi^2$ rejection at all four anchor τ — partially attributed to per-symbol-pooling inflation by the median-p sensitivity of §6.4.1 (which passes at all four anchors), but with an irreducible per-symbol component at τ = 0.99 (5 / 10 symbols reject individually) — flags multi-lag conditional structure that Christoffersen's two-state Markov independence test misses. The probable mechanism is volatility-regime persistence within `normal` (post-shock weekends, calm range-bound weekends, recovery weekends) that the current three-state $\rho$ aggregates. Splitting `normal` into sub-regimes — using either trailing realised-vol tertiles or a rolling regime classifier — should absorb part of the multi-lag autocorrelation. A second candidate mechanism is omitted state: on-chain tokenized-stock prices, weekend futures microstructure, or event-level covariates may carry information the current regime labeler discards. The DQ rejection is therefore not just a call for more buckets; it is evidence that the current state representation still leaves predictable structure in misses, which is exactly the gap a richer Solana-native tape can help close.

**Halt / corp-action filter on the OOS panel (gated on scryer wishlist).** A complementary diagnostic — orthogonal to the sub-regime work above — is to filter the §6.4 OOS panel for weekends in which one or more symbols had a NASDAQ trading halt or an underlying-equity corporate action (split, special dividend, merger announcement) in the post-publish window, then rerun the §6.4.1 DQ test on the filtered panel. The hypothesis is that a non-trivial fraction of the 5 / 10 per-symbol rejections at τ = 0.99 reflects structural exceptions rather than persistent calibration gaps. Two scryer-side data items gate this work: (a) a `yahoo.corp_actions` venue (yfinance `Ticker.actions` carries split + dividend history for all ten symbols decades back; clean wishlist add) and (b) a NASDAQ historical-halts backfill for 2023-01 → 2026-04 (the existing `nasdaq_halts.live` poller is forward-only since 2026-04-24; the public NASDAQ archive covers ~18 months back, full coverage requires a paid SIP or commercial vendor). Both items are queued in `scryer/wishlist.md`. The deliverable returns to §6.4.1 as a one-table sensitivity update and, if the filtered τ = 0.99 reject-count drops below 3 / 10, refines the §9.1 structural-ceiling attribution from "cannot resolve the 1% tail" toward "cannot resolve the 1% tail given a non-trivial fraction of weekends carry structural exceptions the regime labeler does not encode." A natural v2 extension converts the diagnostic filter into a fitted *centre-shift* adjustment on ex-dividend weekends — mechanical (Monday opens lower by the dividend amount net of overnight risk premium), not a fit — together with a per-event-type variance uplift on the broader corporate-action class; we treat that as v2 calibration-surface work distinct from the v1.5-targeted diagnostic filter, because the overlay must compose with F1's existing $\gamma_\text{earn}$ term in §4.1 and with the per-(symbol, regime) calibration surface rather than just the pre-DQ panel.

**Asymmetric / Student-t F0.** F0 is a Gaussian fallback. A Student-t or skew-normal alternative would reduce the high-vol band over-coverage noted in §6.3 in-sample. Mechanical port; deliverable when re-evaluating $\texttt{REGIME\_FORECASTER}$ becomes part of a v2 surface rebuild.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

Two paper-relevant items with no methodology component.

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.7, closes §9.8).** §6.7 reports a partial-only side-by-side numerical comparison against Pyth Hermes (265 obs, 2024+, eight-of-ten symbols) and Chainlink Data Streams v11 (87 obs, Feb–Apr 2026, eight xStock tickers; pre-decoder-correction sample). The dashboard upgrade has three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders, replacing §6.7.2 with a uniform-decoder dataset of comparable horizon to the §6.4 OOS window; (b) Pyth coverage extending to the off-hours and pre/post-market feeds and to the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time), closing the §6.7.1 sample-composition gap; (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape — continuous capture began 2026-04-26 on the scryer ingest fleet (`data/processed/redstone_live_tape.parquet`) — to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.8.1. The dashboard becomes paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification. A Phase 2 deliverable per `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes §9.7).** The §6 OOS coverage claim is backtested. The Rust + Anchor serving stack of §8 is functionally complete and verified (75/75 byte-for-byte parity), but has not run as a production feed consumed by a real protocol. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode that requires production-environment realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). The output is a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery used here. Importantly, this closes the external-validity gap; it does not, by itself, resolve the current Berkowitz / DQ rejections, which is why the statistical upgrades above remain distinct work packages rather than presumed consequences of going live.

## 10.4 Downstream protocol-policy work

The §3.5 non-claims and the §9.9 disclosure (no optimal-liquidation-policy benchmark) signal that one independent decision-theoretic question is out of scope for the coverage-inversion primitive paper.

**Paper 3 — Optimal liquidation-policy defaults.** Given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? The decision-theoretic mapping from band $[L_t, U_t]$ to action $\{\texttt{Safe}, \texttt{Caution}, \texttt{Liquidate}\}$ requires the three ingredients §9.9 enumerates: a borrower-book LTV weight distribution, a protocol-specific cost model, and a declared semantics for what counts as the correct realised action. The existing protocol-compare scaffolding (`scripts/run_protocol_compare.py`, `src/soothsayer/backtest/protocol_compare.py`) plus the planned walk-forward extension supports this paper. See `reports/paper3_liquidation_policy/plan.md`.

The research structure is therefore methodology (this paper) → policy (Paper 3). A reviewer of the present paper need not commit to that framing; the coverage-inversion primitive stands alone as an oracle contract regardless of how downstream consumers use it.

## 10.5 Scope expansion

**Multi-region replication (closes §9.10).** All ten tickers in this evaluation are US-listed; the factor switchboard privileges US-session futures. A natural replication target is tokenised LSE equities (factor `FTSE`, vol index `^VFTSE`) and tokenised JP equities (factor `^N225` or `NK=F`, vol index `^VXJ`). The methodology generalises by construction (the `FACTOR_BY_SYMBOL` and `VOL_INDEX_BY_SYMBOL` switchboards of §5.4 are designed for this); the deliverable is empirical evidence that the surface achieves §6-comparable Kupiec / Christoffersen pass rates on a non-US weekend window.

**Additional RWA classes — the methodology as a missing-layer for the broader RWA wave.** GLD and TLT in the present panel exercise non-equity factor + vol-index pairs and serve as anchors that the calibration-transparency primitive is not US-equity-specific. The tokenized RWA wave currently moving on-chain is materially broader than the equity slice this paper evaluates: tokenized treasuries (BlackRock BUIDL and Ondo OUSG together exceed $5B AUM as of submission), tokenized commodities (Paxos PAXG, with silver and broader baskets in flight), tokenized credit and FX derivatives, and the equities-on-Solana xStock universe that motivated the present evaluation. Each new class is, methodologically, a new `(factor, vol_index)` pair plus a re-fit of the calibration surface — no methodology change is required.

What changes across these classes is the *information set* available during the closed-market window. Tokenized US equities have ES/NQ futures + VIX. Tokenized gold has GC futures + GVZ. Tokenized treasuries have ZN/ZF/ZB futures + MOVE. Tokenized credit has CDS spreads + index volatility. Each pair satisfies the methodology's requirement of a continuous off-hours information set; for any such class, the calibration-transparent primitive is directly applicable and the public-good infrastructure it provides — auditable bands at consumer-specified $\tau$, with on-chain receipts — generalises by construction.

§9.10 enumerates the explicit out-of-scope cases: real estate (no off-hours information set; NAV updates are discrete and infrequent), illiquid agricultural commodities (futures exist but are discontinuous), and instruments whose primary discovery happens at irregular cadences. We do *not* claim every RWA needs Soothsayer-shaped calibration infrastructure — only that for the substantial subset with continuous off-hours data, it is the missing infrastructure layer that responsible institutional-scale onboarding requires. `docs/methodology_scope.md` in the artifact accompanying this paper carries the per-class fit/no-fit table.

The deliverable for this future-work item is therefore not "extend Soothsayer to one new ticker"; it is "demonstrate the calibration-transparency primitive on a representative sample from each broad RWA class with a continuous off-hours information set, using the same Kupiec / Christoffersen disclosure framework as §6, on a panel that protocol integrators and institutional risk teams already speak."

## 10.6 Sample-size eligibility — research direction surfaced by the §6.4.1 residual

The §6.4.1 per-symbol DQ disclosure surfaces a structural feature of the methodology that has been latent in the analysis but not yet formalised: the methodology stack has *sample-size requirements per component*, and a symbol's eligibility for a given target $\tau$ is conditional on its calibration history meeting those requirements. The eight-mechanism-family scorecard documented in `reports/methodology_history.md` (entries dated 2026-04-28 / 2026-04-29) demonstrates that HOOD's $\tau = 0.99$ miscalibration is *invariant under every parametric tail-modeling family we tested* (GPD / pooled-tail / per-symbol vol regression / multi-horizon HAR-RV / Mondrian conformal / CAViaR-SAV / CAViaR-AS / Hawkes-POT) and that this invariance correlates exactly with HOOD's *minimum data footprint* in the universe (245 OOS weekends, 73 pre-2023 calibration weekends, smallest of the ten symbols). The other nine symbols, all with $n \in [340, 450]$ pre-2023 calibration weekends, satisfy the methodology stack's empirical sample-size floors and yield the v1.5 BMA hybrid's 2 / 10 reject-count at $\tau = 0.99$ documented in §10.2.

Promoting this observation from a per-symbol disclosure to a *deployment policy* is a single research direction with a methodology layer (per-component sample-size floors) and a product layer (on-chain admission contract). The two layers share one empirical anchor (the HOOD case), one table (the eligibility map), and one deployment surface (the Soothsayer router program); they are one work package, not two. Whether the work eventually merits a standalone paper, a technical-report extension to the present paper, or a section in a later downstream paper (Paper 3's lending-protocol policy is the closest fit) is a decision deferred to the time the work is actually done — consistent with the project's depth-over-volume publication discipline.

Sketch of the work package:

- For each component of the methodology stack — F1 σ regression, empirical-quantile at the served claimed-$q$, pooled-tail above the cross-symbol threshold, CAViaR-SAV/AS via Engle-Manganelli quantile autoregression, GPD on tail exceedances per Pickands-Balkema-de Haan, Hawkes-POT for self-exciting tail clustering — derive the empirical sample-size floor below which the component fails to fit or produces unstable bounds. The methodology log entry tabulates these floors as derived from this project's eight-trial scorecard plus the standard finite-sample-rate bounds in conformal prediction [vovk-book-2005; barber-nexcp-2023], quantile regression, and EVT.
- Combine the per-component floors into an *eligibility table* mapping calibration history $n$ to the maximum $\tau$ a symbol can be served at. The methodology log entry proposes a five-row table: $n < 50$ not eligible (use F0 stale-hold with non-calibrated flag); $n \in [50, 100)$ eligible at $\tau \leq 0.85$; $n \in [100, 200)$ eligible at $\tau \leq 0.95$; $n \in [200, 500)$ eligible at $\tau \leq 0.99$ via the v1.5 BMA stack; $n \geq 500$ adds the optional Hawkes-POT layer.
- Operationalise the eligibility criterion as an *on-chain admission contract* in the Soothsayer router program: a freshly-listed tokenised RWA enters at $\tau_\max = 0.85$ and graduates to $\tau_\max = 0.99$ as its calibration history accumulates past the 200-row floor. This handles the new-token-launch trust-bootstrap problem that any deployed oracle for emerging tokenised assets faces — the protocol commits ex ante to refusing tail coverage until calibration is demonstrably sufficient, rather than serving a defective band whose miscalibration can only be detected ex post.

The present paper's contribution is the calibration-transparent primitive itself; the eligibility framework is the deployment-policy companion that the HOOD residual makes inevitable. The framework requires no Paper-1-side change beyond this disclosure of the conjecture.

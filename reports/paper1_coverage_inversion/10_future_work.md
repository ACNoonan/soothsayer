# §10 — Future Work (draft)

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in five categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3), the two companion papers that complete the trilogy (§10.4), and scope-expansion paths beyond US-equity weekends (§10.5). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/v2.md` and `docs/ROADMAP.md`; this section reproduces the items that are paper-relevant and explains the precondition each one waits on.

The organising principle is simple: the present draft already demonstrates that a calibration-transparent interval oracle is useful, but the same diagnostics that keep the paper honest also define the next research frontier. Berkowitz rejection marks the boundary between per-anchor calibration and full-distribution calibration. DQ rejection marks the boundary between simple violation de-clustering and a richer conditional-dynamics model. The remaining items below should therefore be read as a deliberately gap-driven V2 agenda rather than as an unprioritised wishlist.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

Four upgrades are blocked only on data history. Each becomes runnable as soon as its trigger fires.

**V2.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP).** The most likely reviewer ask, and the most-watched §9 item. Cong et al. show that on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens during closed-market intervals; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch on-chain history through the cutoff of this paper, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (`docs/v5-tape.md`, continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA is Q3–Q4 2026 at current accumulation. The deliverable is a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of the §6 OOS results admitting it into the candidate pool $\mathcal{F}$ alongside $\texttt{F1\_emp\_regime}$ and $\texttt{F0\_stale}$.

**V2.2 — Rolling calibration-surface rebuild.** The deployed surface is a one-shot fit on pre-2023 data. A production deployment will see the buffer schedule drift as the calibration target ages relative to the live regime. The fix is a monthly cron that rebuilds `v1b_bounds.parquet` and the two surface CSVs, re-measures `BUFFER_BY_TARGET` from each rebuild's held-out slice, and writes the fresh values to `buffer.json` for hot-reload. The trigger is the live-deployment window (§9.7); until then, the static surface is correct. Paper-side, the deliverable is a §6 buffer-trajectory figure across ≥ 6 monthly rebuilds.

**V2.3 — MEV-aware consumer-experienced coverage.** §9.12's open question. Requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events, not just the mid we measure against $P_t$. The V5 tape supplies on-chain prints; Jito-bundle reconstruction supplies the ordering. ETA is Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. The deliverable is a §9 disclosure of the gap $\Delta = \text{reported} - \text{consumer-experienced}$ in basis points, not a methodology change.

**V2.4 — Distribution-level calibration upgrade.** §6.4.1 reports Berkowitz on continuous PITs reconstructed from a 19-point τ grid; the test rejects, and we attribute the rejection to buffer-induced safe-direction over-coverage outside $[0.68, 0.99]$. The immediate follow-up is diagnostic — full distributional KS plus a per-regime Berkowitz decomposition — but the real V2 target is methodological: move from an anchor-calibrated interval oracle toward a more continuous distributional correction. Candidate paths are a denser claimed-quantile grid, a direct CDF-calibration layer, or a continuous correction replacing the current flat-extrapolated anchor schedule. The deliverable is not just "more tests"; it is a clearer answer to whether Soothsayer can graduate from per-anchor calibration to distribution-level calibration without sacrificing the current deployment-facing simplicity.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

Five upgrades require coding effort, not data accumulation. The first is the **near-term v1.5 deployment** (specified in `docs/v1.5-deployment-spec.md`; gated on scryer halt / corp-action data); the remainder are v2 territory.

**v1.5 deployment — pooled-tail + state-augmentation + BMA-CAViaR per-symbol hybrid (near-term).** The Family A continuation in `reports/methodology_history.md` (2026-04-28 very late evening) documents an eight-trial mechanism-family program that tested GPD (Trial 1), per-symbol Mondrian conformal (Trial 2), pooled-tail cross-symbol quantile (Trial 3), single- and multi-horizon volatility regressors (Trials 6, Family D), CAViaR-SAV and CAViaR-AS quantile autoregression (Family A), and Hawkes-POT self-exciting tail clustering (Family B). The combination that survives is: Trial 3 + Trial 6 + a Bayesian Model Averaging of three forecasters at τ = 0.99 with weights derived from 3-fold rolling time-series cross-validation on the pre-2023 calibration set. On held-out OOS, this combined v1.5 stack passes all four scorecard thresholds at τ = 0.99 with **per-symbol DQ reject-count of 2 / 10** (down from 5 / 10 in the current production); pooled realised 0.986; Christoffersen $p_\text{ind}$ = 0.392; Kupiec $p_{uc}$ = 0.081; mean half-width 510 bps. Two of the three structural rejectors from §6.4.1 (SPY via CV-favored CAViaR-SAV; AAPL implicitly via the equal-weight averaging of three CV-tied methods) are closed by the BMA scheme. HOOD remains the sole residual — addressed in §10.2 below by the halt / corp-action filter or in §10.5 by future-data integration. Production-port effort: ~7-10 days for the Python + Rust + parity + paper-update cycle once scryer items 15a + 15b ship. v1.5 ships *before* Paper 1 publication; the §6.4.1 headline updates to the BMA numbers at submission time.

**Conformal / continuous-calibration upgrade.** §9.4's principal weakness is that the per-target buffer is a heuristic, not a theorem. We tested vanilla split-conformal (Vovk), Barber et al. (2022) nexCP at 6/12-month half-lives, and a block-recency baseline; all three under-corrected the OOS gap relative to the per-target heuristic on this data, with bootstrap CIs that exclude zero (`reports/v1b_conformal_comparison.md`). Conformal is therefore *not* an obvious win on the existing claimed-coverage grid + sample size — but two changes flip the comparison: a finer claimed-quantile grid above $0.995$ (already partially landed in the 2026-04-25 grid extension to {0.997, 0.999}) shifts the inversion regime where conformal corrections are operationally meaningful, and a multi-split walk-forward evaluation distinguishes drift from sampling noise on the buffer comparison itself. The conformalised quantile-regression variant of Romano-Patterson-Candès (2019) is the closest methodological match: the F1 log-log volatility regression becomes a quantile regressor, and the empirical buffer becomes the conformal correction. More broadly, this work package asks whether the current piecewise anchor schedule should survive at all, or whether a continuous correction fit can dominate it on both interpretability and out-of-sample stability.

**Sub-regime granularity and richer state.** The §6.4.1 DQ-test pooled-$\chi^2$ rejection at all four anchor τ — partially attributed to per-symbol-pooling inflation by the median-p sensitivity of §6.4.1 (which passes at all four anchors), but with an irreducible per-symbol component at τ = 0.99 (5 / 10 symbols reject individually) — flags multi-lag conditional structure that Christoffersen's two-state Markov independence test misses. The probable mechanism is volatility-regime persistence within `normal` (post-shock weekends, calm range-bound weekends, recovery weekends) that the current three-state $\rho$ aggregates. Splitting `normal` into sub-regimes — using either trailing realised-vol tertiles or a rolling regime classifier — should absorb part of the multi-lag autocorrelation. A second candidate mechanism is omitted state: on-chain tokenized-stock prices, weekend futures microstructure, or event-level covariates may carry information the current regime labeler discards. The DQ rejection is therefore not just a call for more buckets; it is evidence that the current state representation still leaves predictable structure in misses, which is exactly the gap a richer Solana-native tape can help close.

**Halt / corp-action filter on the OOS panel (gated on scryer wishlist).** A complementary diagnostic — orthogonal to the sub-regime work above — is to filter the §6.4 OOS panel for weekends in which one or more symbols had a NASDAQ trading halt or an underlying-equity corporate action (split, special dividend, merger announcement) in the post-publish window, then rerun the §6.4.1 DQ test on the filtered panel. The hypothesis is that a non-trivial fraction of the 5 / 10 per-symbol rejections at τ = 0.99 reflects structural exceptions rather than persistent calibration gaps. Two scryer-side data items gate this work: (a) a `yahoo.corp_actions` venue (yfinance `Ticker.actions` carries split + dividend history for all ten symbols decades back; clean wishlist add) and (b) a NASDAQ historical-halts backfill for 2023-01 → 2026-04 (the existing `nasdaq_halts.live` poller is forward-only since 2026-04-24; the public NASDAQ archive covers ~18 months back, full coverage requires a paid SIP or commercial vendor). Both items are queued in `scryer/wishlist.md`. The deliverable returns to §6.4.1 as a one-table sensitivity update and, if the filtered τ = 0.99 reject-count drops below 3 / 10, refines the §9.1 structural-ceiling attribution from "cannot resolve the 1% tail" toward "cannot resolve the 1% tail given a non-trivial fraction of weekends carry structural exceptions the regime labeler does not encode."

**Asymmetric / Student-t F0.** F0 is a Gaussian fallback. A Student-t or skew-normal alternative would reduce the high-vol band over-coverage noted in §6.3 in-sample. Mechanical port; deliverable when re-evaluating $\texttt{REGIME\_FORECASTER}$ becomes part of a v2 surface rebuild.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

Two paper-relevant items with no methodology component.

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.7, closes §9.8).** §6.7 reports a partial-only side-by-side numerical comparison against Pyth Hermes (265 obs, 2024+, eight-of-ten symbols) and Chainlink Data Streams v11 (87 obs, Feb–Apr 2026, eight xStock tickers; pre-decoder-correction sample). The dashboard upgrade has three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders, replacing §6.7.2 with a uniform-decoder dataset of comparable horizon to the §6.4 OOS window; (b) Pyth coverage extending to the off-hours and pre/post-market feeds and to the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time), closing the §6.7.1 sample-composition gap; (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape — continuous capture began 2026-04-26 on the scryer ingest fleet (`data/processed/redstone_live_tape.parquet`) — to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.8.1. The dashboard becomes paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification. A Phase 2 deliverable per `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes §9.7).** The §6 OOS coverage claim is backtested. The Rust + Anchor serving stack of §8 is functionally complete and verified (75/75 byte-for-byte parity), but has not run as a production feed consumed by a real protocol. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode that requires production-environment realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). The output is a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery used here. Importantly, this closes the external-validity gap; it does not, by itself, resolve the current Berkowitz / DQ rejections, which is why the statistical upgrades above remain distinct work packages rather than presumed consequences of going live.

## 10.4 Companion papers (trilogy)

The §3.5 non-claims and the §9.9 disclosure (no optimal-liquidation-policy benchmark) signal that two independent decision-theoretic questions are out of scope for the coverage-inversion primitive paper. Both are addressed by companion papers already in plan.

**Paper 2 — OEV mechanism design.** Given a calibration-transparent oracle, what auction or trigger mechanism for oracle-extractable value (OEV) maximises protocol welfare? The existing OEV-recapture literature (Chainlink SVR, RedStone Atom, API3 OEV, UMA Oval, Pyth Express Relay) is written under an opaque-oracle assumption that calibration-transparent oracles explicitly violate; the calibration receipt of §4.5 is a primitive the auction can directly condition on. Deliverable in plan: an auction simulator (M0–M4 instantiation with builder/searcher coalition modelling), a theoretical equilibrium result on rent monotonicity in band sharpness, and an empirical-replay panel on the historical Aave V2/V3 + Kamino liquidation panel. See `reports/paper2_oev_mechanism_design/plan.md`.

**Paper 3 — Optimal liquidation-policy defaults.** Given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? The decision-theoretic mapping from band $[L_t, U_t]$ to action $\{\texttt{Safe}, \texttt{Caution}, \texttt{Liquidate}\}$ requires the three ingredients §9.9 enumerates: a borrower-book LTV weight distribution, a protocol-specific cost model, and a declared semantics for what counts as the correct realised action. The existing protocol-compare scaffolding (`scripts/run_protocol_compare.py`, `src/soothsayer/backtest/protocol_compare.py`) plus the planned walk-forward extension supports this paper. See `reports/paper3_liquidation_policy/plan.md`.

The trilogy structure — methodology (this paper) → mechanism (Paper 2) → policy (Paper 3) — is explicitly designed so that each layer's deliverable is independently citable. A reviewer of the present paper need not commit to the trilogy framing; the coverage-inversion primitive stands alone as an oracle contract regardless of how downstream consumers use it.

## 10.5 Scope expansion

**Multi-region replication (closes §9.10).** All ten tickers in this evaluation are US-listed; the factor switchboard privileges US-session futures. A natural replication target is tokenised LSE equities (factor `FTSE`, vol index `^VFTSE`) and tokenised JP equities (factor `^N225` or `NK=F`, vol index `^VXJ`). The methodology generalises by construction (the `FACTOR_BY_SYMBOL` and `VOL_INDEX_BY_SYMBOL` switchboards of §5.4 are designed for this); the deliverable is empirical evidence that the surface achieves §6-comparable Kupiec / Christoffersen pass rates on a non-US weekend window.

**Additional RWA classes — the methodology as a missing-layer for the broader RWA wave.** GLD and TLT in the present panel exercise non-equity factor + vol-index pairs and serve as anchors that the calibration-transparency primitive is not US-equity-specific. The tokenized RWA wave currently moving on-chain is materially broader than the equity slice this paper evaluates: tokenized treasuries (BlackRock BUIDL and Ondo OUSG together exceed $5B AUM as of submission), tokenized commodities (Paxos PAXG, with silver and broader baskets in flight), tokenized credit and FX derivatives, and the equities-on-Solana xStock universe that motivated the present evaluation. Each new class is, methodologically, a new `(factor, vol_index)` pair plus a re-fit of the calibration surface — no methodology change is required.

What changes across these classes is the *information set* available during the closed-market window. Tokenized US equities have ES/NQ futures + VIX. Tokenized gold has GC futures + GVZ. Tokenized treasuries have ZN/ZF/ZB futures + MOVE. Tokenized credit has CDS spreads + index volatility. Each pair satisfies the methodology's requirement of a continuous off-hours information set; for any such class, the calibration-transparent primitive is directly applicable and the public-good infrastructure it provides — auditable bands at consumer-specified $\tau$, with on-chain receipts — generalises by construction.

§9.10 enumerates the explicit out-of-scope cases: real estate (no off-hours information set; NAV updates are discrete and infrequent), illiquid agricultural commodities (futures exist but are discontinuous), and instruments whose primary discovery happens at irregular cadences. We do *not* claim every RWA needs Soothsayer-shaped calibration infrastructure — only that for the substantial subset with continuous off-hours data, it is the missing infrastructure layer that responsible institutional-scale onboarding requires. `docs/methodology_scope.md` in the artifact accompanying this paper carries the per-class fit/no-fit table.

The deliverable for this future-work item is therefore not "extend Soothsayer to one new ticker"; it is "demonstrate the calibration-transparency primitive on a representative sample from each broad RWA class with a continuous off-hours information set, using the same Kupiec / Christoffersen disclosure framework as §6, on a panel that protocol integrators and institutional risk teams already speak."

# §10 — Future Work

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in three categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), and empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`. Berkowitz rejection (§9.4) marks the boundary between per-anchor calibration and full-distribution calibration; DQ rejection marks the boundary between simple violation de-clustering and a richer conditional-dynamics model. The items below are organised around closing those boundaries.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

**V3.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP).** The most likely reviewer ask, and the most-watched §9 item. Cong et al. show that on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens during closed-market intervals; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch on-chain history through the cutoff of this paper, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (`docs/v5-tape.md`, continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA is Q3–Q4 2026 at current accumulation. The deliverable is a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of the §6 OOS results admitting it into the candidate pool alongside the deployed Mondrian forecaster.

**V3.2 — Rolling artefact rebuild.** The deployed v2 / M5 artefact is a one-shot fit on pre-2023 data. A production deployment will see the per-regime quantile and the $c(\tau)$ schedule drift as the calibration target ages relative to the live regime. The fix is a monthly cron that re-runs `scripts/build_mondrian_artefact.py` against an updated panel, re-measures the 12 trained quantiles and the 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ scalars, and writes the fresh values to a versioned `mondrian_artefact_v2.parquet` for hot-reload. The trigger is the live-deployment window (§9.5); until then, the static artefact is correct. Paper-side, the deliverable is a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across ≥ 6 monthly rebuilds.

**V3.3 — MEV-aware consumer-experienced coverage.** §9.9's open question. Requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events, not just the mid we measure against $P_t$. The V5 tape supplies on-chain prints; Jito-bundle reconstruction supplies the ordering. ETA is Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. The deliverable is a §9 disclosure of the gap $\Delta = \text{reported} - \text{consumer-experienced}$ in basis points, not a methodology change.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

**Full-distribution conformal calibration.** §6.4.1 reports Berkowitz on M5's walk-forward PITs at LR $= 173.1$ and DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ at $p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$; the per-anchor-only calibration profile (§7.2.5) is the deepest open methodological item. The v2 / M5 architecture *partially* realises a conformal-prediction upgrade — the per-regime quantile $q_r(\tau)$ is the standard Mondrian split-conformal quantile under exchangeability within a regime, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is the OOS-fit conservatism that closes the train-OOS distribution-shift gap. What remains for v3 is a *full-distribution* conformal procedure: rather than four discrete anchor τ each calibrated separately by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage guarantees at every τ simultaneously. The conformalised quantile-regression variant of Romano-Patterson-Candès (2019) is the closest methodological match. The deliverable is a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on the v3 walk-forward PITs at the cost of the conformal procedure's wider per-anchor bands.

**Sub-regime granularity and richer state.** The §6.4.1 DQ rejection — partially attributed to per-symbol-pooling inflation by the median-p sensitivity (which passes at all four anchors), but with an irreducible per-symbol component at τ = 0.99 — flags multi-lag conditional structure that Christoffersen's two-state Markov independence test misses. The probable mechanism is volatility-regime persistence within `normal` (post-shock weekends, calm range-bound weekends, recovery weekends) that the current three-state $\rho$ aggregates. Splitting `normal` into sub-regimes — using either trailing realised-vol tertiles or a rolling regime classifier — should absorb part of the multi-lag autocorrelation. A second candidate mechanism is omitted state: on-chain tokenized-stock prices, weekend futures microstructure, or event-level covariates may carry information the current regime labeler discards.

**Conditional EVT for the τ = 0.99 tail.** The textbook reviewer ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold extreme value theory per Pickands-Balkema-de Haan. An exploratory unconditional GPD trial on the v1 architecture (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with a generalized Pareto fit on upper-decile exceedances of the standardised residuals; the per-symbol DQ rejection pattern at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical to the empirical fit, identifying the τ = 0.99 miscalibration mechanism as GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence rather than tail-quantile noise. The technically-correct response is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on the standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000], a v3 calibration-surface upgrade that targets exactly the conditional-persistence mechanism the trial surfaced.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.7, closes §9.6).** §6.7 reports a partial-only side-by-side numerical comparison against Pyth Hermes (265 obs, 2024+, eight-of-ten symbols) and Chainlink Data Streams v11 (87 obs, Feb–Apr 2026, eight xStock tickers; pre-decoder-correction sample). The dashboard upgrade has three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders, replacing §6.7.2 with a uniform-decoder dataset of comparable horizon to the §6.4 OOS window; (b) Pyth coverage extending to the off-hours and pre/post-market feeds and to the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time), closing the §6.7.1 sample-composition gap; (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape — continuous capture began 2026-04-26 on the scryer ingest fleet — to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.6.1. The dashboard becomes paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes §9.5).** The §6 OOS coverage claim is backtested. The Rust + Anchor serving stack of §8 is functionally complete and verified (75/75 byte-for-byte parity), but has not run as a production feed consumed by a real protocol. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode that requires production-environment realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). The output is a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery used here. This closes the external-validity gap; it does not, by itself, resolve the current Berkowitz / DQ rejections, which is why the statistical upgrades above remain distinct work packages.

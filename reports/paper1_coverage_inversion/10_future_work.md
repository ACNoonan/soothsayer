# §10 — Future Work

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in three categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), and empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

**V3.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP).** The most likely reviewer ask. Cong et al. show on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch history, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA Q3–Q4 2026. Deliverable: a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of §6 results admitting it into the candidate pool.

**V3.2 — Rolling artefact rebuild.** The deployed M5 artefact is a one-shot fit; in production the per-regime quantile and $c(\tau)$ schedule will drift as the calibration target ages. The fix is a monthly cron that re-runs `scripts/build_mondrian_artefact.py` against an updated panel and writes a versioned artefact for hot-reload. Trigger: the live-deployment window (§9.5). Paper-side deliverable: a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across ≥ 6 monthly rebuilds.

**V3.3 — Path-fitted conformity score and execution-aware variants.** §6.6 establishes the methodology and a small-N first read on the endpoint-vs-path gap; §6.6.1's robustness checks land on a ${\sim}7$–$10$pp residual gap at $\tau = 0.95$. Three workstreams: (a) **Methodology** — refit the conformity score on the running maximum of the residual rather than its endpoint, $\max_{t \in [\text{Fri 16:00, Mon 09:30}]} |P_t - \text{point}| / \text{fri\_close}$. The same Mondrian split-conformal architecture and serving formula apply; bands will be wider but path-calibrated by construction. The methodology is now mechanically validated — a first cut on the n=3,861 CME-projected subset (`reports/tables/v1b_robustness_path_fitted.csv`) widens the served band $\sim 5\%$ at $\tau = 0.95$ ($313.7 \to 328.9$ bps) and $\sim 10\%$ at $\tau = 0.99$ ($637.7 \to 700.2$ bps) while preserving endpoint Kupiec ($p = 0.829 \to 0.419$ at $\tau = 0.95$); the c-bump moves from $1.073$ to $1.022$ as the score base widens. The CME projection is too smooth to deliver a *binding* path-coverage benefit (path coverage was already saturated at $0.99$ on this subset because the band centre tracks the same factor), so the binding empirical question — does path-fitting close the §6.6 perp/on-chain residual gap — still requires the consumer-experienced perp/on-chain sample. (b) **Sample maturation** — block-bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification at conventional power require the perp leg to accumulate ${\sim}50$+ weekends; the on-chain leg should overlap the panel after the next artefact rebuild. (c) **Execution-aware** — beyond path coverage, an MEV-conditional variant requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events. ETA Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. This is the right primitive for band-aware AMMs (Paper 4) where the consumer cannot use the step-up-one-anchor lever.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

**Full-distribution conformal calibration.** §6.3.1 reports Berkowitz on M5's walk-forward PITs at LR $= 173.1$ and DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ at $p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$; the per-anchor-only calibration profile is the deepest open methodological item. The v2 / M5 architecture *partially* realises a conformal upgrade — the per-regime quantile is the standard Mondrian split-conformal under exchangeability within a regime, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is the OOS-fit conservatism. What remains is a *full-distribution* conformal: rather than four discrete anchors each calibrated by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage at every $\tau$ simultaneously. Romano-Patterson-Candès CQR [romano-cqr-2019] is the closest match. Deliverable: a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on v3 walk-forward PITs at the cost of wider per-anchor bands.

**Conditional EVT for the τ = 0.99 tail.** The textbook ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold EVT (Pickands-Balkema-de Haan). An exploratory unconditional GPD trial on v1 (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with a GPD fit on upper-decile exceedances; the per-symbol DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical, identifying the miscalibration mechanism as GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence rather than tail-quantile noise. The technically-correct response is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000].

**Sub-regime granularity (empirically refuted, not on the v3 path).** A 5-cell sub-split using VIX tertiles within `normal` (`reports/tables/v1b_robustness_vol_tertile.csv`) leaves Berkowitz LR essentially unchanged ($173 \to 175$) while widening the $\tau = 0.95$ band by $9\%$ — pure cost. Recorded here so the candidate is not re-suggested.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.7, closes §9.6).** Three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders; (b) a broader Pyth comparator sample across off-hours, pre/post-market, and the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time); (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-26) to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.6. Paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes §9.5).** The Rust + Anchor serving stack is functionally complete and verified, but has not run as a production feed consumed by a real protocol. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode requiring production realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). Output: a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery.

A companion paper (Paper 3) addresses the downstream decision-theoretic question — given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? — under the explicit borrower-book LTV distribution and protocol cost model that §9.7 enumerates. Multi-region replication (LSE, JP equities), additional RWA classes (treasuries, commodities, credit), and the per-symbol sample-size eligibility framework are tracked in `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

## 10.4 Post-M5 diagnostic candidates (roadmap evidence, not adopted)

A four-way bake-off ran after Paper 1's validation loop closed (`reports/v3_bakeoff.md`, `reports/tables/v3_bakeoff_*.csv`). Three candidates were evaluated head-to-head with M5 against the per-symbol Kupiec target the §6.4.1 bimodality flagged. Findings are recorded as roadmap evidence; **none are adopted in this paper**.

- **C1 — Locally-weighted conformal (LWC).** Standardise the conformity score by a per-symbol pre-Friday scale estimator $\hat\sigma_\text{sym}(t)$ (trailing 26-week relative-residual std). All ten symbols pass per-symbol Kupiec at $\tau = 0.95$ on the 2023+ OOS slice (worst $p = 0.168$ on QQQ; M5 has 2/10 passing, worst $p \approx 0$); pooled coverage at $\tau = 0.95$ is matched ($0.9503$). Cost: $+8.5\%$ on mean half-width ($385.3$ vs $354.9$ bps). Cross-sectional $\hat\rho$ unchanged ($0.249$ vs $0.252$). **Lead candidate for v3 per-symbol calibration**, but not adopted in this paper — the bake-off ran outside the validation loop, and a proper adoption requires the rolling-rebuild pipeline of V3.2 plus a paper-grade walk-forward.
- **C2 — Symbol-class Mondrian (M6b2).** Replace the regime axis with a 6-cell symbol-class taxonomy. Already deployed for the Lending profile (M6b2 sidecar). At $\tau = 0.95$: $-14.7\%$ on mean half-width at matched coverage, 8/10 per-symbol Kupiec pass with the two failures (NVDA, SPY) being *too-wide* failures. **Better home is Paper 3** (lending policy consequences of per-class collateral buffers and Kamino/MarginFi reserve-buffer evaluation are the natural application).
- **C4 — Stacked LWC + symbol-class.** Double-counts per-symbol scale (LWC standardises the score; class cells already partition by scale). Pareto-dominated by both C1 and C2; the OOS $c(\tau)$ bump degenerates to $1.000$ at every $\tau$. **Rejected** (`VALIDATION_BACKLOG.md` W9).

The M6a common-mode partial-out track [`M6_REFACTOR.md`] is also a candidate fix for the $\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$ residual but is gated on a Friday-observable $\bar r_w$ forward predictor; the W8 prototype rejected the predictor at OOS $R^2 < 0.4$ on the available feature set (`reports/methodology_history.md` 2026-05-03 W8 entry). Shipping is deferred until either a Sunday-Globex republish architecture or V3.1's on-chain xStock TWAP delivers the Friday-observable signal.

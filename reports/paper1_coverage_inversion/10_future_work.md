# §10 — Future Work

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in three categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), and empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

**On-chain xStock TWAP forecaster.** The most likely reviewer ask. Cong et al. show on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch history, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA Q3–Q4 2026. Deliverable: a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of §6 results admitting it into the candidate pool.

**Forward-tape rolling rebuild.** The deployed M6 artefact is a one-shot fit at the 2023-01-01 split anchor; in production the per-regime quantile and $c(\tau)$ schedule will drift as the calibration target ages. The forward-tape harness (§6.7) is the operational scaffolding for this: a content-addressed frozen artefact is evaluated weekly against newly-arriving weekends, with the σ̂ variant comparison sibling (§7.3.6) re-validating the σ̂ choice on truly held-out data. The rolling-rebuild upgrade adds a quarterly (or trigger-based) re-roll of the freeze: re-run `scripts/build_lwc_artefact.py` against an updated panel, re-freeze, and write a versioned artefact for hot-reload. Trigger: forward-tape harness flags drift, *or* a scheduled cadence aligned with the live-deployment window (§10.3). Paper-side deliverable: a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across $\geq 6$ rebuilds.

**Path-fitted conformity score and execution-aware variants.** §6.6 establishes the methodology and a small-N first read on the endpoint-vs-path gap; the τ=0.95 raw gap is $16.1$pp on the perp-listed subset, decomposing after three robustness checks to a residual ${\sim}9$–$15$pp at $\tau = 0.95$. Three workstreams: (a) **Methodology** — refit the conformity score on the running maximum of the residual rather than its endpoint, $\max_{t \in [\text{Fri 16:00, Mon 09:30}]} |P_t - \text{point}| / (P_{\text{Fri}} \cdot \hat\sigma_s)$. The same Mondrian split-conformal architecture and serving formula apply; bands will be wider but path-calibrated by construction. A first cut on the n=3,853 CME-projected subset (`reports/tables/m6_lwc_robustness_path_fitted.csv`, refit under the deployed EWMA HL=8 σ̂) is mechanically validated; the binding empirical question — does path-fitting close the §6.6 perp/on-chain residual gap — still requires the consumer-experienced perp/on-chain sample. (b) **Sample maturation** — block-bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification at conventional power require the perp leg to accumulate ${\sim}50$+ weekends; the on-chain leg should overlap the panel after the next artefact rebuild. (c) **Execution-aware** — beyond path coverage, an MEV-conditional variant requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events. ETA Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. This is the right primitive for band-aware AMMs (Paper 4) where the consumer cannot use the step-up-one-anchor lever.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

**Full-distribution conformal calibration.** The pooled Berkowitz LR on M6's PITs and the DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.95$ remain the deepest open methodological items. M6 *partially* realises a conformal upgrade — the per-regime quantile is the standard Mondrian split-conformal under exchangeability within a regime, the σ̂ standardisation is per-symbol pre-Friday, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is a near-identity OOS-fit conservatism at three of four anchors. What remains is a *full-distribution* conformal: rather than four discrete anchors each calibrated by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage at every $\tau$ simultaneously. Romano-Patterson-Candès CQR [romano-cqr-2019] is the closest match. Deliverable: a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on next-generation walk-forward PITs at the cost of wider per-anchor bands.

**Cross-sectional common-mode partial-out.** The §6.3.6 / §9.4 residual rejection localises to cross-sectional within-weekend correlation ($\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$) — orthogonal to per-symbol scale (which M6's σ̂ standardisation closes) and to regime granularity (which a vol-tertile sub-split refutes). The candidate fix is a common-mode partial-out: residualise each symbol's relative residual against a Friday-observable cross-sectional factor $\bar r_w$ before computing the conformity score. The W8 prototype rejected the predictor at OOS $R^2 < 0.4$ on the available feature set (`reports/methodology_history.md` 2026-05-03 W8 entry); shipping is deferred until either a Sunday-Globex republish architecture or a future on-chain xStock TWAP feed (§10.1) delivers the Friday-observable signal.

**Conditional EVT for the τ = 0.99 tail.** The textbook ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold EVT (Pickands-Balkema-de Haan). An exploratory unconditional GPD trial on Soothsayer-v0 (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with a GPD fit on upper-decile exceedances; the per-symbol DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical, identifying the miscalibration mechanism as GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence rather than tail-quantile noise. The technically-correct response is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000].

**Sub-regime granularity (empirically refuted, not on the next-generation path).** A 5-cell sub-split using VIX tertiles within `normal` (`reports/tables/m6_lwc_robustness_vol_tertile.csv`) leaves Berkowitz LR essentially unchanged ($170 \to 172$) while widening the $\tau = 0.95$ band by $9\%$ — pure cost. Recorded here so the candidate is not re-suggested.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.5, closes §9.6).** Three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders; (b) a broader Pyth comparator sample across off-hours, pre/post-market, and the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time); (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-26) to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.6. Paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes the residual of §9.5).** The forward-tape harness (§6.7) carries deployed-artefact evaluation against truly held-out weekends — the cleanest possible held-out coverage statement. What it does not do is consume the band as a live integrator-experienced price feed under adversarial conditions. The Rust + Anchor M5 serving stack is functionally complete and verified; the M6 Rust port is gated to a productionisation milestone (§4.7 / §8.5). A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode requiring production realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). Output: a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery.

A companion paper (Paper 3) addresses the downstream decision-theoretic question — given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? — under the explicit borrower-book LTV distribution and protocol cost model that §9.7 enumerates. Multi-region replication (LSE, JP equities), additional RWA classes (treasuries, commodities, credit), and the per-symbol sample-size eligibility framework are tracked in `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

## 10.4 Architectural roadmap items

The §7.3 σ̂ selection procedure considered five variants under a pre-registered three-gate criterion; finer half-life grids, alternative weight kernels, or hybrid rules are open future work, gated on accumulating forward-tape evidence to re-validate any new selection on truly held-out data. The per-symbol-class Mondrian track (a 6-cell symbol-class taxonomy replacing the regime axis; better per-symbol Kupiec on the bake-off sample at $-14.7\%$ width) remains in scope as a Paper 3 lending-policy primitive — its natural home is per-class collateral buffers and Kamino/MarginFi reserve-buffer evaluation, not the calibration-transparency primitive this paper validates. The stacked LWC + symbol-class candidate (W9) was empirically refuted (Pareto-dominated by both individual variants; OOS $c(\tau)$ degenerates to identity).

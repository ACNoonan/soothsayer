# §10 — Future Work

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete follow-ups in three categories: methodology upgrades that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology upgrades that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), and empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology upgrades

**V3.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP).** The most likely reviewer ask. Cong et al. show on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens; our base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch history, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (`docs/v5-tape.md`, continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA Q3–Q4 2026. Deliverable: a third forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of §6 results admitting it into the candidate pool.

**V3.2 — Rolling artefact rebuild.** The deployed M5 artefact is a one-shot fit. A production deployment will see the per-regime quantile and $c(\tau)$ schedule drift as the calibration target ages relative to the live regime. The fix is a monthly cron that re-runs `scripts/build_mondrian_artefact.py` against an updated panel and writes a versioned artefact for hot-reload. Trigger: the live-deployment window (§9.5). Paper-side deliverable: a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across ≥ 6 monthly rebuilds.

**V3.3 — MEV-aware consumer-experienced coverage.** §9.9's open question. Requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events. ETA Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates ≥ 3 months. Deliverable: a §9 disclosure of the gap $\Delta = \text{reported} - \text{consumer-experienced}$ in basis points.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology upgrades

**Full-distribution conformal calibration.** §6.4.1 reports Berkowitz on M5's walk-forward PITs at LR $= 173.1$ and DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ at $p = 5.7 \times 10^{-6}$; the per-anchor-only calibration profile is the deepest open methodological item. The v2 / M5 architecture *partially* realises a conformal upgrade — the per-regime quantile is the standard Mondrian split-conformal under exchangeability within a regime, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is the OOS-fit conservatism. What remains is a *full-distribution* conformal: rather than four discrete anchor τ each calibrated by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage at every τ simultaneously. Romano-Patterson-Candès conformalised quantile regression (2019) is the closest match. Deliverable: a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on v3 walk-forward PITs at the cost of wider per-anchor bands.

**Sub-regime granularity and richer state.** The DQ rejection flags multi-lag conditional structure that the current three-state $\rho$ aggregates. Splitting `normal` into sub-regimes — using either trailing realised-vol tertiles or a rolling regime classifier — should absorb part of the multi-lag autocorrelation. A second candidate mechanism is omitted state: on-chain tokenised-stock prices, weekend futures microstructure, or event-level covariates may carry information the current labeler discards.

**Conditional EVT for the τ = 0.99 tail.** The textbook ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold EVT per Pickands-Balkema-de Haan. An exploratory unconditional GPD trial on v1 (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with a GPD fit on upper-decile exceedances of standardised residuals; the per-symbol DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical, identifying the miscalibration mechanism as GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence rather than tail-quantile noise. The technically-correct response is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000].

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.7, closes §9.6).** The dashboard upgrade has three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders; (b) a broader Pyth comparator sample across off-hours, pre/post-market, and the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time); (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-26) to a paper-grade weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.6. The dashboard becomes paper-grade once each comparator crosses the per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes §9.5).** The Rust + Anchor serving stack is functionally complete and verified, but has not run as a production feed consumed by a real protocol. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode requiring production realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). Output: a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery.

A companion paper (Paper 3) addresses the downstream decision-theoretic question — given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? — under the explicit borrower-book LTV distribution and protocol cost model that §9.7 enumerates. Multi-region replication (LSE, JP equities), additional RWA classes (treasuries, commodities, credit), and the per-symbol sample-size eligibility framework are tracked in `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

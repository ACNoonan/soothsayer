# §10 — Extensions

The limitations of §9 are disclosures, not retractions. This section names concrete extensions that build on the calibration-transparency primitive validated above, in three categories: methodology extensions that wait on data accumulation (§10.1), methodology extensions that wait on engineering effort (§10.2), and empirical-comparator and live-deployment work (§10.3). The full operational roadmap with quarter-grain triggers lives in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and `reports/methodology_history.md`.

## 10.1 Data-gated methodology extensions

**On-chain xStock TWAP forecaster.** Cong et al. show on-chain tokenised-equity prices anticipate Monday opens; the base forecaster does not consume this signal because the Solana tokenised-equity launch (mid-2025) leaves only ~30 weekends of post-launch history, well short of the ~150 weekends needed for stable per-(symbol, regime) Kupiec validation. The V5 forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-24) is the data source. ETA Q3–Q4 2026. Deliverable: an additional candidate forecaster $\texttt{F\_tok}$ and a re-derivation of §6 results admitting it into the candidate pool.

**Forward-tape rolling rebuild.** The deployed artefact is a one-shot fit at the 2023-01-01 split anchor; in production the per-regime quantile and $c(\tau)$ schedule will drift as the calibration target ages. The forward-tape harness (§6.7) is the operational scaffolding for this: a content-addressed frozen artefact is evaluated weekly against newly-arriving weekends, with the σ̂ variant comparison sibling (§7.4.6) re-validating the σ̂ choice on truly held-out data. The rolling-rebuild upgrade adds a quarterly (or trigger-based) re-roll of the freeze: re-run `scripts/build_lwc_artefact.py` against an updated panel, re-freeze, and write a versioned artefact for hot-reload. Trigger: forward-tape harness flags drift, *or* a scheduled cadence aligned with the live-deployment window (§10.3). Deliverable: a §6 schedule-trajectory figure across $\geq 6$ rebuilds.

**Path-fitted conformity score (library-grade, τ-conditional).** §6.6 establishes the path-fitted methodology and a small-$N$ first read on the endpoint-vs-path gap; the $\tau = 0.95$ raw gap is $16.1$ pp on the perp-listed subset, decomposing after three robustness checks to a residual $\sim 9$–$15$ pp. Methodology is library-grade and unit-tested (`soothsayer.backtest.calibration.compute_score_lwc_path`; 7 unit tests in `tests/test_path_fitted_score.py`, all pass). An empirical first-cut on the CME-projected subset ($n = 1{,}557$ OOS rows) confirms mechanical correctness (`reports/tables/m6_lwc_robustness_path_fitted.csv`). **The architectural recommendation is τ-conditional**: at $\tau \ge 0.95$, where path coverage is the operational concern, the cluster-conditional + path-fitted combination is empirically calibrated (DQ $p = 0.912$ at $\tau = 0.95$ on the equity cluster + CME-projected subset) and provides path coverage as an operational primitive; at $\tau < 0.95$, endpoint scoring on the unpartialled residual remains the dominant test (path extrema introduce within-symbol temporal autocorrelation in the violation series that DQ rejects at body-of-distribution $\tau$; `baseline_path` rejects DQ $p = 0.000$ at $\tau \in \{0.68, 0.85\}$ regardless of partial-out — `reports/tables/paper1_f3_cluster_path_fitted.csv`, `..._per_symbol.csv`). The binding empirical question — does path-fitting close the §6.6 perp / on-chain residual gap on consumer-experienced path data — accumulates as the V5 forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-24) crosses $N \ge 300$ path-coverage weekends. Continuous-consumption AMM use cases require this path-fitted variant rather than the endpoint conformity score; that extension is sketched here and not pursued empirically. An MEV-conditional variant requires Solana mempool-or-bundle granularity to reconstruct the price a consumer would have *executed at* near band-edge events; ETA Q3 2026 once Jito bundle data accumulates $\ge 3$ months.

## 10.2 Engineering-gated methodology extensions

**Full-distribution conformal calibration.** The pooled Berkowitz LR on the deployed PITs and the DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.95$ remain the deepest open methodological items. The deployed architecture *partially* realises a conformal upgrade — the per-regime quantile is the standard Mondrian split-conformal under exchangeability within a regime, the σ̂ standardisation is per-symbol pre-Friday, and the $c(\tau)$ bump is a near-identity OOS-fit conservatism at three of four anchors. What remains is a *full-distribution* conformal: rather than four discrete anchors each calibrated by their own $c(\tau)$, fit a single conformal correction over the entire residual CDF that delivers exchangeability-based finite-sample coverage at every $\tau$ simultaneously. Romano-Patterson-Candès CQR [romano-cqr-2019] is the closest match. Deliverable: a §6 / §7 update reporting Berkowitz pass on next-generation walk-forward PITs at the cost of wider per-anchor bands.

**Cluster-conditional + path-fitted architecture (τ-conditional).** The §6.3.6 / §9.4 residual rejection localises to a within-weekend cluster topology — equity (8 symbols, $\hat\rho_\text{within} = 0.477$) vs safe-haven (GLD/TLT, $\hat\rho_\text{within} \approx 0$). A1.5's cluster-internal partial-out within the equity cluster reduces $\hat\rho_\text{within}$ from $0.477$ to $0.077$, preserves per-symbol Kupiec 8/8, and tightens half-width $-11.6\%$ at $\tau = 0.95$ (`reports/tables/paper1_a1_5_cluster_internal_partial_out.csv`). Composed with the path-fitted conformity score (library-grade, unit-tested per `compute_score_lwc_path` — §10.1), the recommendation is **τ-conditional**: at $\tau \ge 0.95$, cluster-conditional + path-fitted is empirically calibrated (DQ $p = 0.912$ at $\tau = 0.95$ on the equity cluster + CME-projected subset) and provides path coverage as an operational primitive; at $\tau < 0.95$, endpoint scoring on the unpartialled residual remains the dominant test (path extrema introduce within-symbol temporal autocorrelation in the violation series that DQ catches at body-of-distribution $\tau$; `baseline_path` rejects DQ $p = 0.000$ at $\tau \in \{0.68, 0.85\}$ even without partial-out — `reports/tables/paper1_f3_cluster_path_fitted.csv`). The architectural recommendation is bounded, not blanket. A Friday-observable cross-sectional factor $\bar r_w$ predictor is a separate avenue (W8 prototype rejected the predictor at OOS $R^2 < 0.4$ on the available feature set; `reports/methodology_history.md` 2026-05-03 W8 entry); shipping is deferred until either a Sunday-Globex republish architecture or a future on-chain xStock TWAP feed (§10.1) delivers the signal. The safe-haven cluster ($n = 2$) does not need partial-out treatment: $\hat\rho_\text{within} \approx 0$ in baseline.

**Conditional EVT for the τ = 0.99 tail.** The textbook ask at $\tau \to 1$ is peaks-over-threshold EVT (Pickands-Balkema-de Haan). An exploratory unconditional GPD trial (`reports/v1b_evt_pot_trial.md`) replaced the empirical-quantile estimator at $\tau \geq 0.95$ with a GPD fit on upper-decile exceedances; the per-symbol DQ rejection at $\tau = 0.99$ was bit-for-bit identical, identifying the miscalibration mechanism as GARCH-style conditional volatility persistence rather than tail-quantile noise. The technically-correct response is the McNeil-Frey conditional EVT framework (GARCH first, GPD on standardised innovations) [mcneil-frey-2000].

**Sub-regime granularity (empirically refuted, not on the next-generation path).** A 5-cell sub-split using VIX tertiles within `normal` (`reports/tables/m6_lwc_robustness_vol_tertile.csv`) leaves Berkowitz LR essentially unchanged ($170 \to 172$) while widening the $\tau = 0.95$ band by $9\%$ — pure cost. Recorded here so the candidate is not re-suggested.

**Block-correlation / two-factor t-copula refinement of §6.3.4.** The slight over-dispersion of the homogeneous t-copula vs empirical (emp/t variance $0.63$ at $\tau = 0.95$) reflects $\hat R$ averaging over heterogeneous correlations — equity-equity correlations are higher than equity-safe-haven correlations (which are negative). A block-correlation or two-factor t-copula would fit better but is a §10 mention only: the cluster topology from §9.4 plus the homogeneous t-copula bracket from §6.3.4 are sufficient for the present paper.

## 10.3 Empirical comparators and live deployment

**Public comparator dashboard (extends §6.5, closes §9.6).** Three components: (a) a multi-year v11 Chainlink reconstruction across all regimes against the corrected v10 + v11 decoders; (b) a broader Pyth comparator sample across off-hours, pre/post-market, and the AAPL/GOOGL/HOOD/NVDA RH feeds (currently 0% available at our query time); (c) accumulation of the RedStone REST forward-cursor tape (continuous capture since 2026-04-26) to a multi-symbol weekend sample, structurally limited to underlier-tickers per §9.6. Strong comparator evidence accumulates as each surface crosses its per-comparator sample-size threshold for weekend-block bootstrap CIs and per-regime stratification.

**Live-deployment OOS validation (closes the residual of §9.5).** The forward-tape harness (§6.7) carries deployed-artefact evaluation against truly held-out weekends — the cleanest possible held-out coverage statement. What it does not do is consume the band as a live integrator-experienced price feed under adversarial conditions. The Rust + Anchor serving stack and the wire-format rollout state are documented in §8. A live window — six months of devnet-then-mainnet operation against a real consumer — would expose any failure mode requiring production realism (rate-limit edges, signer-rotation events, mempool-ordering near band edges). Output: a §6 appendix-style update reporting realised coverage on the live window against the same Kupiec + Christoffersen + DQ + Berkowitz battery.

The downstream decision-theoretic question — given a calibrated band, what should a lending protocol do? — is out of scope here and would need an explicit borrower-book LTV distribution, a protocol-specific cost model, and a declared semantics for the correct action under realised prices (§9.7). Multi-region replication (LSE, JP equities), additional RWA classes (treasuries, commodities, credit), and the per-symbol sample-size eligibility framework are tracked in `docs/ROADMAP.md`.

## 10.4 Architectural extensions

The §7.4 σ̂ selection procedure considered five variants under a pre-registered three-gate criterion; finer half-life grids, alternative weight kernels, or hybrid rules are open extensions, gated on accumulating forward-tape evidence to re-validate any new selection on truly held-out data. The per-symbol-class Mondrian track (a 6-cell symbol-class taxonomy replacing the regime axis; better per-symbol Kupiec on the bake-off sample at $-14.7\%$ width) sits naturally as a per-class collateral-buffer primitive for downstream lending-policy work, not as a calibration-transparency replacement for the architecture validated here. The stacked LWC + symbol-class candidate (W9) was empirically refuted (Pareto-dominated by both individual variants; OOS $c(\tau)$ degenerates to identity).

**Denser anchor grid.** The deployed schedule is calibrated at four anchors $\mathcal{T}_\text{anch} = \{0.68, 0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$; off-anchor $\tau$ is served by linear interpolation of $q_r(\tau)$ and $c(\tau)$ between the bracketing anchors (§4.6, §9.10). The non-monotonic $c(\tau) = \{1.000, 1.000, 1.079, 1.003\}$ schedule produces an interpolated multiplier (e.g. $c(0.90) \approx 1.040$, $c(0.97) \approx 1.041$) at points with no separately audited calibration evidence. Two extensions: (a) extend the calibration grid to e.g. $\mathcal{T}_\text{anch}' = \{0.68, 0.80, 0.85, 0.90, 0.95, 0.97, 0.99\}$ and re-run §6.3 / §6.4 at the additional anchors, lifting the audited surface from four points to seven; (b) replace linear interpolation with monotone-preserving interpolation (e.g. PCHIP) so the served $c$-curve cannot dip below an audited anchor between two higher-valued anchors. Either path closes the §9.10 disclosure.

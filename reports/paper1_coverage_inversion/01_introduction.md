# §1 — Introduction

Tokenised equities and other real-world assets (RWAs) on public blockchains trade 24/7. Their underlying price-discovery venues do not. A US equity listed on NASDAQ is open 6.5 hours per weekday — roughly 32% of wall-clock time. In the other 68% the price continues to move (earnings, macro prints, overseas sessions) but the venues that establish reference prices are closed. The JELLYJELLY incident on Hyperliquid (March 2025) drove ~$12M of HLP losses through a manipulable single-venue oracle path; a Volcano Exchange post-mortem of a tokenised-asset perp episode documents $9.21M in Hyperliquid liquidations exceeding the Binance volume that fed the oracle, and identifies the propagation mechanism in plain language: *uncertainty present in the input was not represented in the output*. The structural fix is symmetric: an oracle whose product contract makes the input uncertainty a first-class output a downstream consumer can read on every block. Any DeFi protocol that accepts tokenised RWAs as collateral or as inputs to an automated market maker must answer at every block: *with the underlying venue closed, what is the fair price — and how uncertain is it?*

The question recurs across the broader RWA wave moving on-chain in 2025–2026: tokenised equities (xStocks on Solana, Backed-issued tokens), treasuries (BlackRock BUIDL, Ondo OUSG; >$5B AUM as of Q1 2026), commodities (Paxos PAXG), and the next wave of credit and FX. Every asset class with a continuous off-hours information set inherits the same closed-market-pricing problem and would benefit from the same calibration-transparent risk-reporting primitive. The methodology fits asset classes with such a signal; it does not fit instruments without one (illiquid commodities, pure NAV real estate). §9.8 enumerates the generalisation map.

## 1.1 How existing oracles answer, and what they leave unanswered

**No incumbent RWA oracle deployed at scale today publishes a calibration receipt verifiable against public data at the aggregate feed level** — a statement of the form *"over $N$ historical prediction windows, our 95% band contained the realised price $N_\text{in}$ times."* Five archetypes are surveyed below; each resolves the *what*, and none publishes the rolling dataset from which an aggregate-level realised-coverage claim could be independently reproduced. Numerical width comparisons against these archetypes are in §6.5 and are necessarily disclaimer-heavy (small samples, consumer-supplied wrap multipliers, large-cap-skewed composition); the categorical contribution — a verifiable per-read calibration receipt — is what the literature does not currently provide.

| Archetype | Representative | What a consumer sees |
|---|---|---|
| Stale-hold | Chainlink Data Streams v10 [chainlink-v10] | Last trade held forward + binary stale/live flag; no `bid`/`ask`/confidence on the wire |
| Synthetic-marker | Chainlink Data Streams v11 [chainlink-v11] | Weekend `bid`/`ask` with a `.01` suffix at 100% incidence on SPYx/QQQx/TSLAx (§6.5.2) — generated bookends, not real quotes |
| Publisher-dispersion metric | Pyth regular [pyth-agg; pyth-conf] | $\sigma$ across permissioned publishers; not an aggregate coverage claim; collapses to near-zero at venue close |
| Executable-overnight | Pyth Pro / Blue Ocean [pyth-pro; blueocean-pyth] | Real 24/5 book Sun–Thu 8 PM – 4 AM ET; does not cover the canonical xStock weekend; paid enterprise |
| Undisclosed methodology | RedStone Live [redstone-live]; v10 `tokenizedPrice` | Continuous closed-market value; methodology described qualitatively only |

Two structural observations. (i) Pyth's documentation [pyth-conf] recommends *individual publishers* calibrate to ~95%, but this is per-publisher self-attestation, not a verifiable property of the aggregate. (ii) Cong et al. [cong-tokenized-2025] document that off-hour tokenised-stock returns anticipate Monday opens, so the on-chain SPL xStock is informationally distinct from its underlier in exactly the closed-market window the oracle covers; feeds pricing the underlier ticker carry an additional instrument-mismatch gap.

## 1.2 Coverage as a first-class API parameter

The design point of this paper is an oracle whose *product contract* is a calibration statement. Instead of publishing a single band and leaving its coverage level implicit, we treat target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ as a published, parametric product input and serve the band that has *empirically* delivered coverage $\tau$ on a rolling historical sample, stratified by asset, by pre-publish regime, and rescaled by a per-symbol pre-Friday scale. The empirical calibration claim is made at four audited anchors $\mathcal{T}_\text{anch} = \{0.68 \approx 1\text{-}\sigma,\,0.85,\,0.95,\,0.99\}$ — the lower anchor is set at the approximate one-standard-deviation point so consumers comparing against a Gaussian default have a familiar reference; the API accepts any $\tau \in [0.68, 0.99]$ and linearly interpolates the per-regime quantile and the $c(\tau)$ bump between anchors, so an off-anchor $\tau$ inherits an *interpolated* (not separately validated) band — see §9.10 for the disclosure and §10.4 for the denser-anchor-grid follow-up. The deployed wire format publishes one band per symbol per Friday with $\tau$ stamped on the receipt.

For a point estimator $\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s)$, a regime classifier $\rho$, a strictly-pre-Friday per-symbol scale $\hat\sigma_s(t) = \mathrm{EWMA}_{\mathrm{HL}=8}\bigl(\{\, |P_{\text{Mon},t'} - \hat P_{\text{Mon},t'}| / P_{\text{Fri},t'} : t' < t \,\}\bigr)$, and a per-regime conformal quantile $q_r(\tau)$ trained on the *standardised* score $|P_{\text{Mon}} - \hat P_{\text{Mon}}| / (P_{\text{Fri}} \cdot \hat\sigma_s)$, the deployed architecture (instantiated in §4) serves at consumer target $\tau$ via

$$\bigl[\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s) - c(\tau) \, q_r(\tau) \, \hat\sigma_s(t) \cdot P_{\text{Fri},t},\;\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s) + c(\tau) \, q_r(\tau) \, \hat\sigma_s(t) \cdot P_{\text{Fri},t}\bigr],$$

where $c(\tau) \in \{1.000,\, 1.000,\, 1.079,\, 1.003\}$ at the four anchors is a near-identity OOS-fit multiplicative bump that closes any residual train-OOS distribution-shift gap. **Sixteen deployment scalars total: 12 trained per-regime quantiles on standardised residuals + 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ + 0 walk-forward shifts** — per-symbol scale standardisation absorbs the cross-split realised-coverage variance that would otherwise call for a structural-conservatism shift. Implicit hyperparameters pinned a priori (EWMA half-life 8 weekends, $\geq 8$-past-observation σ̂ warm-up, three Mondrian regimes, top-quartile VIX threshold, 2023-01-01 split date) sit outside the 16-scalar count, which captures the trainable / tuned budget rather than a complete DOF statement.

Two properties follow that are absent from incumbents: (1) **Auditability** — the 16 scalars and the per-Friday $(\hat P_{\text{Mon},r},\, \hat\sigma_s)$ are deterministic functions of public data; any third party can reconstruct the artefact and verify a served band. (2) **Served-coverage receipts** — every read exposes $(\tau,\, c(\tau),\, q_r(\tau),\, \hat\sigma_s(t),\, r)$ alongside the band, so a protocol integrator can log it, backtest, and challenge the provider if realised rate drifts. The framework traces to Christoffersen [christoffersen-1998] and SR 11-7 [sr11-7] / SR 26-2 [sr26-2] model-risk guidance; what is new is the application to a decentralised oracle with a consumer-facing SLA on realised coverage rather than on point accuracy, and the per-symbol locally-weighted standardisation that closes per-symbol calibration on heterogeneous asset panels.

## 1.3 What this paper shows

We evaluate the deployed locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal architecture (full specification in §4) on 5,996 weekend prediction-window rows (639 weekends × 10 symbols) spanning 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24: seven US equities/ETFs (SPY, QQQ, AAPL, GOOGL, NVDA, TSLA, HOOD), gold (GLD), long treasuries (TLT), and a Bitcoin-proxy equity (MSTR). Each window runs Friday 16:00 ET to Monday 09:30 ET. The contribution is the *primitive* — the artefact, its serving-time lookup, and the audit receipts — together with empirical evidence that the architecture delivers per-anchor *and* per-symbol calibration on a decade-scale public-data panel.

Headline empirical claims:

- **Held-out coverage at $\tau = 0.95$ via 10-fold leave-one-symbol-out CV: $0.9497 \pm 0.0128$.** Each held-out symbol's $c(\tau)$ is fit on the other nine symbols' OOS slice and never sees the held-out symbol's data, so the held-out realised coverage is genuinely out-of-fit. All 10 LOSO bands pass Kupiec at $\tau = 0.95$. This is the paper's leading calibration figure. Pooled realised coverage on the deployment-tuned 2023+ slice (1,730 rows, 173 weekends) is $0.950$ at mean half-width $370.6$ bps, consistent with the deployment-tuned target; the agreement at $0.950$ is a *consequence* of fitting $c(0.95) = 1.079$ on this same OOS slice (so the pooled Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$ follows by construction and is *not* held-out evidence — only $c(0.95)$ carries meaningful OOS information; the other three $c(\tau)$ are essentially identity, and the 12 trained per-regime quantiles are frozen on 4,186 pre-2023 rows). **Three orthogonal stability checks** complement LOSO on the deployed-fit slice (each refits the schedule within its slice, so they are stability-of-fit reads rather than held-out validation): (i) all 10 symbols pass per-symbol Kupiec with violation rates in $[3.5\%, 6.9\%]$; (ii) four-anchor split-date sensitivity refits $c(\tau)$ at each split anchor and holds realised $\tau = 0.95$ coverage within $\{0.9539, 0.9520, 0.9503, 0.9504\}$ across {2021, 2022, 2023, 2024}, with Christoffersen passing every cell at $\alpha = 0.05$; (iii) a six-split walk-forward selects $\delta(\tau) \equiv 0$ with worst-split coverage $\geq \tau$ at every powered split. Christoffersen lag-1 $p_{ind} = 0.603$ on the deployed split is reported in §6.3.1 with the $\sim 86$-violation power caveat; the §6.3.6 DQ is the more sensitive test on the residual and rejects pooled — localising to the cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode. We use $\tau = 0.95$ as the paper's primary oracle-validation target. Mapping the served band to a particular operating $\tau$ for a given lending protocol is downstream of this work and not addressed here.

- **Calendar sub-period stability.** Across {2023, 2024, 2025, 2026-YTD} the realised $\tau = 0.95$ coverage stays in $[0.942,\,0.967]$ with Kupiec $p \ge 0.054$ in every cell; the architecture carries 2 rejections across the 16-cell sub-period × $\tau$ grid, both *over*-coverage in 2025 (not the failure mode that warrants a deployment alarm). Christoffersen lag-1 independence is not rejected in any sub-period × $\tau$ cell.

- **Per-symbol calibration vs parametric baselines.** Across the 40-cell (symbol × $\tau$) grid: this paper's architecture **40/40**, GARCH-$t$ 31/40, GARCH-Gaussian 23/40, constant-buffer baseline 12/40. At matched 95% realised coverage GARCH-$t$ widens to $\approx 378$ bps (statistically tied with this paper's $370.6$) but undercovers Kupiec at every $\tau < 0.99$; GARCH-Gaussian undercovers at every $\tau < 0.99$ and over-covers at $\tau = 0.99$. The GARCH-$t$ baseline carries an internal disclosure: NVDA's t-MLE pushes $\hat\nu \to 2.50$ (variance-undefined boundary) and the runner falls back to Gaussian, so 4 of the 31 GARCH-$t$ pass cells use the Gaussian-fallback baseline; the §6.4.2 / §6.4.3 tables flag this inline.

- **Joint-tail empirical distribution for portfolio consumers.** At $\tau = 0.95$ on a 10-symbol panel, empirical $P(k_w \ge 3) = 4.62\%$ (95% bootstrap CI $[1.73,\,7.51]$) versus the binomial-independence reference of $1.15\%$. The worst-observed weekend — Friday 2024-08-02 → Monday 2024-08-05, the BoJ yen-carry-unwind — sat at $k_w = 10$ across all 10 symbols at $\tau = 0.85$ (MSTR $-27.4\%$, HOOD $-17.8\%$, NVDA $-14.2\%$). Reserve-guidance threshold $k^* = 3$ at $\tau = 0.95$ carries $0/24$ sub-period × threshold-convention stability rejections.

- **Architectural claim corroborated under simulation.** Under four DGPs spanning homoskedastic, regime-switching, drift, and structural-break specifications (10 synthetic symbols × 600 weekends × 100 reps, seed = 0), the deployed architecture's per-symbol Kupiec pass-rate at $\tau = 0.95$ is $98.6$–$99.9\%$ while the unweighted-Mondrian comparator's collapses to $29$–$31\%$ across the same DGPs — reproducing the per-symbol bimodality of an unweighted Mondrian fit on synthetic data with known ground truth and confirming that per-symbol $\hat\sigma_s$ standardisation closes it. The simulation evidence is corroborative: it post-dates the per-symbol bimodality observation on real unweighted-Mondrian data (§6.4.2) but pre-dates the empirical fix on real data, and it adds a known-DGP control where the failure mechanism can be isolated. A panel-admission threshold of $N \ge 200$ weekends is established for newly-listed symbols under the production threshold.

- **Forward-tape harness operational; held-out evidence accumulating.** A content-addressed frozen artefact (`lwc_artefact_v1_frozen_20260504.json`, freeze date 2026-05-04) is evaluated weekly via a launchd-driven harness against forward weekends as they accumulate; a sibling variant-comparison runner carries held-out re-validation of the $\hat\sigma_s$ choice without re-selecting on forward data. **At submission $N = 1$ weekend** (`reports/m6_forward_tape_1weekends.md`, 2026-05-01) — preliminary by the harness's own threshold ($N \ge 4$ to clear the preliminary banner; $N \ge 13$ for moderate forward-Christoffersen power). The held-out backbone for the present submission is the §6.3.3 split-date sensitivity, leave-one-symbol-out CV, and calendar sub-period stability, not the forward-tape harness.

- **Diagnostics define the next-generation roadmap.** Two residuals carry forward to §10. (i) Berkowitz rejects pooled — bands are *per-anchor calibrated*, not full-distribution calibrated — and the rejection localises to *cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode* ($\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$, orthogonal to $\hat\sigma_s$); points at full-distribution conformal upgrades and a Friday-observable common-mode partial-out (§10.2). (ii) On a 24/7 stock-perp reference ($n = 118$ symbol-weekends, §6.6) the architecture's endpoint-vs-path gap is $+16.1$ pp at $\tau = 0.95$ — an honestly-calibrated endpoint band exhibits a structural shortfall on a sample whose intra-weekend variance is larger than its endpoint variance; the path-fitted conformity score is the methodology fix (§10.1).

These claims are about the oracle interface and serving-time calibration. We do not address the downstream decision-theoretic question — which $\tau$ a particular lending protocol should consume — in this paper.

## 1.4 Contributions and structure

**C1.** Coverage inversion as an oracle primitive (§3). **C2.** A 12-year locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime calibration on public data, with a 16-scalar artefact rebuildable by any third party (§4, §5). **C3.** Held-out-quantile, deployment-calibrated Kupiec + Christoffersen validation in the form required by institutional model-risk management (§6), per-symbol Kupiec across 40 (symbol × $\tau$) cells against parametric baselines, calendar sub-period stability, a four-DGP simulation study confirming the per-symbol fix is structural, and a forward-tape harness against a content-addressed frozen artefact. **C4.** Joint-tail empirical characterisation of portfolio breaches (§6.3.4–§6.3.5) — overdispersion measurement ($\mathrm{Var}(k_w)/\mathrm{Var}_\text{binom} = 2.32$ at $\tau = 0.95$, where the binomial-independence reference is $\mathrm{Binom}(10, 0.05)$), the $k_w$ reserve-guidance threshold and its sub-period stability (0/24 cells reject), and the worst-weekend vignette (2024-08-05 BoJ unwind, $k_w = 10$) — operationalising the per-anchor-vs-full-distribution residual into a measurable consumer-facing handle no incumbent oracle publishes. **C5.** A three-layer ablation with bootstrap CIs (§7) isolating per-regime conformal as the legacy-narrowing component, per-symbol $\hat\sigma_s$ standardisation as the per-symbol-bimodality fix, and the $\hat\sigma_s$ selection procedure under multi-test correction.

We do not claim point-accuracy or absolute bandwidth dominance over a hand-tuned consumer-supplied wrap of an existing publisher-dispersion feed (§6.5 reports width parity at matched coverage against a Pyth $\pm 50 \cdot \mathrm{conf}$ wrap on the available subset); the contribution is the calibration receipt and its per-symbol generalisation, not the band width.

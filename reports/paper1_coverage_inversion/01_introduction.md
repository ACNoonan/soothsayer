# §1 — Introduction

Tokenised equities and other real-world assets (RWAs) on public blockchains trade 24/7. Their underlying price-discovery venues do not. A US equity listed on NASDAQ is open 6.5 hours per weekday — roughly 32% of wall-clock time. In the other 68% the price continues to move (earnings, macro prints, overseas sessions) but the venues that establish reference prices are closed. The JELLYJELLY incident on Hyperliquid (March 2025) drove ~$12M of HLP losses through a manipulable single-venue oracle path; a Volcano Exchange post-mortem of a tokenized-asset perp episode documents $9.21M in Hyperliquid liquidations exceeding the Binance volume that fed the oracle, and identifies the propagation mechanism as "uncertainty present in the input, not represented in the output." Any DeFi protocol that accepts tokenised RWAs as collateral or as inputs to an automated market maker must answer at every block: *with the underlying venue closed, what is the fair price — and how uncertain is it?*

The question recurs across the broader RWA wave moving on-chain in 2025–2026: tokenised equities (xStocks on Solana, Backed-issued tokens), treasuries (BlackRock BUIDL, Ondo OUSG; >$5B AUM), commodities (Paxos PAXG), and the next wave of credit and FX. Every asset class with a continuous off-hours information set inherits the same closed-market-pricing problem and would benefit from the same calibration-transparent risk-reporting primitive. The methodology fits asset classes with such a signal; it does not fit instruments without one (illiquid commodities, pure NAV real estate). §9.8 enumerates the generalisation map.

## 1.1 How existing oracles answer, and what they leave unanswered

Five classes of RWA oracle are deployed at scale today. Each resolves the *what*; none publishes a *calibration claim verifiable against public data at the aggregate feed level* — a statement of the form *"over $N$ historical prediction windows, our 95% band contained the realised price $N_\text{in}$ times."*

| Archetype | Representative | What a consumer sees |
|---|---|---|
| Stale-hold | Chainlink Data Streams v10 [chainlink-v10] | Last trade held forward + binary stale/live flag; no `bid`/`ask`/confidence on the wire |
| Synthetic-marker | Chainlink Data Streams v11 [chainlink-v11] | Weekend `bid`/`ask` with a `.01` suffix at 100% incidence on SPYx/QQQx/TSLAx (§6.5.2) — generated bookends, not real quotes |
| Publisher-dispersion metric | Pyth regular [pyth-agg; pyth-conf] | $\sigma$ across permissioned publishers; not an aggregate coverage claim; collapses to near-zero at venue close |
| Executable-overnight | Pyth Pro / Blue Ocean [pyth-pro; blueocean-pyth] | Real 24/5 book Sun–Thu 8 PM – 4 AM ET; does not cover the canonical xStock weekend; paid enterprise |
| Undisclosed methodology | RedStone Live [redstone-live]; v10 `tokenizedPrice` | Continuous closed-market value; methodology described qualitatively only |

Two structural observations. (i) Pyth's documentation [pyth-conf] recommends *individual publishers* calibrate to ~95%, but this is per-publisher self-attestation, not a verifiable property of the aggregate. (ii) Cong et al. [cong-tokenized-2025] document that off-hour tokenised-stock returns anticipate Monday opens, so the on-chain SPL xStock is informationally distinct from its underlier in exactly the closed-market window the oracle covers; feeds pricing the underlier ticker carry an additional instrument-mismatch gap. None publishes the rolling dataset from which an aggregate-level calibration claim could be independently reproduced.

## 1.2 Coverage as a first-class API parameter

The design point of this paper is an oracle whose *product contract* is a calibration statement. Instead of publishing a single band and leaving its coverage level implicit, we treat target coverage $\tau \in (0,1)$ as a published, parametric product input and serve the band that has *empirically* delivered coverage $\tau$ on a rolling historical sample, stratified by asset, by pre-publish regime, and rescaled by a per-symbol pre-Friday scale. The methodology is parametric in $\tau$ across the served range $\tau \in [0.68, 0.99]$; the deployed wire format publishes one such band per symbol per Friday, with $\tau$ stamped on the receipt.

For a point estimator $\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s)$, a regime classifier $\rho$, a strictly-pre-Friday per-symbol scale $\hat\sigma_s(t) = \mathrm{EWMA}_{\mathrm{HL}=8}\bigl(\{\, |P_{\text{Mon},t'} - \hat P_{\text{Mon},t'}| / P_{\text{Fri},t'} : t' < t \,\}\bigr)$, and a per-regime conformal quantile $q_r^{\text{LWC}}(\tau)$ trained on the *standardised* score $|P_{\text{Mon}} - \hat P_{\text{Mon}}| / (P_{\text{Fri}} \cdot \hat\sigma_s)$, the deployed architecture (denoted **v2** throughout, instantiated in §4 and reached at the bottom of §7.2's ablation ladder) serves at consumer target $\tau$ via

$$\bigl[\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s) \cdot \bigl(1 - c(\tau) \, q_r^{\text{LWC}}(\tau) \, \hat\sigma_s(t)\bigr),\;\hat P_{\text{Mon},r}(s) \cdot \bigl(1 + c(\tau) \, q_r^{\text{LWC}}(\tau) \, \hat\sigma_s(t)\bigr)\bigr],$$

where $c(\tau) \in \{1.000,\, 1.000,\, 1.079,\, 1.003\}$ at $\tau \in \{0.68, 0.85, 0.95, 0.99\}$ is a near-identity OOS-fit multiplicative bump that closes any residual train-OOS distribution-shift gap. **Sixteen deployment scalars total: 12 trained per-regime quantiles on standardised residuals + 4 OOS-fit $c(\tau)$ + 0 walk-forward shifts** — the v1 walk-forward $\delta(\tau)$ schedule collapses to zero under per-symbol σ̂ standardisation.

Two properties follow that are absent from incumbents: (1) **Auditability** — the 16 scalars and the per-Friday $(\hat P_{\text{Mon},r},\, \hat\sigma_s)$ are deterministic functions of public data; any third party can reconstruct the artefact and verify a served band. (2) **Served-coverage receipts** — every read exposes $(\tau,\, c(\tau),\, q_r^{\text{LWC}}(\tau),\, \hat\sigma_s(t),\, r)$ alongside the band, so a protocol integrator can log it, backtest, and challenge the provider if realised rate drifts. The framework traces to Christoffersen [christoffersen-1998] and SR 11-7 [sr11-7] / SR 26-2 [sr26-2] model-risk guidance; what is new is the application to a decentralised oracle with a consumer-facing SLA on realised coverage rather than on point accuracy, and the per-symbol locally-weighted standardisation that closes the per-symbol bimodality of the simpler Mondrian-without-σ̂ deployment.

## 1.3 What this paper shows

We evaluate on 5,996 weekend prediction-window rows (639 weekends × 10 symbols) spanning 2014-01-17 through 2026-04-24: seven US equities/ETFs (SPY, QQQ, AAPL, GOOGL, NVDA, TSLA, HOOD), gold (GLD), long treasuries (TLT), and a Bitcoin-proxy equity (MSTR). Each window runs Friday 16:00 ET to Monday 09:30 ET. The contribution is the *primitive* surrounding any point estimator — the artefact, its serving-time lookup, and the audit receipts — together with empirical evidence that the locally-weighted variant of the primitive delivers per-anchor *and* per-symbol calibration.

Headline empirical claims:

- **At $\tau = 0.95$ on the 2023+ slice (1,730 rows, 173 weekends):** realised $0.950$, Kupiec $p_{uc} = 0.956$, Christoffersen $p_{ind} = 0.603$, mean half-width $370.6$ bps. **All 10 symbols pass per-symbol Kupiec** (vs 2/10 under the predecessor v1 Mondrian-without-σ̂ architecture); the per-symbol Berkowitz LR range collapses from $0.9$–$224$ to $3.2$–$16.7$. HOOD specifically: violation rate $13.9\% \to 4.0\%$, Kupiec $p\;0.000 \to 0.329$. The 12 trained per-regime quantiles are held-out (frozen on 4,186 pre-2023 rows after the 80-row σ̂ warm-up drop); the four $c(\tau)$ scalars are deployment-tuned on the same OOS slice with three of four near-identity (only $c(0.95) = 1.079$ carries meaningful OOS information). A six-split walk-forward, a four-anchor split-date sensitivity (with Christoffersen passing every (split × $\tau$) cell at $\alpha = 0.05$), and a 10-fold leave-one-symbol-out CV (10/10 LOSO bands pass Kupiec; std $5.7\times$ tighter than v1) all ratify the deployed schedule. We use $\tau = 0.95$ as the paper's primary *oracle-validation* target; the deployed default for protocol consumption is $\tau = 0.85$, picked on protocol-EL grounds (Paper 3).

- **At $\tau = 0.99$:** realised $0.990$, $p_{uc} = 0.942$, mean half-width $635.0$ bps — closing Soothsayer-v0's tail ceiling at $0.972$ that v1 also closed.

- **Per-symbol fix predicted in advance under simulation.** Under four DGPs spanning homoskedastic, regime-switching, drift, and structural-break specifications (10 synthetic symbols × 600 weekends × 100 reps, seed=0), v1's per-symbol Kupiec pass-rate at $\tau = 0.95$ is $29$–$31\%$ across DGPs while v2's is $97.6$–$99.6\%$. The bimodality is structural (predicted by the architecture), not noise. A panel-admission threshold of $N \geq 200$ weekends is established for newly-listed symbols under the production threshold.

- **Forward-tape held-out evidence accumulating weekly.** A content-addressed frozen artefact (`lwc_artefact_v1_frozen_20260504.json`, freeze date 2026-05-04) is evaluated weekly via a launchd-driven harness against forward weekends as they accumulate; the σ̂ variant comparison sibling carries held-out re-validation of the σ̂ choice without re-selecting on forward data. **At submission $N = $ [N TBD; first non-stub report 2026-05-12].**

- **Ablation isolates four load-bearing components.** §7.1 rules out a deployable simpler baseline (constant-buffer fails Kupiec by 5–14pp on OOS); §7.2 isolates the Mondrian per-regime conformal lookup as the legacy-narrowing component (v1 over Soothsayer-v0: 19–20% narrower at $\tau \le 0.95$); §7.2.4 isolates per-symbol σ̂ standardisation as the per-symbol-bimodality fix (v1 → v2: per-symbol Kupiec 2/10 → 10/10 at $+4.5\%$ pooled width tax); §7.3 documents the σ̂ selection procedure with multi-test correction (BH at FDR=0.05 across an 80-cell grid; deployment decision rests on the bootstrap CI on width plus held-out forward-tape re-validation).

- **Diagnostics define the next-generation roadmap.** Berkowitz rejects pooled (LR materially smaller than v1's $173.1$ but non-zero); DQ at $\tau = 0.95$ rejects pooled. The residual localises to *cross-sectional within-weekend common-mode* ($\hat\rho_\text{cross} = 0.354$, orthogonal to σ̂); points at full-distribution conformal upgrades and a Friday-observable common-mode partial-out (§10).

These claims are about the oracle interface and serving-time calibration; they do not prove the same $\tau$ is the welfare-optimal liquidation rule (Paper 3).

## 1.4 Contributions and structure

**C1.** Coverage inversion as an oracle primitive (§3). **C2.** A 12-year locally-weighted Mondrian split-conformal-by-regime calibration on public data, with a 16-scalar artefact rebuildable by any third party (§4, §5). **C3.** Held-out-quantile, deployment-calibrated Kupiec + Christoffersen validation in the form required by institutional model-risk management (§6), plus per-symbol Kupiec + Berkowitz LR collapse, a four-DGP simulation study confirming the per-symbol fix is structural, and a forward-tape harness against a content-addressed frozen artefact. **C4.** A three-layer ablation with bootstrap CIs (§7) isolating per-regime conformal as the v0-narrowing component, per-symbol σ̂ standardisation as the per-symbol-bimodality fix, and the σ̂ selection procedure under multi-test correction.

We do not claim point-accuracy or absolute bandwidth dominance over a hand-tuned consumer-supplied wrap of an existing publisher-dispersion feed (§6.5 reports width parity at matched coverage against a Pyth $\pm 50 \cdot \mathrm{conf}$ wrap on the available subset); the contribution is the calibration receipt and its per-symbol generalisation, not the band width.

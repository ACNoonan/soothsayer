## 1. Setup

### 1.1 Borrower archetype

A **near-origination borrower** is one whose LTV at obligation-open equals the reserve's `loanToValuePct` exactly (the maximum a new borrow can be sized at). This is the relevant stress-test archetype because:

- it bounds the worst-case for any new origination on the reserve;
- it's where the buffer between LTV-at-origination and `liquidationThresholdPct` is smallest;
- soothsayer's band is a forward-looking input — the policy question is whether the band gets the borrower flagged before the realized move triggers liquidation.

A **near-liquidation borrower** (LTV = `liquidationThresholdPct` − 0.05pp) is the second archetype, used to measure how aggressively each policy fires on borrowers already under stress.

### 1.2 Decision space

The shared action ladder for all policies is `Safe / Caution / Liquidate`, defined relative to the reserve's `liquidationThresholdPct`:

- **Safe** — effective LTV (with the policy's collateral-price haircut applied) ≤ `liquidationThresholdPct − pad`
- **Caution** — `liquidationThresholdPct − pad` < effective LTV ≤ `liquidationThresholdPct`
- **Liquidate** — effective LTV > `liquidationThresholdPct`

`pad` is a policy-set warning band (we use 1pp for the scoring tables below; sensitivity is queued in §5). Kamino's actual on-chain ladder has only a binary trigger (§3 of `protocol_semantics_kamino_xstocks.md`) — the `Caution` band is a *risk-display* layer, not a Kamino-program-level state.

### 1.3 Policy haircuts

For each policy, the *effective collateral price* used in the LTV calc is:

- **Kamino-incumbent**: `P_oracle` — the Scope-served spot price, post-validity-gates (300s staleness, 5% TWAP divergence, PriceHeuristic guard rail). When the gates fail, the read returns no price; we report "Block" as the protocol's effective decision in that case.
- **Soothsayer τ-band**: `P_lower(τ)` — the band's lower bound at the chosen coverage level. τ=0.85 is the deployment default; τ=0.95 is the stricter-validation comparator.
- **Pyth-conf-haircut** (the cleanest non-Kamino comparator): `P_pyth − k·conf` for k=1; this is structurally what MarginFi-v2 banks already do (`assets use P − conf`; see `docs/sources/lending/marginfi.md` §1.1).

### 1.4 Protocol-loss components (per liquidation)

Per `protocol_semantics_kamino_xstocks.md` §4, a liquidation pays the liquidator `D_repaid × bonus_pct × 0.50` and the protocol `D_repaid × bonus_pct × 0.50`. From the *protocol's* perspective:

```
loss_unnecessary_liquidation  ≈ D_repaid × bonus_pct × 0.50      (lost to liquidator unjustly)
loss_missed_liquidation       ≈ bad_debt_residual                (full bad-debt absorption)
loss_unnecessary_caution      ≈ borrower_revenue_at_risk         (utilization / borrower trust)
```

The asymmetry that drives the policy choice: `bad_debt_residual` can be many multiples of `D_repaid × 5%` (especially during weekend gaps that exceed the buffer), so the cost function is **strictly convex in tail risk**. Soothsayer's served band targeting that convexity is the Paper 3 thesis.

## 2. Buffer geometry — the headline finding

For a near-origination borrower, the **adverse move that triggers liquidation** is:

```
P_critical / P_origination = loanToValuePct / liquidationThresholdPct
move_to_liquidation       = 1 − P_critical / P_origination
```

Per the snapshot 2026-04-27 (`data/processed/kamino_xstocks_snapshot_20260427.json`):

| Symbol | LTV / liq | Gap (pp) | P_crit / P_orig | **Adverse move to liquidation** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **SPYx** | 73 / 75 | 2 | 0.9733 | **−2.67%** |
| **QQQx** | 70 / 72 | 2 | 0.9722 | **−2.78%** |
| TSLAx | 55 / 65 | 10 | 0.8462 | −15.38% |
| GOOGLx | 60 / 70 | 10 | 0.8571 | −14.29% |
| NVDAx | 55 / 65 | 10 | 0.8462 | −15.38% |
| AAPLx | 40 / 50 | 10 | 0.8000 | −20.00% |
| MSTRx | 30 / 40 | 10 | 0.7500 | −25.00% |
| HOODx | 30 / 40 | 10 | 0.7500 | −25.00% |

**Two findings worth surfacing:**

1. **SPYx and QQQx have a ~2.7% near-origination buffer.** A weekend gap of 2.7% is well within the 95th percentile of historical SPY / QQQ Friday-to-Monday returns (per soothsayer's panel). The 2pp LTV-gap framing in `kamino_xstocks_weekend_*` reports understates the geometry — the *percentage move* matters, and a 2pp LTV gap on a 73%-LTV reserve is a 2.7% price move, not a 2% one.

2. **The other 6 reserves have 14–25% buffers**, dominantly because their absolute LTV is lower (30–60%). MSTRx and HOODx at 30/40 require a 25% adverse move — far beyond any historically-observed weekend gap on those underliers. Their borrow limits are 0 (deposit-only collateral), so the at-risk surface is smaller than the headline buffer suggests.

**This is the load-bearing geometric finding.** SPYx and QQQx are the reserves where soothsayer's served band has the most policy-relevant headroom to add value; the other 6 have buffers that absorb most realistic weekend moves under any reasonable policy.

## 3. Decision-flip arithmetic — worked example (SPYx, weekend 2026-04-24)

Using the panel in [`reports/kamino_xstocks_weekend_20260424.md`](../kamino_xstocks_weekend_20260424.md):

- Friday close `P_fri = $713.94`
- Monday open `P_mon = $713.17` (realized −10.8 bps)
- Soothsayer τ=0.85 half-width: 131.2 bps → `P_lower = $713.94 × (1 − 0.01312) = $704.57`
- Soothsayer τ=0.95 half-width: 207.1 bps → `P_lower = $713.94 × (1 − 0.02071) = $699.16`
- Pyth-regular publishes the Friday-close conf (~0 bps weekend; see `docs/sources/oracles/pyth_regular.md` §3); MarginFi-style `P − conf` ≈ `P_fri`.

A near-origination borrower opens with 1 SPYx collateral at LTV=73%, so `debt_USDC = 521.18`.

| Policy | Read price | Effective LTV | Decision (pad=1pp) |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Kamino-incumbent | $713.94 (Scope stale-held) | 73.00% | **Safe** |
| Soothsayer τ=0.85 | $704.57 | 73.97% | **Caution** |
| Soothsayer τ=0.95 | $699.16 | 74.54% | **Caution** |
| Pyth-conf-haircut (k=1) | ≈ $713.94 | 73.00% | **Safe** |

On this specific weekend with a small realized move (−10.8 bps), soothsayer flips the borrower to Caution while Kamino-incumbent stays Safe; neither liquidates. **Outcome:** soothsayer's *unnecessary-caution cost* applies (borrower flagged but not actually breaching the threshold post-realization), while Kamino's missed-liquidation cost is zero (no breach occurred).

This is one of three regimes — and Paper 3's empirical core is the cross-weekend distribution of these outcomes:

- **Soothsayer = Caution, Kamino = Safe, no breach.** Soothsayer pays unnecessary-caution; Kamino is correct ex-post.
- **Soothsayer = Liquidate, Kamino = Safe, large breach.** Soothsayer fires early and saves the buffer; Kamino accumulates bad-debt residual. *This is the case soothsayer wins on.*
- **Soothsayer = Safe, Kamino = Block (gates failed), large breach.** Both miss the liquidation; the protocol takes the residual either way. Soothsayer's band did not help.

The roadmap-required scoring is: **fraction of weekends in each regime**, weighted by the protocol-loss components in §1.4.

## 4. Per-reserve flip thresholds (band half-width required to change Kamino's decision)

For a near-origination borrower at Friday close, the soothsayer band half-width that flips Kamino's `Safe` → soothsayer's `Liquidate` (i.e., `P_lower < P_crit`) is:

```
hw_flip_pct = 1 − P_crit / P_origination = 1 − loanToValuePct / liquidationThresholdPct
```

| Symbol | hw_flip_pct (= adverse-move buffer) | Soothsayer τ=0.85 typical hw | Soothsayer τ=0.95 typical hw | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| SPYx | 2.67% | ~1.31% | ~2.07% | **τ=0.95 is close but does not flip; τ=0.85 stays Safe** |
| QQQx | 2.78% | ~1.38% | ~2.78% | **τ=0.95 lands at the flip exactly** — boundary case |
| TSLAx | 15.38% | ~4.81% | ~10.16% | both well inside, no flip |
| GOOGLx | 14.29% | ~1.78% | ~2.64% | both well inside |
| NVDAx | 15.38% | ~3.38% | ~8.24% | both well inside |
| AAPLx | 20.00% | ~1.57% | ~3.26% | both well inside |
| MSTRx | 25.00% | ~5.53% | ~13.49% | both inside |
| HOODx | 25.00% | ~11.55% | ~14.94% | both inside |

(Typical half-widths from `reports/kamino_xstocks_weekend_20260424.md` §2; per-weekend variability not aggregated here — each weekend is a fresh draw.)

**Interpretation.** Soothsayer's band at τ=0.95 lands at QQQx's flip threshold exactly and gets close on SPYx. **For the other 6 reserves, soothsayer's band lower bound at any reasonable τ does *not* mechanically force a Liquidate decision when Kamino reads Safe** — the buffers are too wide. The policy value-add elsewhere comes from the *Caution* zone (early warning), not from the Liquidate flip.

This is a cleaner statement than the plan's §3 framing. Paper 3's policy claim should be:

- On **SPYx and QQQx**: soothsayer's stricter band can pre-empt liquidations on weekends with adverse moves >2.7%, where Kamino's Friday-close-stale-held Scope read keeps the borrower in Safe.
- On **the other 6 reserves**: soothsayer adds Caution-zone information without typically forcing the Liquidate flip; the value is in operational risk-display, not on-chain action change.

## 5. Protocol-loss expected-value framework

For a single reserve, the protocol's expected loss across the policy space is:

```
E[L | π] = Σ_{outcomes} P(outcome) · L(outcome | π)
```

Outcome family (matching §3's three regimes):

| Outcome | P(.) input | Loss formula |
|---|---|---|
| `correct_safe`            | F(realized_move > -hw_flip_pct) | 0 (baseline) |
| `correct_liquidate`       | F(realized_move ≤ -hw_flip_pct, Kamino-fires) | `D_repaid × bonus_pct × 0.50` (protocol bonus, net) |
| `unnecessary_liquidation` | F(policy fires, no breach) | `D_repaid × bonus_pct × 0.50` paid to liquidator + borrower-utility cost |
| `missed_liquidation`      | F(breach, policy = Safe) | `bad_debt_residual` (potentially large) |
| `unnecessary_caution`     | F(policy = Caution, no breach) | `borrower_revenue_at_risk` (small, recurring) |
| `block_state`             | F(price gates fail under stress) | `bad_debt_residual` (Kamino-incumbent only) |

Inputs to `F(.)`:

- The realized-move distribution per (reserve, regime) — soothsayer has these from the calibration panel.
- `D_repaid` — set by the borrower distribution at the reserve. Without `kamino/liquidations/v1` we estimate `D_repaid` from the deposit-cap × current-utilization snapshot (see [`docs/protocol_semantics_kamino_xstocks.md`](../../docs/protocol_semantics_kamino_xstocks.md) §2).
- `bonus_pct` — the dynamic curve in `[5%, 10%]` (§6 #1 in `protocol_semantics_kamino_xstocks.md`); for first-cut analysis use `bonus_pct = 0.075` (midpoint), with sensitivity over `{0.05, 0.075, 0.10}`.
- `bad_debt_residual` — depends on tail of realized-move distribution beyond the trigger; soothsayer has the panel to estimate this with bootstrap CIs.
- `borrower_revenue_at_risk` — set by reserve interest rates (`borrowRateCurve` in IDL); secondary, parameterized.

The Paper 3 §7 walk-forward evaluation runs this `E[L | π]` across the policy family `{Kamino-incumbent, Soothsayer τ ∈ [0.68, 0.95], Pyth-conf-haircut, EWMA-haircut, stale-hold-Gaussian}` for each reserve and reports:

- per-reserve `E[L]` ranking with weekend-block-bootstrap CIs;
- ranking stability (probability the soothsayer policy is in the top-2 under bootstrap resampling);
- the **stable region** in τ where soothsayer dominates Kamino-incumbent — this is the publishable governance-output object per `plan.md` C3.

## 7. Provisional Paper 3 framing implications

What this analysis sharpens for the §15 abstract / introduction:

1. **The headline number is reserve-specific, not protocol-specific.** SPYx (2.67%) and QQQx (2.78%) are the reserves where soothsayer's band has policy-decision-flipping headroom. Generic "Kamino-vs-soothsayer" framing without per-reserve geometry obscures this.
2. **The buffer's mechanical relation to the LTV ratio means MSTRx/HOODx (with 25% buffers) are not where soothsayer's band wins on liquidation timing.** The deployment story for those reserves is *Caution-zone operational signal*, not a different on-chain decision.
3. **The "block-state" outcome is unique to the Kamino-incumbent path** because soothsayer's band doesn't have validity gates that fail; this is a distinct mode of protocol loss the served-band primitive structurally avoids.
4. **The empirical event panel comes from MarginFi, not Kamino.** Paper 3's empirical core needs the wider lending-stack view; this file is the geometry, not the panel.

## New §1.0 — opening (replaces current lines 3 of `01_introduction.md`)

As of October 2025, TSLAx — Backed Finance's tokenized Tesla SPL on Solana, mint address `XsDoVfqeBukxuZHWhdvWHBhgEHjGNst4MLodqsJHzoB` — was accepted as collateral on Kamino Finance, Solana's largest lending market with $4 billion in total value locked [cong-tokenized-2025, p.20]. Kamino prices TSLAx through Kamino Scope, decoding Chainlink Data Streams v8 RWA Standard reports — a mid-only schema with fields `feedId`, `validFromTimestamp`, `observationsTimestamp`, `nativeFee`, `linkFee`, `expiresAt`, `lastUpdateTimestamp`, `midPrice` (int192), and `marketStatus` (uint32: `0=Unknown, 1=Closed, 2=Open`), and **no `bid`, `ask`, or confidence field on the wire** [chainlink-v8; scope-oracle-type; scope-mainnet-cfg]. Scope's mainnet configuration registers two parallel v8 feeds per xStock, governance-set per reserve: an `Open` feed that refuses every update outside US trading hours (price freezes at Friday 16:00 ET close until Monday open, refresh attempts fail with `OutsideMarketHours`), and an `AllUpdates` feed that accepts off-hours `midPrice` clamped to a static ±500 basis-point band around the `Open` feed's last-good value. Cong et al. [cong-tokenized-2025, Table 4, p.32] document that TSLAx — the same instrument — deviates from the last underlying close by more than 1% in 71% of weekday off-hours and 15% of weekend hours; in 12% of weekday off-hours the deviation exceeds 5%, at which point the `AllUpdates` feed is structurally bounded out of representing it. On Monday September 15, 2025, the TSLAx price rose from $401.59 at 06:00 ET to $451.07 by 09:00 ET — a 14% premarket move ahead of US open [cong-tokenized-2025, p.24]. At no point during these windows is the served price a calibrated estimate of where Tesla will reopen; the uncertainty present in the input is not represented in the output.

This is one live $4B lending market reading one schema for one instrument in a tokenized-equity ecosystem that grew 14× between July and October 2025 to $420M aggregate market cap, 95% of which is concentrated in Backed Finance (acquired by Kraken late 2025) and Ondo Global Markets [cong-tokenized-2025, p.4]. The on-chain pricing layer has consolidated in parallel: Chainlink has been the official oracle of the xStocks Alliance since June 2025; CF Benchmarks (Kraken's FCA-supervised benchmark subsidiary) launched the regulated xStocks Indices family on 2026-05-07, powering Kraken's tokenized-equity perpetual futures (live since 2026-02-24, the first regulated tokenized-equity perps); Pyth secured exclusive on-chain distribution of the Blue Ocean ATS overnight equity book through end-2026. This is no longer hobbyist infrastructure. It is becoming regulated financial infrastructure on a steep growth curve, and across every surface — issuer NAV, regulated benchmark, on-chain v8 / v10 / v11, DEX-internal mid — the wire output is a scalar (and a status flag) with no statistical statement about the joint distribution of the served value and the realised future underlying price. The propagation mechanism documented in earlier crypto-native oracle incidents (the Volcano Exchange post-mortem of a tokenised-asset perp episode reports $9.21M in Hyperliquid liquidations exceeding the Binance volume that fed the oracle [volcano-postmortem]; the JELLYJELLY incident on Hyperliquid drove ~$12M of HLP losses through a manipulable single-venue oracle path [jellyjelly]) is the same: *uncertainty present in the input is not represented in the output*. The infrastructure question this paper addresses is whether the same propagation mechanism shows up — and binds the wrong consumers — when tokenized real-world equities are the collateral.

Any DeFi protocol that accepts tokenised RWAs as collateral or as inputs to an automated market maker must answer at every block: *with the underlying venue closed, what is the fair price — and how uncertain is it?* The structural fix is symmetric: an oracle whose product contract makes the input uncertainty a first-class output a downstream consumer can read on every block. This paper documents one such primitive, deployed on the universe Kamino consumes, validated on twelve years of public-data weekend windows, and instrumented with the calibration receipts an institutional risk-management consumer needs to integrate.

The question recurs across the broader RWA wave moving on-chain in 2025–2026: tokenised equities (xStocks on Solana, Backed-issued tokens), treasuries (BlackRock BUIDL, Ondo OUSG; >$5B AUM as of Q1 2026), commodities (Paxos PAXG), and the next wave of credit and FX. Every asset class with a continuous off-hours information set inherits the same closed-market-pricing problem and benefits from the same calibration-transparent risk-reporting primitive. The methodology fits asset classes with such a signal; it does not fit instruments without one (illiquid commodities, pure NAV real estate). §9.8 enumerates the generalisation map.

## New §1.3 — Forward case (inserted between current §1.2 and current §1.3; current §1.3 becomes §1.4)

### 1.3 Why the binding-demand window is now, not later

The Kamino-TSLAx exposure documented in §1.0 is one collateral asset on one Solana lending market consuming one Chainlink schema; it is a single data point in an ecosystem on a trajectory. Cong et al. [cong-tokenized-2025] tracked 100 tokenized stocks in the four months ending October 2025 — roughly half are equities in the universe this paper prices — across $420M aggregate market cap, growing 14× from the July 2025 baseline. The consumer surface is widening as fast: Kamino's xStocks lending market is the live anchor today; Drift Protocol teased equity perpetuals in February 2026; CFB-anchored Kraken xStock perpetuals went live the same month; Ondo's Solana tokenized-stock launch was announced for early 2026 [coindesk-ondo-solana]. The structural arbitrage frictions Cong et al. document — issuer-controlled redemption windows, KYC/AML constraints, primary-arbitrageur geofencing [cong-tokenized-2025, §3.3.3, p.17; §6.1, pp.41–42] — are durable market-design features, not maturity gaps. The tokenized-vs-underlying off-hours wedge persists by design as the market scales, not in spite of scaling.

Three near-term trigger events convert the latent exposure into binding demand for a calibration receipt on the served price. First, **the first Monday-gap liquidation event in a tokenized-equity lending market** that the realised tail of `(token_price - underlying_open)` cannot be defensibly hedged against under the current oracle stack — the September 15, 2025 TSLAx 14% premarket move is the documented prototype, and any lending market consuming the `AllUpdates` v8 feed during such an event would either freeze at the ±500 bps clamp or accept an off-hours mid that is empirically uncalibrated against the realised reopen. Second, **the first bank-grade or fund-grade integration of tokenized RWA collateral** at which the integrating institution's model-risk officer is asked, under SR 11-7 [sr11-7] or SR 26-2 [sr26-2], to defend the methodology of the price feed — the BMR Article 27 disclosure CFB publishes for its regulated xStock Indices does not approach the documentation bar these regulations set, and no other surveyed feed comes closer. Third, **the first options or structured-product issuance referencing tokenized equities** — strike pricing requires a forward distribution on the underlying, and the conditional-mean passthrough Cong et al. document (Table 10, p.40: λ = 0.903, $R^2 = 0.839$) is necessary but not sufficient.

The calibration-transparent primitive proposed here is positioned specifically for that consumer set: protocols carrying off-hours underlying-side risk on tokenized equity collateral, institutional integrators that must produce model-risk documentation, and derivative issuers needing forward distributions. It is not positioned to displace perp-settlement feeds (Kraken xStock perps via CFB), DEX market-making mids, or any consumer for whom the tokenized-side price *is* the correct settlement reference by contract design. The three trigger events above are each "when, not if" on the 12–24 month horizon implied by the market-cap trajectory; the paper's empirical contribution is to provide a deployable artefact, with an empirically validated coverage receipt, before the first trigger fires.

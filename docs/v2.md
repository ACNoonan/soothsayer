# Soothsayer V2 — Planned Improvements

V1b is the shipping methodology (factor-adjusted forecaster + empirical-quantile CI
+ hybrid per-regime selection + customer-selects-coverage Oracle + empirical
calibration buffer). V2 is the next major methodology revision, primarily gated
on data accumulation rather than engineering effort. This doc enumerates the
upgrades and their triggering conditions.

The full Phase 2+ engineering roadmap (split-conformal, post-violation cooldown,
sub-regime granularity, FOMC/CPI/NFP calendar regressors, symbol-specific
vol-spike detector, fat-tail F0) lives in the Obsidian vault at
`Projects/Soothsayer - Solana Oracle/08 - Project Plan.md` under "Phase 2+
methodology roadmap." This doc focuses on the **data-gated additions surfaced
by the arxiv paper research (2026-04-24)** that are not covered there.

## Status

## Data-gated upgrades

### V2.1 — F_tok forecaster (on-chain xStock TWAP)

**What.** Add a third forecaster `F_tok` whose point estimate is a contemporaneous
on-chain xStock TWAP at fri+24h (or fri+48h for long_weekend). The hypothesis,
following Cong et al. 2025, is that on-chain tokenized-stock prices during
off-hours partially anticipate the Monday open beyond what (Friday close ×
futures return) captures. If true, F_tok should be tighter than F1_emp_regime
in normal regime, and the hybrid policy can route to it where it wins.

**Why this isn't in V1b.** xStocks went live mid-2025. The V5 tape (Chainlink +
Jupiter mid, 1-minute cadence) started collecting 2026-04-24. As of 2026-04-25
we have ~1 day of tape and ~30 weekends of post-launch xStock history. Both are
insufficient for a Kupiec test (need ≥150 weekends for stable rejection power
per regime).

**Triggering condition.** ≥150 weekends of V5 tape with all 8 xStocks present
(ETA: Q3-Q4 2026), AND a directional comparison on the bridge period
(mid-2025 onward, ~50 weekends by paper-revision time) shows F_tok point-RMSE
beats F1_emp_regime on at least one regime.

**Scope.** ~3 days engineering (forecaster module + surface integration) +
1 week backtest validation + paper revision.

**Paper coupling.** Disclosed as a v2 baseline in `arxiv §9.11` (Cong baseline
gap); v2 paper revision adds it as a primary forecaster.

### V2.2 — Rolling calibration-surface rebuild

**What.** Replace the one-shot pre-2023 surface fit with a rolling monthly
rebuild. The buffer (`CALIBRATION_BUFFER_PCT`) is re-measured from each rebuild's
held-out OOS slice. Architecturally: a cron job rebuilds
`data/processed/v1b_bounds.parquet`, the surface CSVs, and updates a
`buffer.json` with the freshly-measured buffer; the Oracle hot-reloads.

**Why this isn't in V1b.** v1b ships with a single static surface. The buffer
size (2.5pp) was measured on a 2023+ slice in April 2026 — fine for the demo,
but a production deployment will see the buffer drift as the calibration target
ages. The 07.1 deep research output names ECMWF's quarterly cadence as the
analog; for a faster-moving market, monthly is the default recommendation.

**Triggering condition.** Phase 1 Week 4 deployment (live-mode Oracle going
into production). Until then, static surface is correct.

**Scope.** ~1 week engineering: rebuild cron + atomic surface swap + rolling
buffer measurement + alerting on buffer drift > X bps. Paper-side: a §6 figure
showing buffer trajectory across rebuilds would strengthen the calibration
section.

**Paper coupling.** §9 mentions this as a stationarity caveat; v2 paper revision
adds the buffer-trajectory figure once we have ≥6 rebuilds (≥6 months operational).

### V2.3 — MEV-aware consumer-experienced coverage

**What.** Measure realized coverage *as the consumer experiences it on-chain*,
not just at theoretical mid-prices. For each weekend in the V5 tape, compute
(a) reported coverage (what we claim) vs (b) consumer-experienced coverage
where Monday-open prints are the actual prices a consumer would have read on
Solana, accounting for sandwich-bot front-running near the band edge. Daian et
al. 2020 (Flash Boys 2.0) implies these can differ near τ=0.95 boundary
violations.

**Why this isn't in V1b.** No Solana MEV log data was collected during Phase 0;
calibration was done against off-chain mid-prices. The V5 tape (started
2026-04-24) is the first dataset with on-chain prints at the cadence needed.

**Triggering condition.** ≥3 months of V5 tape with associated Jito bundle data
(or Helius MEV-tagged transactions). Estimated availability: Q3 2026.

**Scope.** ~3 days engineering: MEV-aware Monday-open reconstruction + extra
coverage column in the v1b_calibration tables. Paper-side: §9 disclosure that
"consumer-experienced coverage may differ from reported coverage by Δ bp; we
report Δ in v2." Honest framing, not a methodology change.

**Paper coupling.** Disclosed in `arxiv §9` (MEV/adversarial-ordering disclosure);
v2 paper revision quantifies Δ.

### V2.4 — Full PIT uniformity diagnostic

**What.** Beyond the three discrete Kupiec τ levels (0.68, 0.95, 0.99), report
the full Probability Integral Transform distribution and run a
Kolmogorov-Smirnov test for uniformity (Diebold-Gunther-Tay 1998). This is the
density-forecast-tradition standard and will be expected by reviewers familiar
with that literature.

**Why this isn't in V1b.** Not data-gated — purely a diagnostic add. Deferred
because Kupiec at the product-defining τ=0.95 is sufficient for the v1b PASS
gate, and PIT-uniformity is more informative for a paper than for a shipping
product (it diagnoses *where* the surface is mis-shaped, useful for v2
forecaster design).

**Triggering condition.** Stage 4/5 of the arxiv paper, OR a reviewer requests
it during peer review.

**Scope.** ~10 lines in `src/soothsayer/backtest/metrics.py` + 1 figure in
`reports/figures/`.

**Paper coupling.** Direct paper deliverable, not a model change. Listed here for
completeness only.

## What V2 explicitly is NOT

- **Not a re-architecture.** The four-layer architecture (raw forecaster +
  calibration surface + hybrid regime selection + empirical buffer) survives.
  V2 adds forecasters and operational rigor; it does not replace the core.
- **Not a coverage-target change.** Default target=0.95 is a product choice,
  not a methodology constraint. Option C lets customers pick anyway.
- **Not contingent on the on-chain Anchor program.** Program-side changes (PDA
  layout, signer-set rotation, multi-replicator consensus) are separate
  workstreams — see Phase 3 in `08 - Project Plan.md`.
